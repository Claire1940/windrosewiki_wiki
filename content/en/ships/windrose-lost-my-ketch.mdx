## Locating Your Ketch: Quest Markers and Map Navigation

If you find yourself thinking "I have **windrose lost my ketch**," the first thing to check is your active quest log. The game utilizes a dynamic objective system that tracks the physical location of your ship once it has been discovered. In the early game, the ship is often held up near a pirate-controlled cave or a fortified camp.

To recover your vessel, you must often interact with specific NPCs like the Doctor or the rescued sailors. They provide the coordinates for Thomas Richards' camp, where your ship is frequently docked during the "Recover the Artifact" questline.

### Steps to Locate a Missing Ship

| Step | Action | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Check Quest Log | Ensure "Get the ship floating" is active. |
| **2** | Visit the Shoreline | Look for the **Ship Icon** on your compass. |
| **3** | Clear Local Pirates | Defeat the lieutenants holding the "Black Marks." |
| **4** | Interact with Wheel | Take control of the ship to set its new home point. |

> **💡 Tip:** If the ship icon is missing from your HUD, return to your main bonfire. The ship often resets to the nearest coastal "Bonfire Range" if it was left adrift during a logout.

## Building the Shipwright's Workshop

Once you have physically located the boat, you may still feel like you have **windrose lost my ketch** because it is not yet functional. To make the ship sailable, you must build a Shipwright's Workshop at your camp. This is a common point of frustration for players because the workshop has specific environmental requirements that aren't always clear.

The most important requirement is a **Roof**. You cannot place the workshop in an open field; it must be protected from the elements. Furthermore, it must be placed within the "Bonfire Range" of your camp to access your stored materials.

### Workshop Construction Requirements

| Material | Quantity | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Wood** | 40 - 80 | Basic structural frame and roofing. |
| **Copper** | 10 - 20 | For advanced tools and workshop machinery. |
| **Fiber** | 15 | Used for lashings and securing the roof. |
| **Bonfire** | 1 | Must be within the active radius. |

## Essential Repairs and Upgrades

After solving the **windrose lost my ketch** mystery by building the workshop, you need to focus on durability. A base Ketch is fragile and will easily be sunk by the pirate sloops prowling near your camp. You will need to mine significant amounts of Copper to craft the necessary ship defenses.

### Top Ship Upgrades for the Ketch

1.  **12-Pounder Cannons**: These are your primary source of damage. They require Copper and Wood.
2.  **Hull Bracings**: Essential for taking hits from Blackbeard’s lieutenants. They steady the hull under strain.
3.  **Boarding Party Gear**: While difficult to master, this allows you to take over enemy ships for extra loot.
4.  **Repair Kits**: Always keep a "Flagship" repair item in your active arsenal to fix the ship mid-battle.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to fight the Blackbeard Flagship without at least Level 1 Hull Bracings. The waves and enemy fire will tear a basic Ketch apart in seconds.

## Gathering Resources: Copper and Wood Farming

To avoid a permanent **windrose lost my ketch** scenario due to sinking, you must keep up with resource demands. Copper is the bottleneck for most players in the 2026 demo.

Copper is typically found in caves or near rocky outcrops on the starter islands. If you have exhausted the supply on your home island, you will need to use your ship (even in its damaged state) to reach neighboring islands. Look for "X Marks the Spot" maps found on drowned pirates, as these often lead to hidden copper caches.

| Resource | Best Location | Tool Needed |
|---|---|---|
| **Copper Ore** | Jungle Caves / Rocky Cliffs | Stone or Copper Pickaxe |
| **Sturdy Timber** | Inland Forests | Copper Axe |
| **Pearls** | Coastal Dodos / Shells | Hand Gathering |
| **Cloth/Fiber** | Tropical Bushes | Hand Gathering |

## Defending Against Blackbeard's Forces

Once your Ketch is sea-worthy, the game shifts into naval combat. You will notice pirate ships "prowling" near your camp. This is a scripted event designed to test your ship's upgrades. If you find yourself in a **windrose lost my ketch** situation because your ship was sunk, you will have to restart the repair process from your workshop.

To win these fights, manage your speed. Turning the wheel while at full sail makes you a fast target but reduces your accuracy. Slowing down allows your 12-pounders to aim more effectively. Always aim for the enemy's waterline to sink them faster, or target their masts to prevent them from fleeing.

## Troubleshooting the "Lost" Ship Bug

In some rare instances, a **windrose lost my ketch** issue might be caused by a technical glitch rather than a gameplay mechanic. If you have completed the repairs and the ship is still not appearing at your dock, try the following:

*   **Check the Map Filter**: Ensure "Player Vehicles" is toggled ON in your map settings.
*   **Check the Shipwright's Menu**: Access the "Manage Ship" tab at your workshop. If the ship is listed but not visible, use the "Recall" function (this may cost resources).
*   **Stamina Check**: Ensure your character has enough stamina to swim out to the ship if it spawned further from the shore.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I find my ship on the map?**

A: If you feel you have **windrose lost my ketch**, it is likely because you haven't finished the "Black Marks" quest. You must defeat the two lieutenants in the jungle camps to unlock the gate leading to the ship's actual docking location.

**Q: How do I fix the "Requires a Roof" error for the workshop?**

A: To build the workshop and finish your ketch, you must place a floor, four walls, and a flat or slanted roof. The workshop must be completely enclosed from above to be considered "indoors."

**Q: What happens if my ketch is destroyed in battle?**

A: If you lose your ship, you can rebuild it at the Shipwright's Workshop. However, you will lose any uninstalled materials and will need to spend Wood and Copper to craft a new hull.

**Q: Where is the best place to find Copper for ship repairs?**

A: Check the cave systems near the "Drowned" spawns. These areas are dangerous but contain the highest density of Copper nodes needed to solve your **windrose lost my ketch** resource shortage.

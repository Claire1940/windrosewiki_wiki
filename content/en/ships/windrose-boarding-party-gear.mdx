## Essential Early-Game Crafting and Resources

Before you can worry about high-level naval combat, you must secure the foundational materials required to craft basic **windrose boarding party gear**. The beaches of the starting islands are rich with resources, provided you know where to look. Shipwrecks are your primary source for nails, which are indispensable for early construction and gear assembly.

| Resource | Primary Source | Common Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nails** | Beached Shipwrecks | Ship building and advanced crafting |
| **Plant Fiber** | Seaweed and Bushes | Coarse fabric and rope |
| **Wood** | Fallen Trees and Driftwood | Basic structures and tool handles |
| **Rough Hide** | Boars and Sea Creatures | Leather armor and upgrades |
| **Copper Ore** | Mountainous Mines | Metal tools and weapon components |

> **💡 Tip:** Use your axe to bash seaweed on the beach. It is often a much faster way to accumulate plant fiber than searching for individual bushes inland.

When gathering wood, pay attention to the physics of the environment. If you angle your chops correctly, a falling tree can strike another tree, knocking it down and doubling your efficiency. This "domino effect" is a vital technique for players looking to quickly gather the 150+ wood required for a new ship or base expansion.

## Combat Mechanics and Weaponry

Combat in Windrose is more than just clicking until an enemy falls. Utilizing the "Heavy Attack" (Middle Mouse Button) is essential for breaking through enemy shields. This attack is also highly effective for mining copper more efficiently. However, heavy attacks consume more stamina, so balancing them with standard strikes and blocks is necessary for survival.

### Ammunition and Ranged Combat
While gunpowder and bullets are the standard for firearms, clever pirates can use unconventional ammunition. Pearls, found by opening closed scallop shells in your inventory, can be used as emergency ammunition. While they require gunpowder to fire, they are surprisingly effective in a pinch when your standard bullet supply runs dry.

1.  **Search for Treasure Markers:** Look for numerical indicators (e.g., 0/3) on islands to find hidden loot containing gunpowder.
2.  **Learn Building Plans:** Always check your "Curios" tab to learn new recipes from found blueprints.
3.  **Upgrade to Copper:** Replace wooden tools with copper versions as soon as you have a workbench and enough ingots.
4.  **Refill Your Lantern:** Use animal fat at a workbench to keep your lantern fueled for cave exploration.

## Ship Management and Boarding Preparation

Your ship is an extension of your **windrose boarding party gear**. Before setting sail to engage enemy vessels, you must visit a Wharf (Warf) to manage your ship's inventory. A well-prepared ship should always carry multiple repair kits in its cargo hold. These can be used via a quick-slot (typically the '6' key) while at the helm to repair hull damage in the heat of battle.

### Naval Essentials Checklist
*   **Cannons:** Ensure all slots are filled and ammo is stocked in the ship's hold.
*   **Repair Kits:** Carry at least 5-10 kits for long journeys.
*   **Fast Travel Bell:** Craft a travel point on every island to allow for quick resource transport.
*   **Shanties:** Press 'B' to start a crew shanty, which boosts morale during long voyages between the three main islands.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to sail beyond the demo boundaries. Your ship will be destroyed instantly if you ignore the "Leaving Area" warning.

## Survival: Food Buffs and Vitality

Your health bar in Windrose is directly tied to your hunger and comfort levels. To increase your maximum health, you must consume high-quality food. Standard fruits like coconuts and bananas provide minimal boosts. For a true boarding party, you need crafted meals that offer Agility or Vitality bonuses.

| Food Item | Quality | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coconuts/Bananas** | Common | Small Health Restore |
| **Cooked Meats** | Uncommon | Medium Health & Vitality |
| **Rare Blue Meals** | Rare | Large Health & +10 Agility |
| **Base Comfort** | Buff | Slows health decay over time |

By mixing different food types, you can stack bonuses. Always ensure you have a "Comfort" buff by spending time in a well-built base before heading out for a boarding mission. This ensures your **windrose boarding party gear** is backed by the highest possible stat pool.

## Advanced Tactics: The Boar Dash and Moat Defense

One of the most dangerous early-game threats is the charging boar. These creatures cannot be blocked conventionally. To defeat them, you must time your dash (Control key) just as the red indicator appears. Successfully dodging a charge often stuns the boar, leaving it vulnerable to a heavy melee combo.

For base defense, the shovel is your best friend. You can set the shovel to "Dig Mode" to create deep trenches. If you dig deep enough to hit the water table, you can create a moat. This prevents land-based monsters from reaching your walls, effectively trapping them in a watery pit while you pick them off from safety.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more copper ingots for my windrose boarding party gear?**

A: Copper ore has a low conversion rate (roughly 4:1). To get a significant amount of ingots, you must mine large quantities of ore from caves using the heavy attack (Middle Mouse) for maximum yield.

**Q: What happens to my gear when I die?**

A: Upon death, a tombstone marker appears on your map. You must return to this location to retrieve your dropped loot. If the marker is in deep water, be careful, as pirates in Windrose cannot swim for long durations without drowning.

**Q: Can I fast travel while on my ship?**

A: Yes, but only while you are at the helm. Once you man the boat, open your map and look for the gold indicator around your discovered fast travel points to teleport the ship and crew.

**Q: How do I learn new crafting recipes for my boarding party?**

A: You must find building plans in treasure chests or as drops. Once found, go to your inventory, select the plan, and click the "Learn" button to add it to your crafting menu permanently.

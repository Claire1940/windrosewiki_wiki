## Locating the Frigate Blueprints

Before you can even think about the physical **windrose frigate materials**, you must secure the design plans. These plans are not found in the starting areas of the game. Instead, you must set sail for the **Tortuga Market**, a bustling hub located in the second biome.

To find Tortuga Market, head toward the second biome on the left side of your world map. It is tucked away in a region that requires a sturdy ship just to reach, so ensure your current vessel is fully repaired before making the trek. Once you arrive at the market, look for the two NPCs known as the "Persons of the Coast." These vendors hold the secrets to the Frigate, but they won't share them with just anyone.

## Mastering the Trust System

The most significant "material" you need for the Frigate isn't wood or iron—it's **Trust**. The Persons of the Coast will only sell you the ship design once you have reached Trust Level 4. This requires a total of 3,000 reputation points. 

To earn these points, you must engage in ship-to-ship combat. When you destroy enemy vessels across the map, they drop specific reputation items. Collecting these and turning them in at the Tortuga Market is the only way to climb the ranks. It is highly recommended to farm these items in the first biome if your ship is weak, or move to the second biome for higher-tier drops that provide more points per turn-in.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a few slots open in your cargo hold when hunting enemy ships. The reputation items needed for Trust Level 4 take up physical space and are vital for unlocking the Frigate.

## Essential Windrose Frigate Materials for Crafting

Once you have purchased the design from the Tortuga Market for 3,000 points, you cannot simply "call" the ship to your location. You must return to your home base and visit the **Wharf**. The Wharf is the only facility capable of handling the construction of a vessel of this magnitude.

The construction phase requires a massive amount of **windrose frigate materials**. While the specific quantities can vary based on your current Wharf level and any active bonuses, you should prepare to gather the following categories of resources:

### 1. Structural Timber
The hull of a Frigate is significantly larger than a Sloop or a Brig. You will need high-grade wood harvested from the more dangerous islands in the second biome. Standard driftwood will not suffice for a ship of this caliber.

### 2. Reinforced Metalwork
To withstand cannon fire, the Frigate requires iron or steel plating. These **windrose frigate materials** are often found in mountainous regions or by salvaging the wrecks of heavy armored ships.

### 3. Fine Canvas and Cordage
Speed is a Frigate's greatest asset. To catch the wind effectively, you must provide high-quality cloth for the sails. This often involves processing raw fibers found on tropical islands or purchasing them from high-end traders.

### 4. Specialized Components
High-tier ships often require "Core" items, which are rare drops from boss encounters or elite fleet battles. Keep an eye out for unique mechanical parts during your travels.

| Material Category | Primary Source | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hardwood Planks** | Island Logging (Biome 2) | Main hull and deck construction. |
| **Iron Ingots** | Mining and Smelting | Reinforcing the bow and gun ports. |
| **Heavy Canvas** | Loom Crafting / Trading | Creating the three-mast sail system. |
| **Ship Blueprint** | Tortuga Market | Unlocks the crafting recipe at the Wharf. |

## Step-by-Step Construction Guide

Building the Frigate is the final hurdle in your journey. Follow this sequence to ensure you don't waste your hard-earned **windrose frigate materials**:

1. **Verify the Blueprint**: Open your inventory and "Learn" the Frigate design you bought from Tortuga.
2. **Upgrade the Wharf**: Ensure your base Wharf is at the required level. If your Wharf is too low-level, the Frigate option will be grayed out even if you have the materials.
3. **Deposit Materials**: Move your wood, metal, and cloth into the Wharf's storage. Construction cannot pull directly from your ship's hold if you are docked.
4. **Initiate Build**: Select the Frigate from the ship menu. Double-check that you aren't accidentally building a lower-tier vessel.
5. **Wait for Completion**: High-end ships take time to construct. Use this period to farm for cannons and upgrades so your Frigate is combat-ready the moment it touches the water.

> **Warning:** Do not delete your old ship until the Frigate is fully built and launched. You will need your current vessel to continue farming resources if you run short on **windrose frigate materials** mid-build.

## Optimizing Your Gathering Route

To gather **windrose frigate materials** efficiently, you should establish a "loop" between the second biome and your base. Start at the Tortuga Market to check your Trust progress, then sail clockwise through the surrounding islands to harvest timber and ore. 

By the time you return to the market, the enemy ships should have respawned, allowing you to farm more reputation items. This dual-purpose farming ensures that you are progressing toward the Trust Level 4 requirement while simultaneously filling your hold with the physical resources needed at the Wharf.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is the Tortuga Market located?**

A: Tortuga Market is located in the second biome, typically on the far left side of the map. Look for a large island icon with a market stall symbol. It is the primary hub for high-tier ship designs and the "Person of the Coast" NPCs.

**Q: Can I find windrose frigate materials in the first biome?**

A: While some basic materials like wood and low-grade iron can be found in the first biome, the Frigate requires higher-tier resources and a blueprint that can only be obtained in the second biome. It is much more efficient to farm in the second biome once your ship is strong enough.

**Q: How do I reach Trust Level 4 quickly?**

A: The fastest way to reach Trust Level 4 is to hunt down "Elite" or "Named" ships in the second biome. These ships drop high-value reputation items that provide more points toward the 3,000-point goal than standard merchant ships.

**Q: What happens if I lose my Frigate after crafting it?**

A: Like all ships in Windrose, the Frigate can be repaired if damaged. However, if it is completely destroyed, you may need to spend a portion of the original **windrose frigate materials** to rebuild it at your Wharf, depending on your current insurance or game difficulty settings. Always keep a reserve of iron and wood at your base for emergencies.

**Q: Is the Frigate the best ship in the game?**

A: As of 2026, the Frigate is widely considered one of the best all-around ships due to its high gun count and respectable speed. While some specialized ships might beat it in a single category, the Frigate's versatility makes it the top choice for most endgame content.

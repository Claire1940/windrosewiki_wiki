Dominating the high seas in Windrose requires more than just basic sailing skills; it demands the right vessel to withstand the rigors of naval warfare. For many captains, the **windrose frigate** represents the pinnacle of early-to-mid-game progression, offering a significant upgrade in durability, firepower, and speed over starter ships. This vessel isn't just handed to you through the main story; it requires a dedicated effort to build reputation and gather the necessary resources.

Acquiring the **windrose frigate** is a multi-step process that involves exploring specific biomes, engaging in ship-to-ship combat to earn trust, and finally managing a complex construction project at your home base. Whether you are looking to clear higher-level content or simply want a more imposing presence on the water, understanding the mechanics of the Tortuga Market and the Wharf is essential. In this comprehensive guide, we will break down exactly how to secure this powerhouse for your fleet in 2026.

## Locating the Tortuga Market

The journey to your new ship begins with a voyage to the Tortuga Market. This specialized trading hub is not available in the starting area. Instead, you must navigate toward the second biome, which is typically located to the left of the initial starting zone on the world map.

Finding the market can be tricky if you aren't paying attention to the biome transitions. As you enter the second biome, keep a close eye on your map for a mission marker or a specific port icon labeled "Tortuga Market." This location serves as the primary hub for the "Person of the Coast" faction, who hold the blueprints for the most advanced naval designs in the region.

| Location Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Biome** | Second Biome (Western Region) |
| **Primary Hub** | **Tortuga Market** |
| **Key NPCs** | Person of the Coast |
| **Main Objective** | Purchase the Frigate Blueprint |

> **💡 Tip:** Ensure your current ship is well-repaired before heading to the second biome, as the pirate activity in this region is significantly higher than in the starting waters.

## Raising Reputation with the Person of the Coast

Once you arrive at the Tortuga Market, you will encounter two NPCs representing the Person of the Coast. They won't simply sell the **windrose frigate** to any passing sailor. You must prove your worth through the game's Trust system. To unlock the ability to learn the frigate design, you must reach **Trust Level 4**, which is currently the maximum reputation level for this faction.

Raising your trust level involves a gameplay loop focused on naval combat. You need to collect specific reputation items that drop from enemy ships. By hunting down hostile vessels in the open sea and returning their cargo or insignias to the NPCs at Tortuga, you will slowly climb the ranks.

### Trust Level Requirements

| Requirement | Value Needed | Method of Acquisition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trust Level** | **Level 4 (Max)** | Turning in faction-specific items |
| **Reputation Points** | **3,000 Points** | Combat drops from enemy ships |
| **Item Source** | Enemy Vessels | Destroying ships in the second biome |

To reach the 3,000-point threshold efficiently, focus on hunting larger merchant convoys or pirate hunters, as they tend to drop higher-quality reputation items. Once you have reached the maximum level, the NPCs will permit you to "learn" the design. Note that learning the design only adds it to your crafting repertoire; it does not place the ship in your inventory immediately.

## Purchasing and Learning the Blueprint

With Trust Level 4 secured, you can interact with the vendor to purchase the **windrose frigate** design. This transaction is the final hurdle before you can begin the physical construction of the vessel. It is highly recommended that you also learn any associated ship components or upgrades available at this tier while you are at the market, as they will be vital for outfitting your new ship later.

After "learning" the ship design, the blueprint is permanently tied to your character's knowledge base. You can verify this by checking your crafting log or ship encyclopedia. However, as the vendors will remind you, they provide the plans, not the lumber. You must return to your own territory to begin the actual labor.

## Constructing the Windrose Frigate at the Wharf

The final and most resource-intensive stage of the process takes place at your base's **Wharf**. The **windrose frigate** is a massive vessel, and its construction requirements reflect its status as a top-tier ship. You cannot simply "summon" this ship; it must be built from the ground up using raw materials gathered throughout your travels.

### Building Materials Overview

While the exact numbers can fluctuate based on game balance updates in 2026, the general categories of materials remain consistent. You will need a significant stockpile of the following:

1.  **Hardwood Planks**: Used for the hull and structural integrity.
2.  **Refined Iron**: Required for the cannon ports, anchors, and reinforcement.
3.  **Heavy Canvas**: Necessary for the large sail area characteristic of a frigate.
4.  **Specialized Components**: Often obtained from dismantling higher-level enemy wrecks.

| Material Type | Primary Source | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hardwood** | Logging in the Second Biome | **Hull Construction** |
| **Iron Ingots** | Mining and Smelting | **Armor & Cannons** |
| **Sail Cloth** | Looting or Crafting | **Mobility & Speed** |
| **Gold Doubloons** | Trading and Quests | **Labor Costs** |

> **Warning:** Do not start the construction process until you have 100% of the required materials. Leaving a ship half-finished at the Wharf can tie up your construction slot, preventing you from repairing your current vessel.

## Naval Combat and Strategy

Once your **windrose frigate** is launched, you will notice an immediate difference in how you handle encounters. Frigates in Windrose are designed for broadside engagements. Unlike smaller sloops that rely on hit-and-run tactics, the frigate can stay in the "pocket," absorbing damage while delivering devastating volleys from its numerous gun decks.

### Key Advantages of the Frigate

*   **Increased Hull Health**: Allows you to take on elite world bosses and fortresses that would sink smaller ships instantly.
*   **Superior Firepower**: The ability to mount more cannons per side means your broadsides can disable enemy masts in a single turn.
*   **Crew Capacity**: More room for sailors means better efficiency in reloading and repairing during active combat.

## Comparing the Frigate to Other Vessels

Is the **windrose frigate** always the right choice? While it is arguably the "best" ship for general combat, it does come with trade-offs. Its larger size makes it a bigger target, and its turning circle is significantly wider than that of a Brigantine or a Sloop.

| Ship Class | Role | Maneuverability | Firepower |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sloop** | Scouting/Early Game | High | Low |
| **Brigantine** | Balanced/Trading | Medium | Medium |
| **Frigate** | **Warfare/Heavy Combat** | **Low** | **High** |

If your goal is to explore shallow reefs or engage in fast-paced smuggling runs where speed and stealth are paramount, you might find the frigate a bit cumbersome. However, for the majority of players aiming to conquer the second and third biomes, the frigate is an indispensable asset.

## FAQ

**Q: Where exactly is the Tortuga Market located?**

A: The Tortuga Market is located in the second biome. If you are looking at your world map, head toward the western (left) side of the starting region. Once you cross into the new biome, look for the faction icons associated with the Person of the Coast.

**Q: Can I get the Windrose frigate without reaching Trust Level 4?**

A: No, the blueprint is locked behind the reputation system. You must reach the maximum trust level (Level 4) and accumulate 3,000 points by turning in items from destroyed enemy ships to unlock the design.

**Q: What happens if my frigate is destroyed?**

A: Like all ships in Windrose, if your vessel is sunk, you will need to pay a significant insurance fee or rebuild it at the Wharf using a portion of the original materials. It is always wise to keep a backup supply of hardwood and iron in your warehouse.

**Q: Is the frigate the final ship in the game?**

A: While the **windrose frigate** is one of the strongest ships available in the current 2026 build, the developers frequently add "Man-O-War" or "Galleon" class ships in late-game expansions. However, the frigate remains the most versatile heavy hitter for the mid-to-late game progression.

## Mastering Different Windrose Ship Types

Because faction reputation is map-specific, players often find themselves "hot-swapping" between different worlds to access specific ship parts or entire hulls that are unavailable on their primary save.

## Advanced Combat Mechanics: Boarding vs. Sinking

Regardless of the **windrose ship types** you encounter, the method by which you defeat them determines your total reward. In 2026, the community consensus is clear: **boarding is significantly more profitable than sinking.**

When you sink a ship, you only receive the loot that drops into the water. However, if you disable a ship and board it, the rewards scale with the number of players present. Only one ship needs to initiate the boarding process. Once the ships are tethered, every player in the session can jump onto the enemy deck. When the ship is officially conquered, every participant receives a full monetary reward.

### Critical Damage and the "Butt" Strategy
Every ship, regardless of its size, has a vulnerability at the rear. When engaging in naval combat, always aim for the "butt" of the enemy vessel. Hits to the rear are registered as critical damage, indicated by yellow damage numbers. High-level ships with upgraded cannons can often sink or disable smaller **windrose ship types** in a single well-aimed volley to the stern.

| Combat Action | Result | Reward Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sinking** | Ship destroyed immediately | Low (Single loot drop) |
| **Boarding** | Tactical deck combat | **High** (Individual gold for all players) |
| **Rear Shot** | Critical damage (Yellow numbers) | Maximum DPS |
| **Broadside** | Standard damage | Moderate DPS |

> **Warning:** Boarding an enemy ship effectively pauses combat with other nearby vessels. If you are overwhelmed by multiple enemies, boarding one can give you the breathing room needed to survive.

## Ship Maintenance and Wharf Services

Keeping your fleet in top shape is surprisingly straightforward. While repair kits are useful for mid-ocean emergencies, the most cost-effective way to maintain any of the **windrose ship types** is visiting a wharf.

At any wharf, you can perform an instant repair for a flat fee of **20 wood**. It does not matter if your ship is at 90% health or 1%, the cost remains the same. This makes wood one of the most valuable resources to keep in your ship's hold at all times.

### Quick Travel and Ship Recovery
Managing multiple ships or exploring distant reaches of the map can lead to logistical headaches. You can use quick travel spots to move between POIs, but these spots are limited. A pro strategy in 2026 involves "recycling" your quick travel points:
1. Travel to a destination.
2. Destroy the quick travel marker to reclaim resources.
3. Summon your ship to your current location.
4. Use the ship's helm to access the world map and travel to other active markers.

## Base Building and Naval Support

While most of your time is spent on the deck of various **windrose ship types**, your land-based operations provide essential support. The campfire acts as the heart of your base, but it is more flexible than it appears. You can pick up and move your campfire at any time without destroying your structures.

Building isn't restricted to your base area, either. You can build small platforms in the world to gain a height advantage over land-based enemies who cannot jump. This is particularly useful when you need to clear a coastal POI to unlock new ship recipes or faction vendors.

### The Role of NPCs
Your base can house various NPCs, such as the Doctor, who provides free supplies like potions and bandages. When you move your campfire, remember to re-settle your NPCs through the campfire interface to ensure your naval support team remains active.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I own multiple windrose ship types at once?**

A: Yes, you can own and store multiple ships. However, you can only have one active ship summoned to your location at a time. You can swap between them at most major wharves.

**Q: Do I lose my ship if it sinks?**

A: If your ship is destroyed, you can respawn it, but you may lose some of the cargo held in the ship's inventory. It is always safer to carry 20 wood for emergency wharf repairs.

**Q: How do I get better cannons for my ship?**

A: Better cannons are unlocked through faction reputation and by finding recipes in high-level POIs. Like the ships themselves, you can gain these recipes faster by playing in multiplayer and exploring different maps.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many people can get money from boarding?**

A: There is no known hard limit. Every player who is physically on the enemy ship when it is conquered will receive the gold reward, making it a highly effective way to farm currency in large groups.

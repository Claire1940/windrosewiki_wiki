## Mastering Navigation and the Windrose Mechanic

The "Windrose" isn't just the title of the game; it is the central UI element that dictates your movement efficiency. Unlike arcade-style sailing games, this title requires you to pay close attention to wind direction and velocity. Ignoring the windrose will result in sluggish movement and make you an easy target for hostile privateers.

### Understanding the Windrose UI
The windrose on your screen provides real-time data on atmospheric conditions. It is divided into several sectors:
1. **The Core:** Shows your ship's current heading.

3. **The Velocity Indicator:** A shifting bar that shows the strength of the gusts.

### Sailing Techniques
To achieve maximum speed, you must angle your sails to catch the wind at a "Broad Reach" (approximately 45 degrees off the wind). Sailing directly into the wind will put you in "Irons," significantly slowing your progress and draining your crew's stamina.

| Maneuver | Wind Angle | Speed Bonus | Stamina Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Running** | 180° (Behind) | +25% | Low |
| **Broad Reach** | 135° | **+40%** | Medium |
| **Beating** | 45° | +10% | **High** |
| **In Irons** | 0° (Facing) | -90% | Very High |

## Resource Management and the "Heat" System

As highlighted in the **windrose steam** launch trailer, "Heat" plays a critical role in gameplay. While the trailer uses the term poetically, in-game mechanics translate "Heat" into a combination of environmental temperature and notoriety. Sailing through tropical zones or engaging in frequent piracy increases your Heat level.

### Managing Your Heat Levels
High Heat levels can lead to crew fatigue, increased water consumption, and the arrival of powerful bounty hunters. To manage this, you must utilize cooling stations or dock at neutral ports to let your reputation cool down.

*   **Cooling Stations:** Found near icebergs or deep-sea vents.
*   **Neutral Ports:** Safe zones where you can pay off bounties or rest the crew.
*   **Night Sailing:** Reduces environmental heat but increases the risk of hitting rocks.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your Heat gauge reaches the red zone, your ship's hull integrity will begin to degrade, and your crew may attempt a mutiny.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Windrose Steam demo progress carried over to the full game?**

A: Typically, progress in the Steam Next Fest demo is for testing purposes and may not carry over to the final release, although players often receive exclusive cosmetic rewards for participating in the demo phase.

**Q: How do I fix the "stuck in irons" issue in Windrose?**

A: To get out of "Irons," you must turn your rudder fully to one side and wait for the ship to drift. Once you are at a 45-degree angle to the wind, your sails will catch again. You can also use "Oars" if your ship class supports them.

**Q: Can I play Windrose Steam with a controller?**

A: Yes, the game features full controller support, which many players find more intuitive for the steering and sail-trimming mechanics.

**Q: What is the benefit of the "Heat" mechanic?**

A: While high Heat is dangerous, it also increases the value of the loot you find and provides a multiplier to your experience gains, making it a "high-risk, high-reward" system for veteran players.

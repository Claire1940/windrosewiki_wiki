## Fast Travel and Maritime Movement

Efficiency is the hallmark of a great pirate. While sailing manually is the best way to soak in the atmosphere and listen to sea shanties (activated by pressing **B**), the **windrose full map** features a robust fast travel system. 

Fast travel is facilitated through "Fast Travel Bells" which must be crafted and placed on each island you wish to visit frequently. This allows you to bypass long sailing times when you simply need to move resources between bases.

### Fast Travel Mechanics

1.  **Island Bells:** Craft a bell and place it at your base. You can then "scoot" between any islands where you have an active bell.
2.  **Ship Helm Travel:** Once you own a ship, you can fast travel directly from the helm.
3.  **The Helm Caveat:** You must be actively manning the wheel (the helm) to see the gold indicator around fast travel points on your map.
4.  **Resource Logistics:** Fast travel is the fastest way to gather loot and return it to your main storage without risking ship combat.

## Treasure Hunting and Discoveries

Exploration is rewarded through the "Discovery" system. As you traverse the **windrose full map**, picking up new items or visiting new locations will unlock crafting recipes. For instance, finding copper ore for the first time unlocks the recipe for copper ingots, which in turn unlocks higher-tier workbenches.

When searching for treasure, look for "shiny" indicators on the ground or tucked away in high places. Game logic often dictates that the best loot is hidden behind obstacles or on elevated platforms that require a bit of parkour to reach.

### Treasure Indicators and Loot

*   **Shiny Glints:** These indicate interactable loot piles.
*   **Building Plans:** Found in treasure chests, these must be "learned" from your inventory before they appear in your build menu.
*   **Curios:** Rare items that provide permanent knowledge or stat boosts.
*   **Gunpowder/Bullets:** Often found in crates near enemy camps or treasure markers.

## Survival and Combat Strategy

The map is home to more than just resources; it is inhabited by dangerous wildlife and rival pirates. To survive the higher-level areas of the **windrose full map**, you must manage your health and hunger through the food buff system.

### Combat and Survival Tips

*   **Food Stacking:** You can mix two different types of food for cumulative health bonuses. Higher rarity foods (Blue/Green) provide agility or vitality boosts.
*   **Heavy Attacks:** Use the middle mouse button for heavy melee attacks. This is effective for both breaking enemy shields and mining ore more efficiently.
*   **The Dash:** When fighting charging boars, watch for the red indicator. Press **Control** to dash out of the way, stunning the beast and leaving it open for a counter-attack.
*   **Base Defense:** If you build your base in a high-traffic area, use a shovel to dig a moat. Filling it with water creates a natural barrier that monsters cannot easily cross.

## Ship Management and Naval Warfare

Your ship is your primary mode of transport across the **windrose full map**. Keeping it in peak condition is vital, especially when Blackbeard’s fleet is patrolling the waters.

### Ship Maintenance Checklist

1.  **Repair Kits:** Always keep multiple repair kits in your ship's cargo hold.
2.  **Quick Repair:** Assign your repair kits to a quick slot (e.g., Slot 6) to apply fixes during active combat.
3.  **The Wharf:** Return to a Wharf to manage your ship, install cannons, or recall the vessel if it becomes stuck.
4.  **Looting Ships:** After sinking an enemy, sail close to the "Ship Spoils" icon and press Space to claim the cargo.

## FAQ

**Q: How many islands are currently available on the windrose full map?**

A: In the current 2026 version/demo, there are three primary islands available for exploration. Each features unique biomes, resources, and enemy types.

**Q: What happens if I sail to the edge of the map?**

A: You will receive a warning about leaving the playable area. If you continue to sail outward, your ship will be destroyed. It is best to stay within the boundaries marked on your world map.

**Q: How do I recover my items if I die in the water?**

A: A red tombstone marker will appear on your map. However, because swimming is limited, recovering loot from the ocean is extremely difficult. It is highly recommended to avoid deep water whenever possible.

**Q: Can I fast travel while my ship is being attacked?**

A: Fast travel requires you to be at the helm and generally out of immediate combat danger. It is always safer to clear nearby threats before attempting to jump between map points.

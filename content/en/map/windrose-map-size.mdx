## Master the Fast Travel System

Given the significant **windrose map size**, traveling manually by boat every time you need to move between islands is inefficient. The game provides a robust fast travel system that relies on specific items and structures. To travel effectively, you must understand the "Bell" mechanic and the construction of travel docks.

To initiate fast travel, you must have a Bell in your inventory. This item acts as the catalyst for warping between points of interest. However, simply having a Bell is not enough; you must also have a destination prepared. Experienced players recommend building a "dodge" or travel setup at every new island you visit.

### How to Set Up a Travel Point
1. **Gather Materials:** Collect enough wood and stone from the local island.
2. **Enter Building Mode:** Press the **B** key to open the construction menu.
3. **Place the Travel Setup:** Select the travel dock/setup and place it in a clear area near the shore.
4. **Link the Base:** Once built, this location becomes a permanent spawn and travel point on your map.

## Resource Management and Tool Durability

One of the unique aspects of the current Windrose demo is the lack of tool durability. While the **windrose map size** is large, you don't have to worry about your pickaxe or axe breaking in the middle of a remote island. This allows you to harvest materials freely to build your fast travel docks without carrying a massive surplus of spare tools.

| Tool Type | Primary Use | Durability Status (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **Pickaxe** | Mining stone and ores | **Infinite** (No degradation) |
| **Axe** | Chopping trees for wood | **Infinite** (No degradation) |
| **Repair Kit** | Fixing ship components | Consumable |

While your personal tools won't break, your ship is a different story. Larger vessels require maintenance and can be damaged during long voyages across the map. Always keep repair items on your boat if you plan to explore the outer reaches of the sea.

## Strategic Base Placement Across the Map

To truly conquer the **windrose map size**, you should aim to have at least three major travel hubs distributed across different sectors of the ocean. This creates a "web" of locations that you can hop between, drastically reducing the time spent in transit.

When you arrive at a new island, your first priority should be resource gathering and building. Since trees and stones are abundant, you can quickly set up a travel dock (by pressing B) and then immediately return to your home base if needed. This "leapfrog" strategy is the most effective way to progress through the main questline.

> **Warning:** Do not leave an island without setting up a travel dock first. If you sail away and forget to mark the location, you will have to manually sail back, which can take upwards of 10-15 minutes depending on the wind and current.

## Exploration Tips for Beginners

*   **Tapping the Map:** You can interact with the map UI even in areas you haven't physically visited. Use this to scout potential island shapes.
*   **Inventory Space:** Even though tools don't break, your inventory is limited. Prioritize building materials (wood/stone) when on new islands to ensure you can build a dock.
*   **The Bell Item:** Always craft a Bell before leaving your main base. Without it, your travel docks are essentially useless for returning home.
*   **Quest Tracking:** If you get lost in the vastness of the map, look at the right corner of your HUD. The quest tracker is the most reliable way to find your way back to the main path.

By following these strategies, the **windrose map size** becomes an opportunity for adventure rather than a barrier to progress. Mastering the building mode and fast travel mechanics will ensure that you spend more time completing quests and less time staring at the open water.

## FAQ

**Q: Exactly how large is the Windrose map size in kilometers?**

A: The developers have not released a specific square kilometer measurement, but the map currently consists of several large island clusters that can take significant time to cross by small boat. The focus is on the "density" of islands rather than just raw empty space.

**Q: Do I need a Bell every time I want to fast travel?**

A: Yes, the Bell is a required item for the fast travel mechanic. It is recommended to keep a stack of them in your inventory so you are never stranded on a distant island.

**Q: Can other players use my travel docks?**

A: In the current demo version, travel docks are specific to the player who built them. However, as the game moves toward full release, multiplayer dock sharing may become a feature.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many travel points I can have?**

A: There is currently no known hard limit to the number of travel setups you can place. Most players find that having one per major island is sufficient for navigating the current map size efficiently.

## Exploring Different Windrose Weapon Types

In Windrose, weaponry is broadly divided into two main categories: character-held weapons used for ground combat and boarding, and ship-mounted weapons used for naval engagements. Each category has its own progression path and tactical applications. Understanding how these **windrose weapon types** interact with the game's mechanics—such as the recipe-unlocking system—is the first step toward becoming an elite player.

### Character Weaponry: Melee and Ranged

Character weapons are your primary tools when exploring points of interest (POIs) or boarding enemy vessels. Unlike many other RPGs where you might find a weapon and simply equip it, Windrose utilizes a "touch-to-learn" recipe system. Any time you interact with a weapon or armor piece that you do not yet have the recipe for, your character automatically learns how to craft it.

| Weapon Category | Combat Style | Key Advantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **One-Handed Melee** | Fast, agile strikes | Allows for use of a secondary item or shield. |
| **Two-Handed Melee** | Slow, heavy damage | High stagger potential against tough NPCs. |
| **Ranged Firearms** | Distance-based combat | Safely engage enemies from high ground or across decks. |
| **Utility Tools** | Support and survival | Includes items like torches that can be used as breadcrumbs. |

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing with friends, share your weapons! Dropping a weapon for a friend to pick up allows them to instantly learn the recipe, effectively doubling your group's crafting knowledge.

### Ship-Mounted Armaments

Ship combat is the heart of the Windrose experience. Your ship’s loadout determines your effective range and how quickly you can disable an enemy for boarding. While specific cannon types vary by faction, they all share a common mechanic: critical hits. To maximize your damage, you must maneuver your ship to "aim for the butt." Hitting the rear of an enemy ship consistently triggers critical damage, indicated by **yellow damage numbers**.

| Ship Weapon Type | Primary Use | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Cannons** | Balanced DPS | Best for mid-range broadsides and general hull damage. |
| **Heavy Long-Guns** | Long-range sniping | Use these to soften up targets before they can return fire. |
| **Swivel Guns** | Close-range defense | Ideal for clearing enemy decks during a boarding approach. |
| **Mortars** | Area of effect (AoE) | Great for hitting multiple ships in a tight formation. |

## How to Unlock and Upgrade Your Arsenal

Progression in Windrose is unique because it is split between character-bound data and map-bound data. While your character retains all the recipes they have touched, your access to high-tier **windrose weapon types** often depends on your standing with local factions.

### Faction Reputation and Map Hopping

Faction reputation is map-specific. This means if you have high favor with a specific group on your own map, any friend who joins your game will benefit from that same favor while they are there. This creates a powerful strategy for unlocking new **windrose weapon types**:

1.  **Divide and Conquer:** Have one friend focus on the reputation for Faction A on their map, while you focus on Faction B on yours.
2.  **Hot Swapping:** Hop between each other's worlds to purchase exclusive weapons and armor that would otherwise take dozens of hours to unlock solo.
3.  **Recipe Sharing:** Once a high-tier weapon is purchased, drop it for your friends so they can learn the recipe permanently.

## Combat Tactics and Survival

Having the best **windrose weapon types** is only half the battle. You must also know how to sustain yourself in a fight and exploit enemy AI.

### The Boarding Meta

Boarding is the most lucrative way to end a fight. When you board a ship instead of sinking it, you gain significantly more gold. In multiplayer, this is even more "OP" (overpowered). If one player boards a ship, every other player in the session can jump onto that same ship. When the ship is conquered, every single player receives a full monetary reward.

> **Warning:** While everyone gets the gold reward from boarding, the physical loot dropped from breaking the ship is limited. Only one player can claim the physical crates, so coordinate with your crew!

### Exploiting Enemy Movement

If you find yourself overwhelmed during land combat, remember that enemy NPCs in Windrose currently do not have a jump mechanic. You can use your building skills to create a simple elevated platform. By standing just a few feet off the ground, you can use your **windrose weapon types**—specifically ranged weapons or long-reach melee tools—to safely eliminate enemies who are unable to reach you.

### Maintenance and Repairs

Even the most powerful weapons won't help if your ship sinks. Always keep a stock of wood on hand for emergency repairs. While repair kits are useful in the heat of battle, you can perform an instant, full repair at any Wharf for a flat cost of **20 wood**. This is significantly faster and cheaper than using multiple kits over time.

## Advanced Weapon Strategies for 2026

As you progress into the late game, the synergy between your character build and your ship becomes vital. You should aim to balance your **windrose weapon types** to cover all ranges.

1.  **Long Range:** Start engagements with ship-mounted mortars or long-guns to weaken the enemy's hull.
2.  **Mid Range:** Close the distance and target the rear of the ship for those massive yellow critical hits.
3.  **Close Range:** Once the enemy ship is disabled, transition to boarding. Use your melee weapons to clear the deck quickly.
4.  **Recovery:** After the fight, visit the Doctor at your base. He frequently provides free potions and bandages that can replenish your supplies for the next encounter.

| Activity | Recommended Weapon Type | Key Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fort Raiding** | Ranged Firearms / Mortars | Stay out of range of stationary turrets. |
| **Ship Boarding** | One-Handed Melee / Pistols | Fast attacks are better for cramped deck fights. |
| **Boss Hunting** | Two-Handed Melee | Focus on staggering the boss to interrupt big attacks. |
| **Mine Exploration** | Torches / Melee | Use torches to mark paths and fight off cave dwellers. |

## FAQ

**Q: Which of the windrose weapon types is best for solo players?**

A: For solo players, a balanced approach is best. Use a one-handed melee weapon paired with a shield for survivability, and ensure your ship is equipped with Standard Cannons for versatile defense. The ability to "cheese" combat by building small platforms is also a lifesaver for solo explorers.

**Q: How do I get more weapon recipes quickly?**

A: The fastest way to expand your recipe book is through multiplayer. Join different servers or have friends join yours. Any time a player crafts or finds a new weapon, have them drop it on the ground. By simply picking it up and putting it back down, you permanently learn that recipe.

**Q: Do weapons break over time?**

A: While character weapons have durability, ship weapons are more reliant on your ship's overall health. Always keep 20 wood available to visit a wharf for an instant repair, which keeps your ship-mounted armaments in top working order.

**Q: Does the faction I choose limit my weapon types?**

A: Factions provide access to specific high-tier items, but because you can travel between different maps and keep your character's recipes, you are never truly locked out of any **windrose weapon types**. You can simply visit a friend's map where they have high reputation with a different faction to buy what you need.

## The Reputation Loop: Insignias and Bounty Agents

The core gameplay loop involving **windrose factions** revolves around the collection and turn-in of Insignias. These are tokens of proof earned by defeating Blackbeard’s ships or clearing out his shore-side encampments. Insignias come in various tiers, with higher-level threats dropping more valuable tokens.

To progress, players must take these Insignias to a Bounty Agent. These NPCs are typically found in faction hideouts or major hubs like Tortuga. Turning them in grants Reputation Points, which are the primary "experience" for faction leveling.

### Benefits of High Reputation
Leveling up your standing is not merely cosmetic. It is the primary method for unlocking:
*   **High-Tier Weapons:** Access to superior cannons, swivel guns, and personal firearms.
*   **Ship Gear:** Improved sails, reinforced hulls, and specialized rudders.
*   **Building Blueprints:** Unique prefab structures and advanced building materials for your camp.
*   **Specialized Workers:** The ability to hire more efficient crew members for your crafting stations.

> **Expert Tip:** Don't hoard your insignias. Turning them in early allows you to access better gear sooner, which significantly reduces the difficulty of mid-game naval combat.

## Navigating the Dual-Currency Economy

Windrose utilizes a realistic dual-currency system that reflects the colonial influences of the era. Understanding which coin to use—and where to get it—is vital for efficient trading.

| Currency | Real-World Inspiration | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Piastre** | Spanish Silver | General trading, basic repairs, and common materials. |
| **Guinea** | English Gold | High-end weapons, rare ship parts, and elite blueprints. |

A common mistake for new players is attempting to sell all their loot at the first merchant they see in Tortuga. However, valuables can usually only be sold to NPCs that have a "Buyer" tag over their heads. These specialized vendors are often tucked away in specific faction hideouts rather than the main town square.

### Currency Exchange and Selling
If you find yourself with an abundance of Silver but need Gold for a new ship upgrade, you can visit the **Smugglers of Port Royal**. They offer currency exchange services, though the initial rates are often unfavorable. 

> **Warning:** Avoid large-scale currency exchanges early in the game. The exchange rates are known to improve as your reputation with the Smugglers increases, making it much more cost-effective to wait until you've reached higher ranks.

To maximize your profits, always check the item tooltips. The bottom of the description will explicitly state which faction is interested in buying that specific item. Selling to the correct faction often yields higher payouts and additional reputation bonuses.

## Side Quests and Letters of Favor

Beyond sinking ships, **windrose factions** offer unique side quests that provide a break from constant naval warfare. These often involve logistics, such as the Rogue Buccaneers requesting large shipments of gunpowder or the People of Tortuga needing specific building supplies.

### Letters of Favor
One of the most valuable rewards from these quests is the **Letter of Favor**. These are non-combat-related items that serve as a universal currency for reputation.
*   They can be taken to any archipelago faction on Tortuga.
*   They allow you to "boost" reputation with a faction of your choice without needing to farm specific combat insignias.
*   They are ideal for balancing out your progress if you find yourself lagging behind in one specific faction's unlock tree.

## Hiring Crew and Optimizing Crafting Stations

As you gain standing with the various groups, you will earn the right to hire crew members. In the current version of the game, these NPCs do not physically walk around your ship during combat, but they serve a much more practical purpose at your base camp.

Assigning hired workers to specific crafting stations provides massive passive buffs that can save hours of resource grinding. For example:
*   **Alchemist Station:** Assigning a skilled worker can grant a **30% chance** to craft an extra elixir for free.
*   **Gear Workshop:** Certain NPCs provide a **30% chance** to recover resources spent when upgrading your weapons or ship armor.

Strategic players should prioritize hiring workers that complement their playstyle. If you find yourself frequently losing masts in battle, a worker that reduces repair costs or resource consumption is invaluable.

## Merchant Contracts: A Game-Changing Feature

Perhaps the most innovative aspect of the **windrose factions** system is the implementation of Merchant Contracts. These are purchased from faction provisioners and allow for the automation of resource gathering.

Once you purchase a contract, you can build a specialized station at your outpost, such as a **Merchant Animal Product Station**. From here, you can literally place orders for specific items—like crab meat or dodo eggs—and have them shipped directly to your base.

### How to Use Merchant Contracts
1.  **Purchase the Contract:** Visit a faction provisioner (requires a specific reputation level).
2.  **Build the Station:** Use your building menu to place the merchant station at your camp.
3.  **Place an Order:** Spend your Piastre or Guinea to request specific goods.
4.  **Delivery:** Wait for the shipment to arrive, bypassing the need to manually hunt or forage for those specific items.

This system is particularly useful for high-level players who prefer to spend their time "plundering at sea" rather than gathering domestic resources. It creates a gameplay loop where combat yields currency, and currency is used to bypass the "grind" of survival mechanics.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I join multiple windrose factions at the same time?**

A: Yes. Currently, there is no penalty for working with all four factions simultaneously. Since they are all united against Blackbeard, you can maximize your reputation with everyone at once.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get English Guineas?**

A: The most reliable way to earn Guineas is by selling high-end valuables to specialized buyers in faction hideouts or by completing high-level bounty contracts against Blackbeard's elite captains.

**Q: Do I lose reputation if I accidentally hit a friendly faction ship?**

A: In the current 2026 build, there is no friendly fire or negative reputation system implemented for the four main factions. They will remain friendly even during chaotic naval battles.

**Q: Where can I exchange my Piastre for Guinea?**

A: You can perform currency exchanges at the Smugglers of Port Royal. Keep in mind that the exchange rate is quite high unless you have improved your reputation with them.

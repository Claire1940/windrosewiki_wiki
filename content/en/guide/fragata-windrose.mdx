## Choosing Your Vessel: Brethren vs. Blackbeard

When you step up to the frigate class, you are faced with a critical choice between the Brethren Frigate and the Blackbeard Frigate. While the Blackbeard variant is often marketed as the "offensive" choice due to its ability to equip 36-pounder cannons, many veteran players find its lower health pool to be a significant liability in multi-ship engagements.

The Brethren Frigate is widely considered the superior **fragata windrose** for general play due to its massive 200,000 HP pool. In a game where repair kits restore a percentage of your total health, having a higher base HP makes every second of your repair duration significantly more valuable.

## The Cannon Meta: Why Devastating Wins

In Windrose, you have three primary cannon types: Tempered, Perfectly Ordered, and Devastating. While the Tempered cannons offer a massive 40% damage boost if you wait 6 seconds between shots, this playstyle often results in lower overall DPS compared to rapid-fire options.

The **Devastating** variant is the current gold standard for any **fragata windrose** build. Its "Ragged" effect increases all damage dealt to the target by 10%, stacking up to three times. When you ascend these guns, you gain an additional 15% reload speed, allowing you to reach maximum stacks almost instantly.

### Cannon Comparison Table

| Cannon Type | Base Effect | Ascension Bonus | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Devastating** | Applies Ragged effect (+10% dmg) | **+15% Reload Speed** | Sustained DPS & Bosses |
| **Perfectly Ordered** | +30% Reload speed after hit | +30% Aiming Speed | Long-range sniping |
| **Tempered** | +40% Volley damage (wait 6s) | +20% Weak spot damage | One-shot ambush builds |

Since frigates feature both a main and a secondary battery, the most effective strategy is to equip Devastating cannons in both slots. This ensures that the Ragged effect is applied regardless of which battery is currently firing, maximizing your damage output during the chaotic reload windows.

## Hull Bracing and Naval Tactics

Your hull is your primary defense, and in 2026, the **Killhole Bracing** is the undisputed champion. Unlike the Iron Resolve hull, which only provides resistance when you are near death, the Killhole Bracing ensures that taking damage does not interrupt your repair kits.

When paired with the **Stretch the Supplies** naval tactic, your repair kits become nearly infinite. This tactic increases repair kit duration by 30% and Grog duration by 200%. On a 200,000 HP Brethren Frigate, this setup allows you to out-heal the incoming damage of an entire fleet.

### Essential Naval Tactics Ranked

1.  **Stretch the Supplies:** Vital for tanking. Increases repair duration and Grog efficiency.
2.  **Silence the Guns:** Excellent for offensive play. Reduces enemy reload and damage upon being hit.
3.  **No Quarter:** Good for clearing smaller ships, as it restores health upon a kill within 50m.
4.  **Ambush:** Generally avoided for frigates due to the requirement of not taking damage for 2 minutes.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid the "Ship Shape" tactic for combat builds. While it repairs you out of combat, the abundance of combat repair kits makes this slot much better utilized by Stretch the Supplies.

## Boarding Gear and Crew Combat

Boarding is where you secure the "double rewards" that make high-level play profitable. To successfully board a level 15 **fragata windrose**, your boarding party gear must be leveled appropriately. If you attempt to board a level 15 ship with level 6 gear, your crew will be slaughtered regardless of your personal combat skill.

### Best Boarding Weapons
*   **Halberd:** The sweeping light attacks can hit 4-5 enemies at once. The overhead heavy attack is powerful but can be tricky to land in tight quarters.
*   **Two-Handed Sword:** Similar to the Halberd but with a more reliable "poke" heavy attack that can hit enemies through your own sailors.
*   **Blunderbuss:** Use this as your secondary. Blast enemies as they come up the stairs, then swap to your melee weapon while the gun reloads passively in your quick bar.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I buy the different hulls for the fragata windrose?**

A: You can purchase all three hull types (Iron Resolve, Stanfast, and Killhole) from the Smugglers of Port Royale provisioner. He also sells the five core tactic books.

**Q: Is the Blackbeard Frigate ever worth using?**

A: Yes, if you prefer a "hit and run" playstyle. Its 36-pounder cannons deal massive burst damage, but you must be very careful with your positioning, as it cannot survive the same amount of sustained fire as the Brethren model.

**Q: How do I use Grog effectively during naval combat?**

A: You must equip Grog to your ship's action bar, just like repair kits. Once activated, it provides a 30-minute buff (which can be extended via tactics) to either your reload speed or total damage. For the Devastating build, reload speed is always the better choice.

**Q: Why are my boarding parties failing even though I have a Frigate?**

A: Check your Boarding Party Gear level. If your gear is level 5 and you are boarding a level 12 or 15 ship, your troopers will lack the stats to survive. Always match your gear level to the level of the ships you are hunting.

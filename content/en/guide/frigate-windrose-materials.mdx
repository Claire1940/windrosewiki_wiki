## The Frigate Progression Path

Before you can set sail in a Frigate, you must progress through the earlier ship tiers. In Windrose, the ship hierarchy currently consists of the Catch, the Brig, and finally, the Frigate. Each class offers three distinct variants: **Stock** (balanced), **Brethren** (tank-focused), and **Blackbeard** (glass cannon).

While the Catch is obtained through the main storyline, the Brig and Frigate require significant material investment. For the Frigate specifically, the Blackbeard variant becomes much more viable due to the massive payoff from its 36-pounder cannons. However, getting to that point requires a massive stockpile of resources that are easily wasted if you aren't careful.

### Essential Frigate Construction Materials

Building a Frigate is a monumental task that requires a variety of refined and raw goods. Below is the breakdown of the primary materials you need to secure before visiting the shipwright.

| Material | Primary Source | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Iron Ingots** | Foothills (Mining) | Ship hull, cannons, and ammunition |
| **Timber (Hardwood)** | Foothills (Logging) | Structural integrity and decking |
| **Linen Fabric** | Garden/Foothills (Flax) | Sails and interior rigging |
| **Tar** | Cursed Swamps | Waterproofing and advanced hull components |
| **Nails** | Crafting (Iron/Copper) | General assembly of all ship tiers |
| **Shipwright Tools** | Specialized Vendors/Loot | Required for high-tier blueprints |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Iron is the most volatile resource in the game. It is used for ship construction, building upgrades, and ammunition. Do not spend it freely on minor gear upgrades before you have secured your Frigate.

## Farming Frigate Windrose Materials Efficiently

Farming in Windrose is governed by specific respawn timers. If you exhaust a resource node, you must wait for the world to replenish itself. A common mistake among new captains is ignoring the "No Respawn" rule for certain environmental objects, which can lead to severe bottlenecks in the late game.

### Resource Respawn Timers

| Resource Type | Respawn Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Trees (Timber)** | **Never** | Once chopped, they are gone from that instance. |
| **Baskets & Bales** | 6 Hours | Found near settlements and docks. |
| **Vegetables (Flax)** | 2 Hours | Essential for starting your garden loop. |
| **Mobs (Enemies)** | 2 Hours | Necessary for looting specialized components. |

To gather enough **frigate windrose materials**, you must treat the Foothills as your primary base of operations. This biome contains the hardwood trees needed for Timber and the Flax required for Linen Fabric. Since trees do not respawn, you should adopt a "take no prisoners" approach: if you see a hardwood tree, chop it down immediately and haul it back to your base.

### The Flax and Linen Loop

Linen Fabric is a major bottleneck for the Frigate. To mitigate this, start collecting Flax as soon as you reach level 6. Instead of turning all your Flax into fabric immediately, save the seeds. Planting these in your base garden creates a passive progress loop. Aim for a steady cycle of 20 to 30 seeds to ensure you have a constant supply of Linen without having to hunt for wild Flax nodes in dangerous territory.

## Ship Equipment and Upgrade Priority

Once you have gathered your **frigate windrose materials** and constructed your vessel, the work isn't over. You must upgrade your gear to survive the level 15+ enemy encounters. Wasting materials on "green" or mediocre gear is a common pitfall. It is often better to wait until you can craft blue or purple tier equipment.

### Recommended Upgrade Order

1.  **Cannons:** These are your primary power spike. A Frigate with level 12 cannons can delete enemy ships in a single salvo.
2.  **Hull:** Survivability is key. A dead ship deals zero damage. Upgrade your hull to withstand the heavy broadsides of enemy Brigs.
3.  **Support Gear:** Items that boost repair speed or duration.
4.  **Boarding Gear:** This should be your lowest priority. While useful, most fights in 2026 are decided by superior positioning and cannon fire.

| Equipment Slot | Recommended Choice | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Cannons** | **Perfectly Ordered 36-pdrs** | Significant reload speed bonus. |
| **Hull Bracing** | **Nail Hold** | Taking damage no longer interrupts repairs. |
| **Support** | **Stretch the Supply** | Increases the duration and effectiveness of repair kits. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Nail Hold" hull bracing to maintain your momentum during intense fights. Being able to repair while under fire is often the difference between victory and a trip back to the respawn point.

## Advanced Naval Tactics for Frigate Captains

With a fully upgraded Frigate, your combat style must evolve. The Frigate is less maneuverable than the Catch or Brig, meaning positioning is everything. You cannot simply circle-strafe your enemies; you must use the environment and ship mechanics to your advantage.

*   **Speed Management:** Slow down to execute tighter turns, then raise your sails to full to regain momentum. This "drift" mechanic is essential for keeping your broadside pointed at the enemy.
*   **Broadside Management:** The 36-pounder cannons are devastating. Instead of swapping sides constantly, focus on one side and use your superior reload speed (boosted by "Perfectly Ordered" gear) to pummel the enemy.
*   **Wave Cover:** In heavy seas, time your shots for the crest of the waves. Conversely, use the troughs of waves to shield your hull from incoming fire.

You can find more detailed community discussions on these tactics on the [official Windrose Steam Community](https://steamcommunity.com/app/windrose) pages, where developers frequently post updates on resource spawn rates and balance changes.

## Avoiding Common Progression Mistakes

Many players struggle with the transition to the Frigate because they treat ship progression as a pure combat problem rather than a logistical one. To succeed in 2026, you must be a master of the economy as much as the sea.

*   **Don't Spend Iron Early:** It is tempting to upgrade your Brig's cannons to the max, but remember that the Frigate requires a massive 120+ Iron Ingot investment.
*   **Don't Ignore Tar:** While Tar is found in the Cursed Swamps, it is one of the easier materials to farm safely. Spend an hour hugging the coastline in the swamps to stock up before you start the Frigate build.
*   **Don't Skip Hardwood:** Since trees do not respawn, every hardwood tree you pass and don't chop is a lost resource for your instance.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to farm frigate windrose materials like Iron?**

A: The Foothills are the premier location for Iron and Timber. Look for rocky outcrops for iron veins and inland forests for hardwood trees. Remember that trees in Windrose do not respawn, so you must explore new areas or join different world instances to find more.

**Q: Which Frigate variant is the best for solo players?**

A: For solo play, the **Brethren** variant is often recommended due to its increased tankiness. However, if you are confident in your positioning and have upgraded your cannons to level 12, the **Blackbeard** variant offers the highest DPS potential, allowing you to end fights before you take significant damage.

**Q: How do I get more Shipwright Tools for my Frigate?**

A: Shipwright Tools are rare drops from high-level enemy ships and can occasionally be purchased from specialized naval vendors in major ports. It is highly recommended to save these specifically for Frigate-tier blueprints rather than wasting them on Brig upgrades.

**Q: Does the Frigate require Tar for all upgrades?**

A: Tar is primarily used for the initial construction of the Frigate and for high-level hull reinforcements. While it isn't needed for every single cannon upgrade, you will need a substantial amount to reach the maximum hull level.

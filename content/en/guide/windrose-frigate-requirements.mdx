As you progress through the treacherous waters of the Caribbean, moving from a humble Ketch to a massive Frigate is the ultimate goal for any aspiring pirate captain. Understanding the **windrose frigate requirements** is essential for players looking to transition into the late-game phase, where naval battles involve multiple high-level hostiles simultaneously. Unlike the smaller vessels you start with, a Frigate offers dual-battery capabilities, allowing you to rain destruction upon your enemies while maintaining a massive health pool.

Meeting the **windrose frigate requirements** involves more than just amassing gold; it requires a strategic understanding of resource farming, faction reputation, and the specific ship variants available. Whether you prefer the tank-like resilience of the Brethren Frigate or the high-velocity damage of the Blackbeard variant, your choice will dictate your playstyle for the rest of the 2026 season. In this guide, we will break down everything you need to know to acquire, build, and optimize your first heavy warship.

## Understanding Windrose Frigate Requirements

To unlock a Frigate, you must first progress through the ship tiers. Most players begin with a Ketch, move to a Brig (such as the Brethren Brig), and finally reach the Frigate class. The primary requirements involve reaching specific level milestones and gathering rare materials like Tumbaga ingots.

| Requirement Type | Detail | Importance |
|---|---|---|
| **Character Level** | Level 12+ recommended for initial builds | **High** |
| **Primary Resource** | **Tumbaga Ingots** (found in Foothills ruins) | **Critical** |
| **Faction Access** | Smugglers of Port Royale & Rogue Buccaneers | **Essential** |
| **Previous Ship** | Must own a Brig-class vessel | **Mandatory** |

The transition to a Frigate is a significant jump in power. While a Brig might have around 90,000 HP, a Brethren Frigate can boast up to **200,000 HP**. This massive health pool is necessary because late-game missions often force you to "punch upwards" against five or more enemy ships at once.

## Choosing Your Frigate Variant

There are two primary paths when fulfilling your **windrose frigate requirements**: the Brethren Frigate and the Blackbeard Frigate. Each serves a distinct tactical purpose on the battlefield.

### The Brethren Frigate: The Unstoppable Tank
The Brethren Frigate is often considered the "safe" choice for solo players. It features the highest HP in the game and a massive cargo hold. While it "steers like a fridge," its ability to soak up damage while repairing makes it nearly impossible to sink in 1v1 or even 1v3 scenarios.

### The Blackbeard Frigate: The Glass Cannon
If you prioritize speed and raw firepower, the Blackbeard Frigate is the superior option. It is the only ship class capable of wielding **36-pounder cannons**, the most devastating weapons in the game. However, this comes at the cost of a significantly lower HP pool compared to the Brethren variant.

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself struggling with maneuverability, the Blackbeard variant offers better acceleration and turning, allowing for "hit and run" tactics that the Brethren Frigate cannot replicate.

## Essential Materials and Resource Farming

The most difficult part of meeting the **windrose frigate requirements** is farming the necessary Tumbaga ingots. These are required for both the construction of the hull and the ascension of your high-tier cannons.

*   **Foothills Region:** This is the primary area for Tumbaga, specifically in the level 6 to 10 zones.
*   **Ruins & Sanctuaries:** Look for map markers labeled "Ruins" or the "Ancient Fire Sanctuary." These locations contain the highest density of ingots.
*   **Boarding Rewards:** Always attempt to board enemy ships rather than sinking them. Boarding provides double rewards, which is the fastest way to stockpile the gold and materials needed for ship upgrades.

## Optimal Ship Loadouts: Cannons and Batteries

Once you have met the basic **windrose frigate requirements**, you must decide on your battery configuration. Frigates use a main and a secondary battery, allowing you to mix and match cannon types.

| Cannon Type | Base Effect | Ascension Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| **Devastating** | Applies **Ragged** (10% dmg increase) | +15% Reload Speed |
| **Perfectly Ordered** | +30% Reload speed after hitting | +30% Aiming Speed |
| **Tempered** | +40% Damage if waiting 6s | +20% Weak Spot Damage |

The current meta for 2026 heavily favors **Devastating Cannons**. By equipping Devastating 24-pounders in your main battery and 12-pounders in your secondary, you can stack the "Ragged" effect almost instantly. This ensures the enemy takes maximum damage from every subsequent volley.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid ascending the Perfectly Ordered 12-pounder cannons. The ascension bonus provides "Aiming Speed," which is currently considered useless in high-level naval combat compared to reload or raw damage buffs.

## Hull Bracing and Naval Tactics

Your choice of hull bracing is just as important as your cannons. While many players gravitate toward the Iron Resolve hull for its damage resistance at low health, the **Killhold Bracing** is the objective winner for Frigate builds.

1.  **Killhold Bracing:** Taking damage no longer interrupts your repair kit duration. It also increases repair kit duration by 30%.
2.  **Stretch the Supplies (Tactic):** This naval tactic further increases repair kit duration by 30% and Grog duration by 200%.

When you combine Killhold Bracing with the "Stretch the Supplies" tactic, your repair kits can run for upwards of 16 seconds. On a 200,000 HP Brethren Frigate, this creates an "ultra-tank" build where you can out-heal the incoming damage of four or five enemy ships simultaneously. For more information on ship stats, check the [official Windrose community hub](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/2052320).

## Boarding Party Requirements

A Frigate is only as good as the crew that boards the enemy. As you move into level 12 and level 15 zones, you will face boarding encounters with 20 or more enemies.

*   **Boarding Party Gear:** Always ensure your gear level matches the level of the ships you are attacking. If you are boarding a level 15 Frigate, you need level 15 boarding party gear.
*   **Weapon Choice:** Use the **Halberd** or **Two-Handed Sword**. These weapons have wide sweeping motions that can hit multiple sailors at once, which is vital when fighting in the cramped quarters of a ship's deck.
*   **Firearms:** The **Blunderbuss** is highly recommended. Its "shotgun" effect can clear stairs and railings instantly, especially if you have the "Over-Penetration" talent.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I buy the different Frigate hulls and tactics?**

A: You can purchase the three different hulls and five tactic books from the Smugglers of Port Royale provisioner. Cannons (12, 24, and 36 pounders) are sold by the Rogue Buccaneers provisioner.

**Q: Does the Blackbeard Frigate have enough health to survive level 15 encounters?**

A: While it has significantly less HP than the Brethren variant, it can survive if you use its superior speed and the Killhold Bracing. However, you must be more diligent with your positioning and repair kit timing.

**Q: What is the best way to use Grog in Windrose?**

A: Grog should be placed in your ship's action bar next to your repair kits. It provides a 30-minute buff to damage or reload speed. With the "Stretch the Supplies" tactic, this duration is tripled, making it very cost-effective for long farming runs.

**Q: Are the windrose frigate requirements different for solo vs. co-op play?**

A: The resource and level requirements remain the same, but solo players are strongly encouraged to build the Brethren Frigate for its higher survivability, whereas co-op players can have one person go "Full Tank" while the other uses a Blackbeard "Glass Cannon" build.

## First Encounter: Captain Thomas Richards

Captain Thomas Richards serves as the introductory challenge in the Coastal Jungle. While he may seem like a standard brawler, his reach and grab attacks can quickly end a run if you are careless. The secret to this fight is rhythm and directional dodging.

### Combat Strategy
The most effective strategy against Richards is a consistent "Dodge to the Right" movement pattern. Most of his swings are horizontal and originate from his left, meaning a right-sided dodge puts you behind his active hitboxes.

1.  **The Triple Dodge:** Dodge right three times in succession to clear his main combo.
2.  **The Heavy Interruption:** If you see him winding up a massive swing, a well-timed heavy attack can actually "scare" him, causing him to abort the animation.
3.  **The Red Blink:** Watch for the red visual cue. This indicates a grab attack. Do not dodge early; wait for the flash to trigger your invincibility frames.

## Second Encounter: Israel Hands

Located in the Foothills, Israel Hands is a significant step up from Captain Richards. He utilizes a mix of melee swipes, charges, and vertical mobility that can be difficult to track. When considering **how many bosses are in windrose**, many players find Israel Hands to be the "skill check" that determines if they are ready for the late game.

### Attack Patterns and Counters
Israel Hands relies on a "Swipe, Swipe, Charge" rhythm. Unlike Richards, dodging backward is often safer here to avoid his wider arc of attack.

*   **The Swipe Combo:** When he swipes twice, dodge backward twice, then immediately follow up with a light attack.
*   **The Charge:** When he flashes red and charges, dodge to the side. This is your best window for a heavy attack.
*   **The Aerial Phase:** He will occasionally zoom upwards. While you can attempt to shoot him with a rifle, it is generally safer to focus on dodging his landing impact and using the window to bandage yourself.

| Attack Type | Visual Cue | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Swipe** | Arm pull-back | **Dodge Back** |
| **Red Charge** | Red eye/body flash | **Dodge Side + Heavy Attack** |
| **Gunshot** | Pistol draw | **Maintain Distance / Side Dash** |

## The Final Challenge: The Tainted Priestess

The Tainted Priestess is the final of the three bosses and requires a complete shift in playstyle. This fight is less about simple dodging and more about mechanical precision and build optimization. You will need a ranged weapon—specifically a musket or a multi-shot pistol—to succeed here.

### The Pustule Mechanic
The Priestess has glowing pustules located across her body. Destroying these is the only way to stagger her and open a vulnerability window for massive damage. 

1.  **Ranged Necessity:** Use a **Sturdy Pistol** (3 shots) or a **Reliable Musket** (fast reload) to pop pustules from a distance.
2.  **Stagger Windows:** Once enough pustules are destroyed, she will slump over. This is when you unleash your highest DPS combos.
3.  **Positioning:** Stay close to her sides. Dodging left is generally more effective for hitting the clusters of pustules on her back and flanks.

### Recommended End-Game Build (2026 Meta)
To make this fight manageable, a high-critical hit build is recommended. By focusing on Mastery and specific gear sets, you can ensure that every hit during her vulnerability phase deals maximum damage.

| Component | Item/Stat | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Weapon** | **Sturdy Hellbard** | +10% Critical Hit Chance |
| **Armor Set** | **Philibuster Set** | Reduced stamina consumption |
| **Necklace** | **Mayor Necklace of Mastery** | Increases Mastery to the 20-point cap |

## General Preparation for All Bosses

Regardless of **how many bosses are in windrose** at the time of your play session, certain universal truths apply to every encounter. The talent tree is your most powerful tool for survival. 

### Essential Talents
*   **Retribution:** This allows you to regain "Temporal Health" by attacking after being hit. It is essential for staying in the fight without constantly pausing to heal.
*   **Planning Ahead:** Increases the reload speed of your holstered gun. This is vital for the Tainted Priestess fight where you swap between melee and ranged frequently.
*   **Perfect Form:** Reduces stamina consumption for dodging. Since all three bosses require heavy dodging, this is a non-negotiable talent for most builds.

## Progression and Region Unlocks

Beating these bosses isn't just about the loot; it's about expanding your horizons. Windrose uses these encounters to gate-keep the most lucrative regions of the map.

1.  **Defeat Thomas Richards** → Unlocks the **Foothills** and better crafting materials.
2.  **Defeat Israel Hands** → Unlocks the path to the **Final Region**.
3.  **Defeat Tainted Priestess** → Grants access to **End-game Frigates** and elite naval upgrades.

The loop of upgrading gear to level 5, then level 10, and finally level 15 corresponds directly with these three major milestones. If you find yourself struggling, it is almost always a sign that your gear level is too low for the region's boss.

## FAQ

**Q: Exactly how many bosses are in Windrose currently?**

A: There are currently three main progression bosses: Thomas Richards, Israel Hands, and the Tainted Priestess. While there are smaller elite enemies, these three are the primary gatekeepers for region progression.

**Q: Do I need a specific weapon type to beat the bosses?**

A: While most bosses can be beaten with any melee weapon, the Tainted Priestess effectively requires a ranged weapon (Pistol or Musket) to pop the pustules on her body and trigger her stagger state.

**Q: What is the best way to heal during a boss fight?**

A: Use the Retribution talent to gain health back through combat. If you must use items, create distance during the boss's "recovery" animations (like after Israel Hands' charge) to safely use a bandage or potion.

**Q: Is there a level cap for gear before the final boss?**

A: For the Tainted Priestess, it is highly recommended to have all gear pieces upgraded to level 15. Additionally, using an Elixir of Concentration or Cruelty can provide the extra edge needed to finish the fight.

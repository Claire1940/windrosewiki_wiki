## The Foundation: The Shipwright's Workbench

Before you can fire a single shot, you must establish your base of operations. The **Shipwright's Workbench** is the primary station for all naval construction. This is where you will spend the majority of your time refining raw materials into deadly components. 

Once you have secured your initial vessel, your primary objective should be to construct this workbench. It serves as the hub for crafting four critical categories of ship equipment:
1.  **Cannons:** Your primary source of damage.
2.  **Hull Armor:** The defensive layer that determines how many hits you can take.
3.  **Boarding Equipment:** Essential for captains who prefer to take prizes rather than sink them.
4.  **Naval Tactics:** Passive items that provide massive stat boosts to your entire crew.

### Early Game Resource Requirements

To begin your journey in **windrose frigate crafting**, you will need to stockpile a significant amount of raw materials. The early game focuses heavily on terrestrial gathering before you can truly conquer the waves.

| Material | Primary Source | Usage in Crafting |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wood** | Trees (Forest Biomes) | Basic ship frames and cannon carriages. |
| **Plant Fibers** | Shrubs and Grass | Refined into Fabric for sails and rigging. |
| **Copper Ore** | Rock Outcrops (Caves/Cliffs) | Essential for casting early-game cannons. |
| **Fabric** | Refined from Fibers | Used for sail upgrades and boarding gear. |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't stop farming once you have enough for one upgrade. The material requirements scale exponentially as you move into higher tiers of ship components.

## Crafting and Equipping Ship Components

Crafting the item is only half the battle. Many novice captains make the mistake of crafting a powerful new cannon and heading straight into battle, only to find their ship performing exactly as it did before. In Windrose, you must manually equip every item at the **Wharf**.

### The Three-Sided Combat System
When crafting cannons, remember that your ship has three distinct firing zones: the Front (Bow), the Left (Port), and the Right (Starboard). Each of these zones operates on an independent cooldown. 

For efficient combat, especially in the early game, focus your **windrose frigate crafting** efforts on the Front and one side (e.g., the Left). This allows you to alternate shots—firing from the front while your side cannons reload, and then turning to fire a broadside while the front reloads. This "rhythm of shooting" is the most effective way to maintain high Damage Per Second (DPS) without being caught in a long reload window.

## Tortuga and Advanced Schematics

As you progress through the main story, you will eventually reach the pirate haven of **Tortuga**. This location is pivotal for anyone serious about high-level **windrose frigate crafting**. Here, you will encounter two types of NPCs that will change how you upgrade your ship: **Bounty Agents** and **Provisioners**.

### Reputation and Schematics
You cannot simply buy the best gear with gold (Piastas) alone. You must first earn the respect of the local factions.
*   **Bounty Agents:** Turn in Pirate Insignias (looted from defeated enemies) to increase your Reputation.
*   **Provisioners:** Once your Reputation is high enough, these vendors will sell you advanced **Schematics**.

| Faction | Specialization | Recommended Item |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Smugglers** | Naval Tactics | **Passive HP Regeneration** |
| **Tortuga Locals** | Hull Armor | **Reinforced Oak Plating** |
| **Buccaneers** | Cannons/Ammo | **Heavy Iron Long-Nines** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** After purchasing a Schematic, you must open your Journal and "Learn" it before it will appear as a craftable option at your Shipwright's Workbench.

## The Ascension System: Pushing Beyond Blue Rarity

Once you have mastered the basics of **windrose frigate crafting**, you will find that "Blue" (Rare) items are the standard. However, to survive the late-game elite encounters, you must utilize the **Ascension** system.

Ascension allows you to take a piece of equipment you are fond of and "ascend" it to the next rarity tier (e.g., Blue to Purple). This process is expensive and requires rare materials found only in high-level zones or by defeating world bosses.

### Benefits of Ascending Gear
*   **New Passives:** Every time an item increases in rarity, it unlocks a new passive bonus. This could be anything from a 10% reduction in reload time to a chance to ignite enemy sails on hit.
*   **Increased Base Stats:** A Purple-tier hull will always provide significantly more health than its Blue counterpart.
*   **Build Synergy:** By carefully selecting which items to ascend, you can create specialized builds, such as a "Tank" ship with massive health and regeneration or a "Glass Cannon" focused entirely on reload speed and crit damage.

## Combat Consumables: The Captain's Secret Stash

Even the best-crafted frigate will eventually take damage. Part of the **windrose frigate crafting** loop involves the production of consumables that keep you in the fight.

1.  **Repair Kits:** These are effectively "health potions" for your ship. You should never leave the wharf without a full stack. If you run low during a long engagement, sink enemy ships and loot the floating debris; they frequently drop extra kits.
2.  **Grogs:** These provide temporary buffs to your crew. Using a Grog before a fight can increase your damage output or decrease the damage your ship takes by a significant percentage.
3.  **Chainshot:** This is a secondary ammunition type. While standard roundshot is for the hull, Chainshot is designed to take out sails. A "Hindered" ship moves much slower, allowing you to stay in its blind spot (the rear) where it cannot fire back.

## Effective Resource Farming for Shipbuilders

To keep up with the demands of high-level crafting, you need a steady stream of **Piastas** (Spanish silver coins). While you can get these from general piracy, there are more efficient methods:
*   **Selling Artifacts:** This is the absolute best way to make money. Look at the description of any Artifact you find; it will tell you which specific vendor (Fender) to take it to for the maximum payout.
*   **Lost Goods:** Keep an eye out for white glints on the ocean surface. These lost goods often contain refined materials that save you time on gathering.
*   **Faction Missions:** Completing tasks for the Smugglers or Buccaneers often rewards you with both Piastas and the specific materials needed for their unique schematics.

For more information on ship statistics and naval history, you can check out resources like the [Naval History and Heritage Command](https://www.history.navy.mil/) for inspiration on classic frigate designs.

## FAQ

**Q: Where do I get the materials for windrose frigate crafting?**

A: Basic materials like Wood and Copper Ore are found on the starting islands. Advanced materials and Schematics are found in Tortuga and by increasing your Reputation with factions like the Smugglers.

**Q: How do I use the Schematics I bought in Tortuga?**

A: You must first go into your inventory or Journal and "Learn" the schematic. After that, the item will appear as a crafting option at your Shipwright's Workbench back at your base.

**Q: Can I upgrade my ship's rarity?**

A: Yes, through the Ascension system. You can take Blue (Rare) gear and upgrade it to Purple (Epic) using rare materials. This unlocks additional passive bonuses for your ship.

**Q: What is the best way to get Piastas for crafting?**

A: The most efficient method is selling Artifacts to the correct Fenders. Each Artifact description indicates which vendor will pay the highest price for it. Killing elite pirates and looting "Lost Goods" at sea are also great secondary sources.

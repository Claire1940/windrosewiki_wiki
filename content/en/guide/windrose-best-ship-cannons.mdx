## Unlocking Your Naval Potential

Before you can worry about the heavy-hitting artillery, you must first secure a vessel and the infrastructure to maintain it. The journey begins with the main quest, "I need a bigger boat." This quest acts as the gatekeeper for all naval content in Windrose. Upon completing the restoration of the broken vessel, you gain the ability to construct a Shipwright’s Workshop at your camp.

The Shipwright’s Workshop is the heart of your naval operations. This is where you will build, upgrade, and "ascend" your gear. While the **windrose best ship cannons** eventually come from rare blueprints found in the world, your early-game survival depends on the basic cannons you craft and improve here.

### Essential Materials for Early Upgrades

Upgrading your ship is a resource-intensive endeavor. Copper is the lifeblood of the shipyard, required for almost every modification.

| Material | Primary Source | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Copper** | Mining nodes & Disassembling weapons | Base for cannons, bracing, and gear |
| **Nails** | Crafted from Copper or found in Shipwrecks | Essential for Hull Bracing and structural upgrades |
| **Piesta** | Selling artifacts to Smugglers | Currency for rare blueprints and high-tier gear |
| **Insignias** | Looted from Blackbeard Pirates | Used to increase Faction Reputation |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't ignore shipwrecks along the coastline. They are the most efficient source of nails, saving you a massive amount of copper that would otherwise be spent at the crafting bench.

## Finding the Windrose Best Ship Cannons

While the basic upgrades at your workshop will get you through the initial zones, they pale in comparison to the rare gear available in Tortuga. Located just above the center of the map, Tortuga is a neutral ground for bounty hunters, smugglers, and various archipelago factions.

For those seeking the **windrose best ship cannons**, the Rogue Buccaneers are your primary contact. They sell the blueprints for the devastating **36 pounders**. These cannons are widely considered the gold standard for naval combat due to their massive burst damage.

The 36 pounders deal a staggering 2,500 damage per shot. While the 15-second reload time is longer than standard cannons, the ability to cripple an enemy ship in a single broadside makes them the undisputed choice for high-level play.

## The Reputation Grind: Unlocking Rare Blueprints

You cannot simply buy the **windrose best ship cannons** with Piesta alone. Each faction in Tortuga requires a specific reputation level before they will share their secrets. The Rogue Buccaneers, who hold the plans for the 36 pounders, require you to prove your worth by hunting their enemies.

### How to Earn Reputation Quickly

1.  **Letters of Favor:** These are obtained by completing specific faction quests. Each letter is worth 10 reputation points. Most quests reward at least five letters, making this the fastest way to rank up.
2.  **Insignias:** These drop from Blackbeard Pirates. The difficulty of the pirate determines the value of the insignia. Low-level pirates provide 1 point, while elite captains can provide significantly more.
3.  **Targeted Hunting:** Reputation points are separate for each faction. If you want the best cannons, focus exclusively on quests for the Rogue Buccaneers until you reach the required rank.

> **Warning:** Be careful when turning in insignias. Ensure you are talking to the correct faction representative in Tortuga, as reputation cannot be transferred between groups once spent.

## The Ultimate Vessel: The Frigate

Even the **windrose best ship cannons** are only as good as the platform they are mounted on. While you start with a modest boat, the endgame goal is the Epic Frigate. This ship is sold by the Brethren of the Coast and represents the pinnacle of naval engineering in Windrose.

**Frigate Specifications:**
*   **Total Guns:** 36
*   **Cost:** 3,000 Piesta
*   **Reputation Required:** Level 4 (Brethren of the Coast)
*   **Specialty:** Massive broadside potential and high durability.

To afford the 3,000 Piesta price tag, you should focus on exploring ruins to find high-value artifacts. These contraband items should be taken to the Smuggler’s Waters and sold at their main base for maximum profit. Keeping an eye out for lost cargo beams during your travels can also provide a steady stream of extra currency.

## Enhancing Combat with Tactics Books

Beyond the physical cannons, you can further optimize your ship's performance using Tactics Books. These are sold by the Smugglers of Port Royal and provide passive bonuses that can turn the tide of a battle.

| Tactics Book | Effect | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ship Shape** | Out-of-combat health regeneration | Essential for long voyages and consecutive battles |
| **Silence the Guns** | Reduces enemy reload speed and damage | Perfect for brawling with larger, slower ships |
| **Boarding Drill** | Increases crew damage during boarding | Best for players who prefer to capture ships rather than sink them |

The combination of 36 pounders and the "Silence the Guns" tactic creates a massive advantage. By hitting the enemy first, you reduce their ability to fire back, effectively mitigating the longer reload time of your heavy artillery.

## Maintaining and Equipping Your Gear

A common mistake new captains make is attempting to upgrade their gear while at sea. In Windrose, all ship modifications must be handled through a Wharf.

*   **Proximity:** You can only equip or swap items like cannons when your ship is docked or very close to a built Wharf.
*   **The Upgrade Loop:** To upgrade an existing cannon, you must first unequip it at the Wharf, carry it to the Shipwright’s Workshop for the upgrade, and then return to the Wharf to re-install it.
*   **Customization:** The Wharf is also where you can rename your ship and change its hull colors, allowing you to strike fear into your enemies with a personalized aesthetic.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the windrose best ship cannons for the mid-game?**

A: Before you have the reputation for 36 pounders, the Upgraded Long Nines are the best choice. They offer a significant damage boost over basic cannons and can be crafted using copper and standard workshop upgrades.

**Q: Where can I find the Rogue Buccaneers?**

A: The Rogue Buccaneers are located in the merchant town of Tortuga, which is situated just above the middle of the world map. They act as the primary vendor for high-tier cannon blueprints.

**Q: Is the Frigate worth the 3,000 Piesta cost?**

A: Yes. The Frigate allows you to mount 36 guns simultaneously. When paired with the **windrose best ship cannons**, it becomes the most powerful offensive force in the game, capable of taking down elite pirate forts and legendary sea creatures.

**Q: How do I get more copper for cannon upgrades?**

A: The most efficient way to get copper is to use a Disassembly Table on weapons found at points of interest. Additionally, mining copper nodes in the mountainous regions of the starter islands provides a steady supply for early-game crafting.

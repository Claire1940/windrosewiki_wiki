## Optimizing Your Combat Controls

Before engaging with the local wildlife, you must ensure your controls are optimized for high-speed encounters. The default keybindings in Windrose can feel cumbersome during intense duels. Many veteran players recommend moving the **Target Lock** function from the default 'T' key to the **Middle Mouse Button**. This allows you to keep your fingers on the movement keys while maintaining focus on your opponent.

Note that remapping to the middle mouse button will require you to move the "Heavy Attack" and "Toggle Destroy Mode" functions. Consider the following layout for a more fluid experience:

| Action | Default Key | Recommended Key | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Target Lock** | T | Middle Mouse | Faster access; more intuitive for Souls-like combat. |
| **Heavy Attack** | Middle Mouse | F1 or Mouse 4 | Prevents accidental heavy swings during locks. |
| **Toggle Destroy Mode** | Middle Mouse | F2 | Building actions don't need high-speed accessibility. |
| **Dodge** | Left Control | Mouse 5 | Keeps movement (WASD) completely free. |

## Advanced Combat Mechanics

Combat in Windrose is a dance of stamina management and rhythmic timing. A common mistake for beginners is "turtleing"—holding the block button indefinitely. While blocking prevents damage, it completely halts your stamina regeneration. To succeed, you must drop your guard between enemy swings to let your stamina bar refill.

### Parrying and Shield Breaking
Most enemies have shield icons displayed beneath their health bars. These represent their posture or "stun" meter. By successfully parrying—blocking just as an attack is about to land—you deal massive posture damage. Once all shield icons are broken, the enemy enters a stunned state, allowing for a flurry of safe attacks.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Watch for enemies that emit a red glow or sparkle during an attack animation. These are "Unblockable Attacks" that cannot be parried. You must use your dodge (Left Control) to avoid these entirely.

### The Rally System
Windrose features a health recovery mechanic similar to "rally" systems in other action RPGs. When you take damage, a faded section remains on your health bar for a few seconds. If you deal damage to an enemy during this brief window, you will recover a portion of that lost health. This encourages aggressive counter-play rather than immediate retreat.

## Understanding Weapon Scaling and Stats

Every weapon you find or craft in Windrose scales with specific character attributes. This is indicated by a letter grade (S, A, B, C, D, E) and a corresponding icon. Understanding these symbols is vital for building a powerful character.

| Icon | Attribute | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Rabbit** | **Agility** | Increases damage for fast, dex-based weapons. |
| **Bicep** | **Strength** | Increases damage for heavy, crushing weapons. |
| **Crosshair** | **Precision** | Enhances ranged weapon effectiveness. |
| **Eye** | **Mastery** | Affects specialized skill and elemental damage. |
| **Heart** | **Vitality** | Directly boosts your total health pool. |
| **Lung** | **Endurance** | Increases your total stamina for more actions. |

For example, a **Sturdy Saber** with an 'A' grade in Agility will become significantly more powerful as you invest points into that specific stat. Always check the bottom right of the weapon tooltip to see which attribute you should prioritize.

## Survival and Nutrition Guide

Unlike many survival games where hunger is merely a nuisance, food in Windrose is the primary driver of your character's stats. Your health bar is dynamic; it expands or shrinks based on the quality of the food you have consumed. You have two dedicated food slots and one elixir slot.

### The Coconut Strategy
As soon as you spawn on the starting island, head to the nearest palm trees. Look for coconuts growing at the top and interact with them (E) to harvest. Eating a raw coconut provides a 7-minute buff to your maximum health. This is the easiest way to ensure you don't get "one-shot" by early-game threats like boars.

### Cooking for Success
As you progress, you should move away from raw fruit and toward cooked meals. Combinations like **Boiled Crab** and **Coconut Milk with Bananas** can provide 15-minute buffs that significantly increase both health and endurance. Always keep your food slots filled before entering a dungeon or sailing to a new island.

## Base Building and the "Rested" Mechanic

Your base is more than just a place to store loot; it is a sanctuary that provides the "Rested" buff. This buff increases your stamina regeneration speed and expands your total stamina pool. The effectiveness of this buff is determined by your base's **Comfort Level**.

### Maximizing Comfort
To raise your comfort level, you must place unique decorative items within the radius of your bonfire. Building multiple items of the same type (e.g., five chairs) will not stack the bonus. You must build one item from each distinct category.

| Category | Example Item | Comfort Increase |
|---|---|---|
| **Seating** | Wooden Chair | +1 |
| **Storage/Bed** | Wardrobe / Bed | +1 |
| **Table** | Dining Table | +1 |
| **Decoration** | Canopy / Rug | +1 |
| **Lighting** | Standing Torch | +1 |

### Efficient Building Tips
*   **Resource Refund:** Dismantling any structure provides a 100% refund of the original resources. Do not be afraid to experiment with your layout.
*   **Roof Requirements:** Crafting stations like the Weaponsmith Workshop require a roof to function. If a station says it is "exposed," it will not work.
*   **Smart Storage:** When standing near your chests, you can press 'Q' to automatically deposit items that already have a stack in a nearby container. This saves hours of manual inventory management.
*   **Camera Modes:** Press 'Q' while in build mode to switch to "High Camera Mode." This provides a bird's-eye view, making it much easier to align walls and foundations.

## Exploration and Naval Navigation

With 30 islands to discover, exploration is the heart of the Windrose experience. However, traveling between islands can be dangerous if you aren't prepared.

### The Fast Travel System
You can craft **Fast Travel Bells** at your workbench using 10 Copper Ingots and 3 Rope. You are limited to 9 active bells at any time. A pro-tip for explorers is to always keep one bell in your inventory. If you find yourself deep in a dangerous territory with a full inventory, drop the bell, teleport home to unload, and then teleport back to your exact location.

### Ship Maintenance
You obtain your first boat after mining copper and speaking to the **Doctor** NPC at your bonfire. A unique feature of Windrose is the ability to "summon" your boat to your current location by pressing **'K'**. This works even if your boat is on the other side of the island. If your ship is destroyed in combat, you must build a **Wharf** at your base to restore it for a small resource fee.

### Scavenging Shipwrecks
Don't ignore the debris scattered along the shorelines. Destroying shipwrecks and crates provides free Wood, Plant Fiber, and—most importantly—**Nails**. Crafting nails requires refined metal, so finding them for free in the early game is a massive boost to your building capabilities.

For more information on ship upgrades and late-game materials, check the [Windrose Steam Community Hub](https://steamcommunity.com/app/windrose) for the latest patch notes and player-made maps.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get the Doctor to move into my base?**

A: The Doctor is introduced after you mine your first batch of copper. If he doesn't appear automatically, go to your **Bonfire**, select the "Settle" option, and choose the Doctor from the list of available NPCs.

**Q: Why is my health bar so small even though I'm at full health?**

A: In Windrose, your maximum health is determined by the food you eat. If you are hungry or have only eaten low-quality food, your health bar will shrink. Use these **windrose guides** to find recipes like Boiled Crab to expand your health pool.

**Q: Can I parry every attack in the game?**

A: No. While most physical strikes can be parried, any attack accompanied by a red glow or sparkle is unblockable. You must use your dodge maneuver to avoid these specific hits.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many Fast Travel Bells I can place?**

A: Yes, you can currently have a maximum of 9 Fast Travel Bells active at once. Since there are 30 islands, you should place them strategically at major hubs or resource-rich locations.

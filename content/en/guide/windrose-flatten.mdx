## How to Unlock and Craft the Shovel

Before you can begin manipulating the earth, you must acquire the primary tool for the job: the shovel. Unlike basic stone tools that are available from the very start of the game, the shovel requires a bit of industrial progress. You cannot simply find it lying around; it must be forged through your own efforts at a workbench.

The first step in this journey is the discovery and processing of copper. Copper is a mid-tier resource that marks your transition from the primitive age to more advanced survival. Once you have located copper ore in the world, you must smelt it into bars. Only after you have successfully produced copper bars will the shovel recipe become available at your workbench.

| Material | Quantity Required | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Copper Bars** | 2-4 | Smelted from Copper Ore in a Furnace |
| **Wood** | 3-5 | Gathered from trees using an axe |
| **Workbench** | Level 1 | Basic crafting station required for assembly |

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry extra wood in your inventory when heading out for a landscaping project. Shovels have durability, and while the copper is the main cost, having wood on hand allows you to craft replacements quickly if you have a portable workbench or are near your base.

## Understanding Shovel Modes and Controls

The shovel in Windrose is a versatile multi-tool. It isn't just for moving dirt; it can also serve as a rudimentary defensive weapon if you are caught off guard by local wildlife. However, its primary utility lies in its three distinct terrain manipulation modes. 

Once the shovel is equipped, you can cycle through its various functions using the **Z** and **X** keys on your keyboard. Understanding which mode to use is the difference between a clean building site and a chaotic mess of holes and mounds.

1.  **Weapon Mode:** Use the shovel to strike enemies. While not as effective as a sword or mace, it provides decent knockback.
2.  **Dig Mode:** This mode allows you to remove layers of earth. It is essential for creating trenches, basements, or uncovering buried items.
3.  **Raise Mode:** Use this to fill in holes or build up the elevation of a specific area. It consumes "dirt" resources often gathered while digging.
4.  **Flatten Mode:** The most important tool for builders, used to level the ground to a consistent height based on where you are standing.

| Key Bind | Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Z / X** | Cycle Modes | Switches between Weapon, Dig, Raise, and Flatten |
| **Left Click** | Execute Action | Performs the specific task of the selected mode |
| **Right Click** | Secondary | Often used for blocking or alternative interactions |

## Mastering the Windrose Flatten Technique

The **windrose flatten** mechanic is specifically designed to align the surrounding terrain with the height of the tile you are currently targeting. This is particularly useful when you have used the "Raise" function and ended up with awkward, jagged hills that prevent you from placing floor tiles or walls.

To use this effectively, stand on the elevation you want the rest of the area to match. Select the flatten option and click on the adjacent uneven ground. The game will automatically adjust the height to create a smooth, level surface. This process is essential for creating large-scale farms where irrigation and movement need to be unimpeded.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when flattening near water sources. Accidentally lowering the terrain near a shoreline can cause localized flooding or ruin the placement of water-based structures like docks or water wheels.

## Advanced Landscaping and Base Foundations

When planning a massive fortress, the **windrose flatten** process should be your first priority after clearing trees and rocks. Attempting to build on unlevel ground often leads to "unsupported" structural messages, which can cause your buildings to collapse.

### Creating Defensive Trenches
By combining the Dig mode with the Flatten mode, you can create perfectly vertical moats around your base. Dig a perimeter line, then use the flatten tool at the bottom of the trench to ensure your movement isn't slowed down by small bumps while patrolling the area.

### Terraced Farming
In mountainous biomes, a single flat plane might not be possible. In these instances, use the shovel to create "steps" or terraces. Use the **windrose flatten** feature on each individual tier to create multiple levels of flat soil, perfect for different types of crops that may have varying sunlight or moisture requirements.

| Project Type | Recommended Mode | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Foundation** | Flatten | Easy |
| **Defensive Moat** | Dig + Flatten | Medium |
| **Mountain Path** | Raise + Flatten | Hard |
| **Treasure Hunting** | Dig | Easy |

## Finding Treasures and Hidden Chests

Beyond mere construction, the shovel is your ticket to wealth. Throughout the world of Windrose, you will encounter various landmarks or maps that hint at buried treasure. These chests are often completely submerged beneath the surface, making them invisible to the naked eye.

When you suspect a treasure is nearby, switch your shovel to Dig mode. You can dig quite deep into the earth to uncover these rewards. Once the chest is exposed, you can interact with it to claim your loot. After you have finished looting, it is good practice to use the **windrose flatten** or Raise modes to fill the hole back in, preventing you or your mounts from falling into it later.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I see the shovel recipe at my workbench?**

A: You must first obtain copper ore and smelt it into copper bars using a furnace. The recipe for the shovel is gated behind the discovery of copper materials. Once you have the bars in your inventory, the workbench will display the crafting requirements.

**Q: Does the windrose flatten tool use up resources?**

A: Generally, flattening adjusts the existing earth. However, if you are significantly raising the ground to meet a higher level, you may need to have dirt or soil in your inventory, which is collected while using the Dig mode.

**Q: Can I use the shovel to mine stone or ore?**

A: No, the shovel is specifically designed for soft terrain like dirt, sand, and grass. To harvest stone or metal ores, you will need to craft and use a pickaxe. Using a shovel on hard rock will deplete its durability without yielding resources.

**Q: How do I stop the "windrose flatten" tool from making the ground too low?**

A: The flatten tool levels the ground to the height of the specific spot you are clicking on or the spot you are standing on, depending on your current camera angle. To avoid lowering the ground too much, ensure you are targeting a "high" point that you want to maintain as the standard elevation for your project.

## Essential Windrose Beginner Guide: Combat Fundamentals

Combat in Windrose is more than just clicking until an enemy falls. It requires a tactical approach to stamina, positioning, and timing. One of the first things you should do is adjust your settings. By default, the Target Lock is bound to the 'T' key, which many players find cumbersome during intense encounters. Navigating to your mouse and keyboard settings and rebinding Target Lock to the **Middle Mouse Button** is highly recommended for better accessibility.

### Stamina and Blocking
A common mistake for new players is holding the block button (Right Click) indefinitely. While blocking prevents damage, it halts your stamina regeneration entirely. If you are out of stamina and continue to hold block, you will remain vulnerable and unable to dodge or counter-attack.

> **💡 Tip:** Always drop your block between enemy swings to allow your stamina bar to refill rapidly. Stamina management is the difference between victory and death.

### Parrying and Shield Breaking
Most enemies have shield icons displayed beneath their health bars. These represent their "Stagger" or "Poise" meter. You can break these shields through well-timed parries—blocking just before an attack lands—or by using heavy weapons. Once all shield icons are removed, the enemy enters a stunned state, allowing for massive damage.

| Weapon Type | Parrying Ease | Primary Scaling | Playstyle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Saber/Agility** | High | Agility | Fast, reactive counters |
| **Crusher/Strength** | Low | Strength | Heavy hits, shield breaking |
| **Precision Weapons** | N/A | Precision | Ranged kiting |

### The Health Recovery Window
Windrose features a "rally" mechanic similar to modern action RPGs. When you take damage, a portion of your health bar remains faded for a short duration. If you deal damage to an enemy during this window, you will recover that faded health. This encourages a more aggressive "calculated risk" playstyle rather than simply running away after every hit.

## Survival and Nutritional Mastery

Survival in 2026 requires more than just avoiding death; it requires optimization. Your total health pool is directly tied to the food you consume. Unlike other survival games where food just prevents starvation, in Windrose, food acts as a primary buff system.

### The Food Slot System
You have access to two food slots and one elixir/potion slot. These buffs do not stack with identical items but can be combined for significant stat boosts. For example, consuming a **Boiled Crab** for health and **Coconut Milk with Bananas** for endurance provides a balanced foundation for exploration.

| Item | Effect | Duration | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coconut** | Increases Max Health | 7 Minutes | Palm Trees (Hit 'E') |
| **Boiled Crab** | Increases Max Health | 15 Minutes | Cooking Station |
| **Elixir of Precision** | +13% Ranged Damage | Variable | Alchemy/Loot |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you see the "Hungry" icon in the bottom left, your health bar will shrink to its base size, making you an easy target for elite enemies like Boars.

## Base Building and the Comfort System

Your base is your sanctuary, but it also serves as a source of powerful buffs. The most important mechanic to understand here is the **Rested Buff**. This buff increases your stamina pool and regeneration speed, which is essential for both combat and resource gathering.

### Increasing Comfort Levels
Comfort is determined by the variety of decorations in your base. Building multiple items from the same category (e.g., three chairs) will not increase your comfort. Instead, you must place one unique item from each decoration category.

| Decoration Category | Example Item | Comfort Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Seating** | Wooden Chair | +1 Level |
| **Table** | Simple Desk | +1 Level |
| **Storage/Bed** | Wardrobe / Canopy | +1 Level |
| **Lighting** | Wall Torch | +1 Level |

### Building Efficiency Tips
*   **100% Refund:** You receive all resources back when dismantling structures. Don't be afraid to experiment with your layout.
*   **Roof Requirements:** Crafting stations like the Weaponsmith Workshop require a roof to function. Always build a shelter before placing your high-tier benches.
*   **Proximity Crafting:** Crafting stations automatically pull resources from any chests within the bonfire's radius. You do not need to have the items in your personal inventory.
*   **Quick Deposit:** Stand near a chest and press 'Q' to automatically deposit items that already have a stack inside that chest.

## Exploration and Naval Travel

With over 30 islands to discover, exploration is the heart of the experience. However, the seas are dangerous, and getting lost can be a setback.

### The Fast Travel Bell
Early in the game, you can craft **Fast Travel Bells** using copper ingots and rope. You can have up to nine of these active at once across the world.
1.  Place a bell at your base.
2.  Carry a spare bell while exploring.
3.  If your inventory is full, drop the spare bell, teleport home, deposit loot, and teleport back.
4.  Pick up the bell and continue your journey.

### Ship Management
You don't need to worry about crafting a boat immediately. After mining copper and speaking to the **Doctor NPC** at your bonfire, he will provide your first vessel. 

> **💡 Tip:** You can summon your ship to your current location at any time by pressing 'K'. There is currently no cooldown on this feature, making it incredibly useful for coastal navigation.

If your ship is destroyed in combat, return to your base and use the **Wharf** to restore it for a small resource fee. For more information on official updates, keep an eye on the [Windrose Steam Page](https://store.steampowered.com/search/?term=Windrose) for developer logs.

## Early Game Resource Scavenging

Before you have the tools to mine high-tier ores, you can find many essential components by scavenging the shoreline. Shipwrecks and crates scattered along the beaches are gold mines for early survivors.

*   **Nails:** While craftable later, you can find them for free by destroying crates and ship debris on the beach.
*   **Plant Fiber:** Essential for rope and basic clothing, gathered from bushes and ship wreckage.
*   **Copper:** Your first major progression milestone. Look for brownish ore veins inland.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get the Doctor NPC to appear at my base?**

A: The Doctor typically becomes available after you mine your first piece of copper. If he doesn't appear automatically, go to your bonfire, interact with it, and select the "Settle" option to bring him into your camp.

**Q: What is the best way to handle the "Kill a Boar" quest?**

A: This quest appears early in this **windrose beginner guide** progression, but boars are surprisingly tough. Do not rush them with basic gear. Practice parrying on dodos first, craft at least one piece of hide armor, and use the health recovery window to stay in the fight.

**Q: Can I move my bonfire once it is placed?**

A: Yes. Because the game offers a 100% resource refund on dismantling, you can destroy your bonfire and rebuild it elsewhere. Just remember that your building radius is tied to the bonfire's location, so you may need to move other structures as well.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many boats I can own?**

A: You will start with a basic boat provided by the Doctor, but as you progress and upgrade your Wharf, you can unlock larger, more durable ships. You can summon whichever ship is currently set as your "active" vessel using the 'K' key.

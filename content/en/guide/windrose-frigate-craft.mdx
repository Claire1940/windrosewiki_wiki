## Mastering the Windrose Frigate Craft Process

Before you can even look at a Frigate blueprint, you must understand the progression system in *Windrose*. Ships are not merely bought; they are earned through reputation and crafted with sweat and steel. The journey to a Frigate begins with mastering the smaller vessels like the Ketch and the Brig.

To unlock the Frigate design, you must reach **Level 4 Reputation** with the relevant faction, such as the Brethren of the Coast. This is achieved by turning in bounties and Letters of Favor to the Bounty Agent. Once you hit this milestone, the Frigate blueprint becomes available for purchase at the Curios shop or through specific provisioners.

### Reputation and Unlock Requirements

| Ship Tier | Required Reputation Level | Primary Role | Gun Count (Average) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ketch** | Level 0 | Starter / Scouting | 2-4 |
| **Brig** | Level 2 | Combat / Cargo | 14 |
| **Frigate** | **Level 4** | Heavy Combat / Tank | 20+ |

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry Letters of Favor. Each letter is worth 10 reputation points, which can significantly skip the grind when trying to reach level 4 for the Frigate unlock.

## Essential Materials for Shipbuilding

The **windrose frigate craft** is resource-intensive. You cannot rely on basic wood and stone for a vessel of this magnitude. You will need to establish a robust supply chain involving Tier 3 workbenches and advanced smelting techniques.

### Gathering Foothills Iron and Copper
Iron is the backbone of any advanced ship. You can find Foothills Iron in specific cave systems. Interestingly, resources in *Windrose* respawn based on time and player proximity. If you leave a torch or a bonfire in a cave, resources will often respawn around it, allowing for efficient farming loops.

### Processing Timber and Fabric
Standard wood won't suffice for a Frigate's hull. You must upgrade your workbench to **Tier 3** using nails and iron ingots. This allows you to process Hardwood into Timber. Additionally, you will need a massive amount of Flax to create Linen Fabric.

| Material | Source | Processed Into | Requirement Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hardwood** | Forest Biomes | **Timber** | Structural Hull |
| **Flax Fibers** | Open Fields | **Linen Fabric** | Sails & Rigging |
| **Iron Ore** | Foothills Caves | **Iron Ingots** | Reinforcements |
| **Copper Ore** | Coastal Mines | **Nails** | Assembly |

## Step-by-Step Crafting Guide

Once you have the blueprint and the reputation, you must head to a Wharf Shipyard. The **windrose frigate craft** menu will display several variants. Choosing the right one depends entirely on your playstyle—whether you prefer a "tanky" ship with high hit points or a fast "raider" with heavy cannons.

1.  **Purchase Shipwright Tools:** These are specialized items usually bought from the Brethren of the Coast for silver. You will likely need 30+ units for a Frigate.
2.  **Upgrade Your Stations:** Ensure your Smelter and Workbench are at the maximum level. You cannot craft Timber or Iron Nails without Tier 3 stations.
3.  **Mass Produce Nails:** One Iron Ingot typically yields 10 nails. For a Frigate, expect to need upwards of 300-500 nails.
4.  **Farm Flax:** Linen fabric is a major bottleneck. Scout the fields for purple-flowered flax plants and harvest every one you see.
5.  **Assemble at the Wharf:** Bring all materials to the shipyard. Ensure your current ship is "Reserved" so you have the space to commission your new flagship.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When switching ships, remember to manually transfer your cannons and repair kits. Your new ship will come with empty slots!

## Comparing Frigate and Brig Variants

While the Frigate is the goal, understanding the variant system (introduced with the Brig) is vital for the **windrose frigate craft**. Most ships in *Windrose* come in three distinct flavors:

| Variant Name | Focus | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Balanced | Reliable all-rounder | No specialized buffs |
| **Brethren** | Tank/Cargo | **+20,000 HP**, Large Cargo | Slower Max Speed |
| **Blackbeard** | Offensive | **24lb Cannon Support**, Faster | **-20,000 HP**, Small Cargo |

If you are focusing on the **windrose frigate craft**, the Brethren variant is often preferred for solo players because the massive health pool allows you to survive mistakes during boarding actions. However, if you are part of a fleet, the Blackbeard variant's ability to carry 24-pounders makes it a terrifying glass cannon.

## Advanced Combat Tactics for New Captains

After completing your **windrose frigate craft**, you will notice a significant change in handling. Larger ships have a much wider turning radius. You can no longer "spin on a dime" like you could with a Ketch.

*   **Broadside Management:** With more cannons per side (often double the Brig's count), your damage potential is massive. Aim for the waterline of enemy haulers to sink them quickly.
*   **Boarding Actions:** Use your increased crew size to your advantage. A Frigate can hold more sailors, making it much easier to overwhelm Level 5 haulers during a boarding sequence.
*   **The 12-Pounder Strategy:** Consider using "Perfectly Ordered" 12-pounders. These cannons offer a reload speed buff if you fire consecutive shots within 4 seconds, which is much easier to trigger with the Frigate's high gun count.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the Windrose frigate craft blueprint?**

A: You must reach Reputation Level 4 with a major faction. This is done by completing bounties, sinking enemy raiders, and turning in Letters of Favor to the Bounty Agent at any major port.

**Q: Where can I find the iron needed for the frigate?**

A: Iron is primarily found in Foothills caves. Look for darker ore nodes. Unlike copper, iron requires a Tier 2 or higher pickaxe to mine efficiently.

**Q: Is the Frigate faster than the Brig?**

A: Generally, no. While the Frigate has more sails, its massive weight makes it slightly slower and less maneuverable than the Brig. However, its superior firepower and health pool more than make up for the lack of speed.

**Q: Can I customize the look of my Frigate?**

A: Yes! Once you have completed the **windrose frigate craft**, you can use the "Manage Customization" menu at any Wharf to change the ship's name, hull colors, and figurehead. Popular names include "Blood Flare" and "Lingering Death."

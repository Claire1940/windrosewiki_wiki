## Troubleshooting Server Lag and Stuttering

One of the most frequent **windrose multiplayer issues** reported by the community is persistent stuttering during sailing. This is often caused by "entity bloat." In the current build of the game, items dropped by players or destroyed structures do not always despawn immediately, leading to hundreds of tracked objects that the server must process simultaneously.

Currently, dedicated server hosts lack a direct "clear all" command to wipe these entities. However, there is a built-in mechanic that players can utilize to manage this. Items dropped outside of the "bonfire range" are programmed to disappear over time. If your server is experiencing heavy lag, ensure that players are not hoarding or dropping excessive loot within their base perimeters, as the bonfire prevents the natural cleanup process.

### Managing Server Performance

If you are hosting via a provider like Nitrado, you may notice that performance dips as more players join the world. Since official mod support is not yet available, you cannot rely on performance-enhancing plugins. Instead, follow these manual optimization steps:

1.  **Limit Dropped Entities:** Encourage players to destroy unwanted items in containers rather than dropping them on the ground.
2.  **Monitor Bonfire Density:** Too many active bonfires in a small area can keep too many entities "active" in the server's memory.
3.  **Scheduled Restarts:** Regularly restarting your dedicated server can help clear temporary cache issues and stabilize the tick rate.

## Common Gameplay Bugs in Multiplayer

Beyond connection stability, several gameplay bugs specifically plague the multiplayer experience. These range from UI inconsistencies to critical item loss. Knowing how to identify and circumvent these bugs is the best way to deal with **windrose multiplayer issues** until the next major patch.

### The Disappearing Sailor Backpack
A common complaint involves the Sailor Backpack. When players attempt to unequip the backpack by clicking and dragging it to an empty inventory slot, it often appears to vanish. Kraken Express has clarified that the item is not actually destroyed; instead, it is being "thrown" far away from the character's physical location due to a physics bug.

> **💡 Tip:** To avoid losing your gear, always right-click to unequip or ensure you are dragging items directly into a storage chest rather than an empty inventory slot.

### Controller Support and UI
For those playing on PC with a controller, the UI currently lacks DualSense (PS5) icon support. While the game is playable, it primarily displays Xbox prompts. The developers have confirmed that full controller icon customization is planned to coincide with the future console release, but for now, multiplayer participants must rely on the default Xbox layout.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is my Windrose server so laggy when I'm sailing?**

A: This is one of the most common **windrose multiplayer issues** and is usually caused by too many dropped items in the world. Since there is no "clear entity" command yet, ensure items are dropped outside of a bonfire's range so they can naturally despawn over time.

**Q: Can I install mods to fix multiplayer performance on my Nitrado server?**

A: No, Kraken Express does not officially support mods at this time. Using third-party mods can lead to security risks or further server instability. It is best to wait for official patches from the development team.

**Q: Is there a way to see PS5 icons when playing with a controller?**

A: Not currently. The game defaults to Xbox icons. The developers have stated they hope to add an option to toggle between Xbox and PS5 styles as they approach the official console release.

**Q: My backpack disappeared after I unequipped it. Is it gone forever?**

A: Likely not! It is a known bug where the backpack is launched away from your character rather than being destroyed. Check the surrounding area on the map, as it may have landed nearby. To prevent this, avoid dragging and dropping to unequip.

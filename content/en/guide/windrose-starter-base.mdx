## Essential Materials for Early Construction

Before you begin swinging your hammer, you must gather the necessary resources. In Windrose, the starter island is rich with basic materials that are surprisingly versatile when used with the right building techniques. You will primarily need stone, various types of wood, and plant fibers.

| Material | Primary Source | Best Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Small Stone Blocks** | Surface rocks/mining | Foundations, pillars, and chimneys |
| **Sticks / Light Wood** | Trees and saplings | Second-story walls and decorative trim |
| **Bark / Dark Wood** | Mature tree harvesting | Main level walls for high-contrast texture |
| **Plant Fiber** | Bushes and ground flora | Roofs (Leaf Roofs) and fiber pillars |
| **Copper** | Ore veins in ruins | Basic tools and advanced crafting upgrades |

> **💡 Tip:** Explore ancient ruins on the starter island early. These locations often contain recipes for advanced roof shapes and decorative items that are essential for a polished look.

## Designing Your Windrose Starter Base

A successful build starts with a solid floor plan. For a versatile beginner home, an **8x4 grid** is the "sweet spot." This size is large enough to house all early-game utilities without becoming an unmanageable resource sink. Placing your base partially in the water using piers provides an authentic pirate aesthetic and easy access to your future ships.

### Foundation and Layout Steps

1.  **Placement**: Start by placing a **Large Pier** (found in the pre-built structures section) directly into the water near the shoreline.
2.  **Expansion**: Extend the foundation backward using smaller piers. Aim for a total width of 8 units and a depth of 4 units.
3.  **Stone Reinforcement**: Fill the central sections of your pier foundation with **Small Stone Blocks**. This creates a "timbered brick" look and provides higher structural integrity for the upper floors.
4.  **Pillar Heights**: Raise your corner pillars to a height of four stone blocks. This height allows for a spacious ground floor that won't feel cramped once you start adding storage barrels.

## Master the Windrose Building System

Windrose features a robust building engine that allows for significant creative freedom. Understanding the hotkeys and camera modes is vital for placing blocks in difficult-to-reach areas, such as roof peaks or underwater supports.

| Keybind | Function | Expert Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **V** | Build Camera | Switch to a top-down or detached view for roof placement. |
| **P** | Toggle Snapping | Turn off snapping to "glitch" blocks for custom textures. |
| **Q** | Rotation Angle | Change rotation from 15° to 90° for precise detailing. |
| **Z + Scroll** | Block Height | Manually raise or lower a blueprint before placing it. |
| **Middle Mouse** | Destroy Mode | Quickly remove misplaced blueprints without swapping tools. |

## Multi-Level Functionality

To maximize the efficiency of your **windrose starter base**, you should dedicate each floor to a specific aspect of gameplay. A three-story design allows you to separate messy industrial work from your cozy living quarters.

### Level 1: The Warehouse (Ground Floor)
The ground floor should be dedicated to storage. Use the stone foundation to support rows of barrels and crates. 
*   **Organization**: Place crates in the corners and use wall-mounted signs to label resources like "Copper," "Wood," and "Fiber."
*   **Accessibility**: Keep the front area clear for easy movement between your dock and your storage containers.

### Level 2: The Galley and Workshop
The middle floor serves as the heart of the base. This is where you will do the majority of your crafting and food preparation.
*   **The Chimney**: Build a stone chimney against the back wall. While Windrose doesn't have complex smoke mechanics, a stone range hood over your cooking fire prevents the room from looking empty.
*   **Workbenches**: Place your Workbench, Armorer's Bench, and Weapon Station here. 
*   **Dining Area**: Use stools and tables to create a social space. Adding small details like mugs and bowls makes the base feel lived-in.

### Level 3: The Sleeping Loft
The top level is usually a smaller loft area created by the slope of the roof. 
*   **Resting**: Place your beds here. This keeps them away from the noise of the crafting stations.
*   **Revival Point**: Don't forget to interact with your bed to set your revival point. 
*   **The View**: Use large windows or a "grid window" made of pillars to look out over the ocean and spot incoming ships.

## Exterior Detailing and Smelting

The difference between a boxy hut and a "perfect" **windrose starter base** lies in the external details. Adding texture to the walls and roof ridges gives the building a rugged, pirate-themed appearance.

> **Warning:** Building integrity matters. If your blocks turn yellow or red during placement, they are lacking support. Always ensure your upper floors are supported by pillars that reach the ground or the stone foundation.

### The Smelting Patio
Smelting is a loud, hot process. It is best to build a small stone patio off the back of the house specifically for your Kiln and Furnace.
1.  Extend a stone platform 4x4 units from the back door.
2.  Place the **Kiln** and **Furnace** on the corners.
3.  Add a **Bonfire** for light and additional cooking capacity.
4.  Surround the area with a low stone wall to serve as a firebreak and define the space.

### Adding "Pirate" Texture
*   **Roof Ridges**: Use horizontal pillars along the peaks of your roof to break up the flat leaf texture.
*   **Archways**: Place corner stone walls on the inside of your support pillars to create an arched effect.
*   **Overhangs**: Extend your roof by one unit past the walls. This creates natural shadows and protects your wraparound deck from the rain.
*   **The Flag**: No pirate base is complete without a makeshift flag. Use small sticks and fabric pieces at the highest point of your roof to signal your presence to other players.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to build a windrose starter base?**

A: Look for a flat shoreline near a forest and a stone outcrop. Building partially over the water on piers is ideal because it allows you to park your raft or small boat directly next to your storage crates.

**Q: Do I need to worry about my base burning down?**

A: Currently, fire from the furnace or kiln does not spread to wooden walls. However, using stone for the smelting area and the chimney is highly recommended for aesthetic realism and structural stability.

**Q: How do I unlock the leaf roof recipes?**

A: You must find and interact with ancient ruins scattered around the starter island. These ruins often contain "Lost Blueprints" that automatically unlock new building pieces in your crafting menu.

**Q: Can I build a windrose starter base on a mountain?**

A: Yes, but it is much more difficult. You will need to spend significantly more time building tall support pillars to create a level foundation. For beginners, the beach is the most efficient choice.

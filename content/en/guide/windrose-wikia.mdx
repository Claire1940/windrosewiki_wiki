## Mastering the Windrose Wikia Building System

The building mechanics in Wind Rose differ significantly from other survival titles. One of the most player-friendly features is the **100% resource recovery rate**. When you deconstruct a piece of your base, you receive every single material back into your inventory. This encourages experimentation and allows you to refine your design without the fear of wasting precious supplies like stone or plant fiber.

### Foundation and Elevation
When starting your build, elevation is your best friend. Many players make the mistake of building directly into the sand, which can lead to clipping issues or the need for tedious shoveling. Instead, use piers or raised foundations to create a level playing field.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Use the 'Z' key (or your mapped vertical toggle) to adjust the height of your foundations before snapping them into place. Raising your base slightly above the shoreline prevents sand from bleeding through your floorboards.

## Architectural Design: The Intermediate Fortress

An intermediate base should be functional yet inviting. A popular layout involves a 3x4 or 4x4 main room with a wraparound deck. This design allows for easy access to the water for your ships while providing ample space for interior organization.

### Doors and Windows
Alignment is a common hurdle for new builders. In Wind Rose, double doors often do not snap perfectly to the center of a single foundation block. To solve this, seasoned builders suggest using two single doors side-by-side. This creates a functional double-door entryway that aligns perfectly with your floor plan.

For windows, consider the "cross-breeze" effect. Placing windows on opposite ends of your stone walls not only improves the visual appeal but also makes the interior feel less cramped. Always check the "inside" and "outside" faces of your wall segments; stone walls have distinct textures that should be consistent throughout the build.

### Roofing Techniques
Roofing is where your base truly takes shape. The game offers various pitches, with the **26-degree slope** being a favorite for its balanced look. 

*   **Overhangs:** Always extend your roof one tile past your exterior walls. This prevents the "box" look and gives your fortress a professional architectural finish.
*   **The Attic Space:** If your roof gets too high, consider flattening the top with floor pieces to create a functional attic or a lookout point.
*   **Pillars:** Use pillars to support large overhangs. Even if the game's physics don't strictly require them for every tile, they add a layer of realism and structural logic to your porch.

## Interior Comfort and Crafting Modules

A base is more than just walls; it is a machine for progression. To maximize your efficiency, you must organize your interior according to the **windrose wikia** efficiency standards. This involves grouping your workbenches and maximizing your "Rested" bonus.

### The Rested Bonus
Your bed and surrounding decorations determine the quality of your rest. A simple bed with a headboard (crafted using Triton's horns found on beaches) provides a significantly better buff than a basic bedroll. Adding furniture like chairs, tables, and even a spyglass on a desk increases the comfort level of the room.

### Essential Stations
Every intermediate base should house the following modules:
*   **Shipwright's Workshop:** The core station for upgrading your vessel and crafting advanced naval components.
*   **Cooking Fire:** Best placed on an outdoor deck to keep the interior smoke-free and easily accessible from the water.
*   **Storage Hub:** Categorize your chests into "Hard Materials" (stone, ore), "Soft Materials" (fiber, hide), and "Gear/Food."

> **⚠️ Warning:** Structural integrity matters. If you remove a supporting pillar or wall, the connected roof pieces may collapse. While you usually get resources back, items inside the collapse zone can sometimes glitch through the floor.

## Advanced Decoration and Lighting

Lighting is often overlooked but is crucial for nighttime productivity. While basic torches are cheap, they have a limited light radius. For a truly professional look, use wall-mounted torches near doorways and workbenches. 

For a touch of personality, display your achievements. Trophy crabs and other rare drops can be mounted on walls to showcase your dominance over the island's wildlife. Small touches, like placing a wooden bucket near the entrance or a closet next to the bed, turn a sterile building into a lived-in home.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get 100% of my materials back when destroying a wall in Wind Rose?**

A: The game features a full refund mechanic by default. Simply use the deconstruct tool or keybind on any placed structure. This allows you to move your entire base or redesign sections without losing any gathered resources.

**Q: Why can't I place my door in the center of the wall?**

A: According to the current building logic found in the **windrose wikia**, doors snap to the sides of foundation tiles rather than the center. To achieve a centered double-door look, place two single door frames side-by-side across two foundation tiles.

**Q: What is the best way to farm Plant Fiber for large builds?**

A: Equip a sword or a halberd with a wide swing arc. Head into the densest jungle areas and spam your primary attack. This is significantly faster than using a sickle or manual picking, allowing you to gather hundreds of fibers in minutes.

**Q: Do I need to build a roof over my porch?**

A: While not strictly necessary for structural integrity, adding a roof overhang (supported by pillars) protects you from rain and contributes to your home's comfort rating, which helps maintain your stamina buffs.

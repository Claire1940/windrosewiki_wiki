## Understanding Progression and Loot Mechanics

The most common reason players seek a **windrose coop fix** is the confusion surrounding loot spawns. In Windrose, loot is generated specifically for your character when you visit a Point of Interest (POI). However, each POI has a unique ID that becomes bound to your character once it is looted.

If you have already cleared a specific fortress or shipwreck on your own map and then join a friend's game, you will find those chests empty—even if your friend has never been there. This is not a bug, but a design choice to prevent players from infinitely farming the same locations by jumping between different worlds.

| Feature | Progression Type | Shared with Crew? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Loot Chests** | Character Specific | No (Individual drops per player) |
| **Quest Completion** | Character Specific | Yes (If present during completion) |
| **Faction Reputation** | Map Specific | Yes (Guests inherit host's standing) |
| **Crafting Recipes** | Character Specific | Yes (Shared on touch/pickup) |
| **Ship Upgrades** | Character Specific | No (Tied to individual ship) |

> **💡 Tip:** To maximize efficiency, always ensure your entire crew is loaded into the same session before turning in major quests. If a player joins after a quest is completed, they will not receive credit and may require a manual "fix" by re-running the quest on their own map.

## The Faction Reputation "Hot-Swap" Strategy

One of the most powerful aspects of the Windrose multiplayer system is that faction reputation is tied to the map, not the character. This allows for a clever strategy that functions as a **windrose coop fix** for the long reputation grind.

If you focus on gaining favor with the Merchant's Guild on your map, while a friend focuses on the Corsair's faction on their map, you can both benefit from each other's hard work. By simply joining your friend's world, your character will instantly have access to the high-level gear and ship components they have unlocked. You can purchase these items using your own gold and bring them back to your original world.

### How to Execute a World Swap:
1.  **Coordinate Factions:** Assign each friend a specific faction to "max out."
2.  **Host the Session:** Have the person with the highest reputation host the game.
3.  **Purchase Gear:** All visiting players can now access the faction's exclusive shop.
4.  **Save and Exit:** Your character retains all purchased items when returning to solo play.

## Maximizing Boarding Rewards in Coop

Combat in Windrose becomes significantly more lucrative when playing with others. While sinking a ship provides immediate loot, boarding is the superior choice for crews looking to farm gold quickly. When you board and conquer a ship, every player present on the enemy deck receives a full monetary reward.

| Action | Loot Result | Gold Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sinking a Ship** | Random drops in water | Single payout for the killer |
| **Boarding (Solo)** | High-tier chest loot | Standard gold payout |
| **Boarding (Coop)** | Shared chest (one person) | **Full gold payout for every crew member** |

To optimize this, only one player needs to initiate the boarding sequence with their ship. Once the ships are tethered, the rest of the crew should jump overboard and climb onto the enemy vessel. As long as you are standing on the deck when the final enemy falls, you get paid. This is a vital **windrose coop fix** for players struggling to afford expensive late-game ship upgrades.

## Advanced Combat and Repair Tactics

In the heat of battle, your ship will take significant damage. While repair kits are the standard way to stay afloat, they are slow and resource-intensive. A faster **windrose coop fix** for ship health is the Wharf Repair button.

Visiting any friendly wharf allows you to instantly repair your ship to 100% health for a flat cost of **20 Wood**. This cost does not scale with the amount of damage taken, making it incredibly cost-effective for larger galleons.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Boarding a ship acts as a "combat pause." If you are being swarmed by multiple enemies, boarding one ship will cause the others to temporarily stop their assault. Use this time to breathe, but remember that the battle resumes the moment the boarding sequence ends.

### Critical Hits: Aiming for the Stern
When engaging in ship-to-ship combat, always maneuver your vessel to fire at the "butt" (the stern) of the enemy ship. Hits to the rear deal critical damage, indicated by yellow damage numbers. In the late game, a synchronized volley from a full crew hitting the stern can sink even the most formidable frigates in a single pass.

## Base Building and NPC Management

Your base is anchored by your campfire, but many players don't realize that the campfire is mostly a UI tool for managing NPCs. You can pick up and move your campfire at any time without destroying your structures. This serves as a great **windrose coop fix** if you realize you've placed your base in a suboptimal location.

1.  **Deconstruct the Campfire:** You get your resources back.
2.  **Relocate:** Place it in a new area.
3.  **Resettle NPCs:** Open the campfire interface to re-assign your workers to the new location.
4.  **Building Anywhere:** Remember that you don't actually need a campfire nearby to build. You can place torches in mines to track your path or build platforms to cheese ground-based enemies who cannot jump.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't my friend see the loot in the chest I just opened?**

A: Loot is individual. While you might see a legendary sword, your friend might see gold or nothing at all if they have already looted that specific POI ID on their own character. This is a common point of confusion, but not something that requires a technical **windrose coop fix**.

**Q: How do we share crafting recipes in multiplayer?**

A: Sharing recipes is incredibly easy. If one player finds a recipe for a new hull or weapon, any other player who "touches" or interacts with the item or the recipe scroll will instantly learn it as well.

**Q: Does faction reputation reset when I join a new world?**

A: No, but it changes based on the host. Your character's personal reputation isn't what matters; the map's reputation is. If you join a host who is "Friendly" with the Pirates, you will be "Friendly" while in their world. When you return to your "Hostile" world, your status returns to what it was previously.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get a windrose coop fix for quest sync issues?**

A: The most reliable fix is to have the player who is "behind" on the quest line host the session. This forces the game world to recognize the incomplete objectives, allowing the rest of the crew to help them catch up to the current progression point.

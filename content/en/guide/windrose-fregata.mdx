The **windrose fregata** represents the pinnacle of naval engineering in the current 2026 meta, offering a massive leap in firepower and durability compared to the early-game catches and brigs. ## Choosing Your Windrose Fregata: Brethren vs. Blackbeard

When you reach the stage of building a frigate, you are primarily choosing between two distinct philosophies of naval warfare. The **Brethren Frigate** is the gold standard for players who value survivability and sustained combat, while the **Blackbeard Frigate** serves those who want to maximize raw damage at the cost of defensive utility.

| Feature | Brethren Frigate | Blackbeard Frigate |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base HP** | **200,000** | 140,000 |
| **Max Cannon Size** | 24-Pounders | **36-Pounders** |
| **Cargo Capacity** | **Extremely Large** | Moderate |
| **Maneuverability** | Low (Slow Turning) | Moderate (Agile) |
| **Best Use Case** | Tanking multiple ships | Hit-and-run / High DPS |

The Brethren variant is often described as "steering like a fridge," but its massive health pool makes it the most reliable choice for solo players. With 200,000 HP, you can effectively ignore minor damage while your secondary batteries tear enemies apart. Conversely, the Blackbeard variant allows for the installation of 36-pounder cannons, which provide the highest single-volley damage in the game.

> **💡 Tip:** If you find yourself frequently outnumbered, the Brethren Frigate's high HP pool makes your repair kits significantly more effective, as they restore a percentage of your total health.

## The Best Cannon Loadouts for Frigates

The **windrose fregata** is unique because it utilizes both a main and a secondary battery. This dual-battery system allows you to stack status effects and maintain a constant stream of fire. In the current meta, the **Devastating** cannon type is widely considered the superior choice for both slots.

### Why Devastating Cannons Rule the Meta
The Devastating cannon applies the "Ragged" effect when you hit a target with more than 50% of a volley. This effect increases all damage dealt to the target by 10%, stacking up to three times. When you have two batteries firing Devastating rounds, you can reach that 30% damage bonus almost instantly.

| Cannon Type | Base Effect | Ascension Bonus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Devastating** | Applies Ragged effect (+10% damage) | **+15% Reload Speed** |
| **Perfectly Ordered** | +30% Reload speed after hit | +30% Aiming Speed |
| **Tempered** | +40% damage after 6s wait | +20% Weak Spot Damage |

While the Tempered cannon offers a high damage burst, the 6-second wait requirement drastically lowers your overall DPS. In a frigate, you want to be "pounding" the enemy constantly. By ascending your Devastating cannons, you gain a permanent 15% reload speed increase, which far outweighs the situational benefits of the other types.

## Essential Hull and Naval Tactics

Your choice of hull bracing and naval tactics will determine how long you can stay in a fight. For the **windrose fregata**, the goal is to create a "healing loop" that makes it impossible for enemy ships to outpace your repairs.

### The Killhole Bracing Meta
The **Killhole Hull Bracing** is arguably the most important upgrade for any large ship. Standard repair kits have their duration reduced when you take damage. In a 1v5 battle, this usually means your repair kit is cancelled almost immediately after use. Killhole bracing removes this penalty, ensuring your 10-16 second repair duration always runs to completion.

### Recommended Naval Tactics
1.  **Stretch the Supplies:** This is the top-tier choice for frigates. It increases repair kit duration by 30% and Grog duration by 200%.
2.  **Silence the Guns:** A great offensive choice that reduces enemy reload speed and damage when you hit them.
3.  **No Quarter:** Useful for aggressive players, as it restores 30% health upon destroying an enemy ship within 50 meters.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid the "Ambush" tactic on a frigate. While the 130% damage bonus sounds great, taking even a single point of damage while approaching an enemy will cancel the effect entirely, making it useless in most real-world combat scenarios.

## Boarding Strategies and Crew Gear

Boarding is where you earn double rewards, making it a vital part of the Windrose gameplay loop. As you move into level 12-15 waters with your **windrose fregata**, you will face up to 20 defenders during a boarding action.

### Weapon Recommendations for Boarding
*   **Halberd:** The best melee weapon for boarding due to its wide sweeping light attacks. You can hit 3-5 enemies simultaneously, which is essential when clearing a crowded deck.
*   **Blunderbuss:** Use this as your secondary. It acts as a shotgun, allowing you to blast enemies as they emerge from the stairs or cluster near the railings.
*   **Two-Handed Sword:** A solid alternative to the Halberd, offering good reach and "poke" damage to hit enemies through your own sailors.

### Boarding Party Gear
There is no "shortcut" for boarding gear—you must keep it leveled to match the ships you are attacking. If you are boarding a level 15 frigate, your boarding party gear should also be level 15. This ensures your troopers have enough health and damage to support you while you sweep the deck.

| Gear Level | Target Ship Level | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 6 | Level 1-10 | Easy |
| Level 10 | Level 10-12 | Moderate |
| **Level 15** | **Level 15+** | **Required** |

## Farming Tumbaga Ingots for Upgrades

To ascend your cannons and hull on the **windrose fregata**, you will need a steady supply of **Tumbaga Ingots**. These are rare materials that cannot be found in the starting zones.

To find Tumbaga, head to the **Foothills region** (Level 6-10 area). Look specifically for locations marked as **Ruins** or the **Ancient Fire Sanctuary**. These areas contain chests and nodes that frequently drop Tumbaga Ingots. Exploring these ruins is the fastest way to gather the 30+ ingots required for a fully ascended frigate loadout.

## FAQ

**Q: Which is the best overall windrose fregata for solo play?**

A: The Brethren Frigate is the best for solo players due to its massive 200,000 HP pool. When combined with Killhole Bracing and the Stretch the Supplies tactic, it becomes nearly impossible for AI fleets to sink you.

**Q: Can I use 36-pounder cannons on the Brethren Frigate?**

A: No, the Brethren Frigate is limited to 24-pounder cannons as its maximum size. If you want to use 36-pounders, you must build the Blackbeard Frigate, which has lower health but higher offensive capabilities.

**Q: How do I use Grog on my ship?**

A: You must drag the Grog into your ship's action bar, similar to how you equip repair kits. Once activated, it provides a long-term buff to reload speed or damage, which is further extended by the Stretch the Supplies tactic.

**Q: Is boarding always better than sinking a ship?**

A: Yes, boarding provides double the loot and resources compared to simply sinking a ship. However, it is much riskier, so ensure your boarding party gear and personal weapons are fully upgraded before attempting to board a high-level **windrose fregata**.

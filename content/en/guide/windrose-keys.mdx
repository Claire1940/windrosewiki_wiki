## Optimizing Your Windrose Keys and Controls

The default control scheme in Wind Rose provides a solid foundation, but many veteran players find certain "key" actions to be cumbersome during intense combat. Specifically, the target lock and heavy attack functions often benefit from remapping to more accessible buttons.

One of the most significant improvements you can make is rebinding the target lock. By default, this is set to the **T** key, which can be difficult to reach while managing movement with WASD. Many players prefer moving this to the **Middle Mouse Button**. However, making this change requires you to shift the default actions already assigned to the middle mouse.

| Action | Default Key | Recommended Rebind | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Target Lock** | T | Middle Mouse | Faster access during combat tracking. |
| **Heavy Attack** | Middle Mouse | F1 or Mouse 4 | Prevents accidental heavy swings during locks. |
| **Toggle Destroy Mode** | Middle Mouse | F2 | Only needed during base editing; keeps mouse clear. |
| **Dodge / Roll** | Left Control | Mouse 5 | Allows for simultaneous movement and evasion. |
| **Summon Boat** | K | K | Keep as default; used primarily outside of combat. |

> **💡 Tip:** When rebinding your **windrose keys**, ensure that your "Destroy Mode" is placed on a key you won't hit accidentally, as dismantling base components provides a 100% resource refund but can disrupt your layout.

## Combat Mechanics and Stamina Management

Combat in Wind Rose is more than just clicking until the enemy falls. It utilizes a sophisticated shield and stun system. Most enemies, such as the Coastal Dodo or the aggressive Boars, feature shield icons beneath their health bars. Breaking these shields through parries or heavy attacks will stun the enemy, opening them up for massive damage.

### The Art of the Parry and Dodge
Parrying requires you to block just before an attack connects. While agility-based weapons like the Saber have generous parry windows, heavier strength weapons like the Crusher may feel more sluggish. It is vital to recognize unparriable attacks—these are telegraphed by a distinct **red glow** or sparkle. When you see this, your only option is to use your dodge key.

### The Health Recovery Window
Wind Rose features a "rally" mechanic similar to classic action RPGs. When you take damage, a faded section appears on your health bar. If you deal damage to an enemy immediately after being hit, you can recover that lost health. 

| Mechanic | Effect | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Blocking** | Reduces incoming damage | **Never** hold block constantly; it stops stamina regen. |
| **Parrying** | Breaks enemy shields | Practice timing on weaker dodos before fighting boars. |
| **Dodging** | Full invulnerability frames | Use for red-glow attacks that cannot be parried. |
| **Stamina Pool** | Dictates actions | Increased by the **Rested Buff** and specific foods. |

## Survival Essentials: Food and Health Buffs

Your maximum health in Wind Rose is not a static number. Much like other survival hits, your vitality is heavily dependent on your diet. You have two dedicated food slots and one elixir/potion slot.

### Early Game Nutrition
As soon as you spawn, head to the nearest palm trees. Coconuts are a literal lifesaver, providing a 7-minute buff to your maximum health. As you progress, you will unlock more complex recipes like Boiled Crab or Coconut Milk with Bananas. These provide longer durations and secondary buffs like increased endurance.

1.  **Harvest Coconuts:** Look for trees with visible fruit and press **E**.
2.  **Boil Your Water:** Never consume raw items if a cooked version is available.
3.  **Stack Buffs:** Always keep two different food items active before entering a boss arena or new island.

## Advanced Base Building and Comfort Levels

Building your base is more than just aesthetics; it directly impacts your character's performance through the **Rested Buff**. This buff increases your stamina regeneration speed and expands your total stamina pool. 

### Boosting Comfort
Your comfort level is determined by the variety of furniture in your base. To maximize this, you must place at least one unique item from every decoration category (Tables, Wardrobes, Canopies, etc.). Building multiple items of the same type does not stack the benefit.

| Comfort Level | Stamina Regen Bonus | Stamina Pool Increase |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1-3** | +10% | +5 |
| **Level 4-6** | +25% | +15 |
| **Level 7-10** | +50% | +30 |

### Efficient Crafting and Storage
When building your workshop, remember that crafting stations automatically pull resources from any chests located within the bonfire's radius. You do not need to have the items in your personal inventory. To keep things organized, use the **Q key** while looking at a chest to "Deposit Similar" items instantly.

> **Warning:** Certain advanced crafting stations, like the Weaponsmith Workshop, require a roof to function. If a station is marked as "Inoperable," check your overhead cover.

## Nautical Exploration and Fast Travel

Navigating the 30+ islands requires a reliable vessel and a strategic approach to travel. You will receive your first boat from the **Doctor NPC** after mining your first batch of copper. Once you have a ship, you can summon it to any shoreline by pressing the **K key**.

### Fast Travel Bells
Fast travel is handled through Bells. You can craft these at a workbench using copper ingots and rope. However, you are limited to **nine active bells** at any given time. Since there are dozens of islands, you must be selective about where you place your permanent teleportation points.

*   **Scavenging:** Destroy shipwrecks along the coast to find free nails and wood.
*   **Ship Repair:** If your vessel is destroyed, visit the **Wharf** to restore it for a small fee.
*   **Inventory Management:** Use a bell to return to base, deposit loot using the **windrose keys** shortcuts, and teleport back to your exploration point immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the Doctor NPC at my base?**

A: The Doctor appears after you have successfully mined copper. If he doesn't show up immediately, interact with your bonfire and select the "Settle" option to manually bring him into your camp.

**Q: Can I change my windrose keys settings mid-game?**

A: Yes, you can access the mouse and keyboard settings at any time through the main menu. Changes take effect immediately, which is helpful for testing different target lock configurations.

**Q: Do I lose resources when I destroy a building?**

A: No, Wind Rose is very player-friendly in this regard. You receive 100% of the crafting materials back when you dismantle a structure using the "Destroy Mode" (defaulted to Middle Mouse or your chosen rebind).

**Q: What is the fastest way to increase my stamina?**

A: The fastest way is to maintain a high Comfort Level at your base and ensure you always have the "Rested" buff active. Additionally, eating foods like Coconut Milk with Bananas provides a flat increase to your endurance stat.

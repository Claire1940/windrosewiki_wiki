To begin this process, you must visit Tortuga and speak with the various faction provisioners. These NPCs sell merchant contracts that, once purchased and placed in your base via the build menu, allow you to order crates of resources. ## Faction Reputations and Merchant Contracts

Unlocking the best **windrose frigate mats** requires building rapport with the three major factions found in Tortuga and Port Royal. Each faction specializes in different resource categories, and as you increase your reputation, the quality of goods they offer improves significantly.

1.  **The People of Tortuga:** They provide the initial contracts for food and natural resources. This is where you will get your primary supply of timber and basic crops.
2.  **The Buccaneers:** Found in Tortuga, these rugged sailors sell animal product contracts. Initially, they only offer basic meats and feathers, but beating regional bosses unlocks higher-tier hides necessary for advanced ship padding.
3.  **The Smugglers of Port Royal:** These are essential for high-end ship components. At Reputation Rank 2, they sell the Keelhold Hull Bracing, which is arguably the best defensive upgrade for mid-game ships.
4.  **Brethren of the Coast:** Reaching Tier 2 with this group is mandatory if you want to move beyond the basic ships and purchase the Brig (often referred to by the community when discussing frigate-class vessels).

## Silver Farming: Financing Your Fleet

The biggest hurdle to using merchants for your **windrose frigate mats** is the cost. Ordering boxes can cost upwards of 500 silver each. To maintain this lifestyle, you must master the art of boarding.

Look for ships on the horizon with a **Chest Symbol** above them. These are treasure galleons or well-stocked merchant vessels. Simply sinking them provides standard loot, but boarding them yields a massive silver bonus. 

*   **Level 2-3 Ships:** ~50 Silver per successful boarding.
*   **Level 5-8 Ships:** 150 to 200 Silver.
*   **Level 10+ Ships:** 300+ Silver.

To board successfully, you must upgrade your boarding gear at the Shipwright’s Workshop. If your soldiers are under-geared, they will be slaughtered before you can claim the prize. While your cannons and hull should always be at the maximum level for your region, your boarding gear can safely lag about five levels behind without sacrificing too much efficiency.

## Advanced Material Processing: Linen and Flax

As you transition into the Foothills region, you will notice that **windrose frigate mats** become more complex. Linen is a primary requirement for the sails of larger ships like the Brig. Unlike wood, which can be easily ordered, high-quality Linen requires a farming loop.

You must find Flax in the Foothills. Once you harvest it, you will receive both the fiber and Flax Seeds. Don't just turn all your fiber into Linen immediately; use the seeds to start a plantation in your base.

### Building Seedbeds
To create an infinite supply of Linen, you need Seedbeds. These require **Fertile Soil**, which can be found in ruins marked with a soil symbol on your map. Use a pickaxe on the "packed dirt" mounds to gather the soil. Each seedbed requires five units of Fertile Soil. Once established, one Flax flower can yield up to four Flax fibers, and because the seeds are returned upon harvest, your supply becomes infinite.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Foothills are home to dangerous wildlife, including wolves that can interrupt your soil gathering. Always clear the immediate area before committing to a digging animation.

## Constructing the Brethren Brig

The pinnacle of mid-game naval engineering is the Brethren Brig. While the "Normal Brig" is faster, the Brethren variant is the preferred choice for players focused on hauling large quantities of **windrose frigate mats**. It boasts a massive 90,000 HP compared to the 50,000 HP of the standard Ketch.

To build it, you will need:
*   **Tons of Timber:** Processed from Hardwood ordered via merchants.
*   **Massive amounts of Linen:** Produced via your Flax plantation.
*   **Tombaga Ingots:** Gathered from high-level wrecks or faction rewards.

The Brig features six cannons instead of three, effectively doubling your damage output. This allows you to take on packs of level 8 and 10 boats, further accelerating your silver gain and material acquisition.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to find windrose frigate mats early in the game?**

A: The most efficient method is to reach Tortuga and purchase merchant contracts from the Buccaneers and the local provisioners. This allows you to have materials delivered directly to your base for silver.

**Q: How do I get more Fertile Soil for my farm?**

A: Look for ruins or crop icons on your map in the Foothills region. You will find "packed dirt" on the ground that can be harvested with a pickaxe. Each mound typically yields enough for a portion of a seedbed.

**Q: Which cannons are best for a Frigate or Brig build?**

A: The Tempered 12-Pounders are highly recommended. They offer a 40% damage boost for your first shot after a short delay, which is devastating when aiming at the rear of enemy ships for critical hits.

**Q: Can I automate the gathering of windrose frigate mats?**

A: While you cannot fully automate the "gathering," the merchant system is as close as it gets. By paying silver, you can have large quantities of wood, hide, and seeds delivered to your base while you are out exploring or fighting.

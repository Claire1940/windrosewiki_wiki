## Essential Infrastructure: The Warf and Windrose Wiki Ships Management

Before you can engage in naval warfare, you must establish a functional base of operations. The most critical structure for any aspiring pirate is the **Warf**. Located under the "Crafting Utilities" section of the build menu, the Warf serves as the primary interface for managing your fleet. You cannot modify your ship's loadout while at sea; you must be docked near a Warf to access the ship management screen.

The Ship Workshop is another vital utility, allowing you to craft the components necessary for your vessel's survival. Within the management menu, you can promote or demote ships from your fleet, assign a flagship, and view your active arsenal. Understanding how to interact with these menus is the first step in optimizing **windrose wiki ships** for long-term survival.

| Utility Name | Category | Primary Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Warf** | Crafting Utility | Manage ship gear, dock vessels, and assign flagships. |
| **Ship Workshop** | Crafting Utility | Create ship-specific components like cannons and hull plating. |
| **Fast Travel Bell** | Utility | Allows for quick movement between established docks. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always build your Warf in a location with enough clearance to maneuver large ships. Overcompensating for turns is common, so give yourself plenty of "parking" space to avoid hull damage.

## Naval Combat Mechanics and Strategy

Combat in Wind Rose is a test of positioning and timing. When engaging an enemy, players have access to different ammunition types and specialized gear. The hotbar for your ship allows you to switch between damaging the enemy's sails to slow them down or targeting the hull to sink them outright.

### Shooting and Leading Targets
The cannons in 2026 remain primitive, meaning they lack the range and accuracy of modern weaponry. To hit a moving target, you must "lead" the shot by aiming slightly ahead of the enemy's current path. 
*   **Right-Click:** Hold to aim and view the firing arc.
*   **Red Indicators:** If the targeting reticle remains red, you may be out of range or blocked by waves.
*   **Cooldowns:** Each volley has a significant reload time. Watch the bars at the bottom of the screen to time your next attack.

### The Boarding Process
Once an enemy ship is sufficiently damaged, it will become "disabled," indicated by a specific icon or change in behavior. This is your cue to pull up alongside the vessel. Press the **Spacebar** to initiate a boarding action. During this phase, you will transition to melee combat. Utilizing weapons that cause "Bleed" effects is highly effective, as the damage can often spread between multiple enemies, allowing your crew to finish the fight quickly.

## Ship Maintenance and Repairs

A damaged ship is a liability. There are two primary ways to maintain your vessel: manual repairs at a dock and combat repairs on the fly. Manual repairs usually require raw wood and can be performed at your Warf. However, for intense battles, you should always carry repair kits in your ship's hotbar.

| Repair Kit Tier | Effect | Crafting Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Regular Repair Kit** | Slow health restoration; best for post-combat. | Wood, Basic Nails. |
| **Combat Repair Kit** | Moderate restoration; usable under fire. | Copper, Rum Bottle. |
| **Master Combat Repair** | Restores 80% ship health instantly. | Steel Nails, Rum Bottle. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Master Combat Repair kits are extremely rare and expensive to craft. Save these specifically for the Blackbeard boss fight or encounters with multiple level 4 transport ships.

## Exploration and Survival Tips

Beyond combat, managing your **windrose wiki ships** involves survival logistics. For example, navigating dark caves or night-time waters requires a lamp fueled by animal fat. While the lamp provides portable light, seasoned sailors often prefer placing floor-standing torches at their docks for better visibility.

When exploring, keep an eye out for "Blackbeard Waters." Sailing into these zones unprepared is a quick way to lose your vessel. If you are being pursued by multiple ships, try to split them up. Hostile AI will often head in different directions if you maneuver around islands, allowing you to engage them in one-on-one "1v1" duels rather than being overwhelmed.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I put fuel in my lamp for night sailing?**

A: You must go to a workbench and select the "Refill/Repair" option. You will need animal fat in your inventory to fill the lamp's fuel gauge.

**Q: Can I repair my ship without a repair kit?**

A: Yes, if you are docked near your Warf, you can use raw wood to repair the hull. This is the most cost-effective method for fixing minor damage sustained during exploration.

**Q: What does the "Active Arsenal Reserve" do in the ship menu?**

A: This slot is used for temporary buffs, such as rum bottles or special insignias. For example, consuming certain items can increase ship health or damage for a duration of one hour.

**Q: How do I stop my ship from overturning when docking?**

A: The ships in Wind Rose have significant momentum. To dock successfully, stop turning your rudder well before you reach your desired heading and reduce your speed to zero knots gradually. Optimizing **windrose wiki ships** handling takes practice, but mastering the "drift" is key to perfect parking.

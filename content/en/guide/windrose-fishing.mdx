While it might seem like a relaxing pastime, fishing is actually the primary gateway to obtaining the highest-tier healing foods and vitality buffs available. ## How to Unlock the Fishing Rod in Windrose

Before you can cast a line into the ocean, you must progress through the initial stages of the game. The fishing rod is not a starting item; it requires specific mid-game resources that are only found in the Foothills biome. This means you must first defeat the boss of the Coastal Jungle to properly unlock the recipes and access the necessary islands.

While some players attempt to "sequence break" by sailing into the Foothills early, the game often restricts certain crafting recipes until the previous biome's boss has been vanquished. Once you have cleared the jungle, your primary objective is to gather three specific materials: Iron, Hardwood, and Crude Rope.

### Required Crafting Materials

To craft your first fishing rod, you will need to upgrade your workbench to Level 2 (the Sawhorse). Once your station is ready, gather the following items:

| Material | Quantity | Location/Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Iron Ingot** | 2 | Smelted from Iron Ore found in the Iron Hills |
| **Hardwood** | 5 | Harvested from Divi Divi trees using a Copper Axe |
| **Crude Rope** | 3 | Crafted from basic plant fibers |

> **💡 Tip:** Look for "Iron Signs" scattered across the Foothills. These markers indicate nearby iron deposits, which are essential for both your rod and future armor upgrades.

## Finding Divi Divi Wood for Hardwood

Hardwood is a critical component that often stumps new players. In the world of Windrose, hardwood is obtained exclusively from Divi Divi trees. These trees are easily identifiable by their "swirly" or "bendy" trunks, which stand out against the straighter palm trees of the coast.

Be prepared for a fight when gathering this resource. Divi Divi trees are frequently guarded by hostile mobs, including:
*   **Goat Herds:** The easiest to manage; simply dodge their charges.
*   **Pirate Encampments:** Dangerous groups that require tactical combat.
*   **Wolf Packs:** Fast and aggressive predators.

If you are low on health, search for Divi Divi trees surrounded by goats, as they are significantly easier to avoid while you chop down the timber.

## Windrose Fishing Mechanics and Techniques

Once you have crafted your rod, the act of **windrose fishing** is relatively straightforward but requires a bit of timing. Unlike other survival games that require complex bait management, this system focuses on visual cues.

To begin, stand near a body of water—either on a beach or a protruding peninsula. Aim your rod and cast the line. You will notice two distinct types of water: **Shallow** and **Deep**. While the game UI may suggest certain fish only appear in specific depths (like the Moon Angel Fish in shallow water), current 2026 gameplay suggests that many species can be caught in both depths, though the rates may vary.

### How to Reel in Successfully
1.  **Wait for the Splash:** Watch your bobber closely. It will dip several times, but you should ignore these small tugs.
2.  **Identify the Flat Splash:** When a fish truly bites, you will see a large, "flat" splash animation. This is your cue to reel in immediately.
3.  **Rarity vs. Time:** Lower-level fish tend to bite quickly. If you find yourself waiting a long time for a splash, it often indicates a higher-rarity fish is on the line.

## Fish Rarities and Fillet Yields

After catching your fish, you must "gut" them to receive Fish Fillets. This is done by right-clicking the fish in your inventory (or pressing X on a controller). The number of fillets you receive depends entirely on the rarity of the fish.

| Fish Rarity | Color | Fillet Yield per Fish |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Uncommon** | Green | 2 – 4 Fillets |
| **Rare** | Blue | 4 – 12 Fillets |
| **Epic** | Purple | Up to 20 Fillets |

By spending just 15 minutes focusing on **windrose fishing**, you can easily accumulate hundreds of fillets, especially if you land a few Epic-tier catches.

## The Master Angler's Challenge

There are currently 10 unique types of fish to collect in the game. While individual fish don't provide unique buffs when eaten raw, completing the collection is highly rewarding. Once you have caught all 10 species, you will unlock a **Special Ring**.

This ring provides a **15% chance to catch extra fish** every time you reel in. For players who plan on doing heavy cooking for late-game bosses, this ring is an essential accessory that drastically increases your resource efficiency.

## Cooking the Best Healing Foods

The true power of fishing lies in the kitchen. To access the best recipes, you must upgrade your cooking station to Level 3. This requires the **Cutting Table**, **Cookware Shelf**, and **Ironworks** upgrades.

### Top Tier Seafood Recipes

The following table highlights the best meals you can create using your fish fillets. For more information on game updates, check the [Official Windrose Steam Page](https://store.steampowered.com/search/?term=windrose) for the latest patch notes.

| Dish | Ingredients | Primary Buff |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Seafood Platter** | Fish, Limes, Salt, Crab Legs | **+20 Vitality** |
| **Fish Chowder** | Fish, Salt, Sweet Potatoes | **+Agility** |
| **Fish and Tostones** | Fish, Bananas | **+10 Vitality** |
| **Seafood Skewers** | Fish, Crab Legs | **+5 Strength** |

> **Warning:** Do not waste your Crab Legs on Seafood Skewers. Save them exclusively for the Seafood Platter, as the +20 Vitality boost is the strongest healing buff in the game until you reach the Swamp biome.

### Finding Secondary Ingredients
*   **Limes:** Found under tall, distinctive trees on larger islands. These can be replanted and grown anywhere without fertile soil.
*   **Salt:** Commonly found in loot crates or purchased from merchant ships (look for the golden chest icon on the map).
*   **Crude Salt:** Can be gathered near coastal areas or from certain pirate drops.

## Upgrading Your Cooking Station

To craft the Seafood Platter, you need a high-level kitchen setup. The transition from Level 2 to Level 3 requires iron-tier components.

1.  **The Cutting Table:** Requires basic wood and copper.
2.  **The Ironworks:** Requires 5 Iron Ingots. You will need to craft two of these in your level 2 workbench.
3.  **The Cookware Shelf:** Once you have the Ironworks and some Hardwood, you can place the shelving to finalize your Level 3 kitchen.

By stacking the buffs from a Seafood Platter and other high-tier foods like Spicy Chicken, you can increase your total Vitality by over 30%. This massive health pool allows you to withstand the "unstoppable" attacks of the first few bosses and makes farming the Drowned enemies significantly safer.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to go windrose fishing for Epic fish?**

A: While fish can appear anywhere, standing on a peninsula that extends into deep ocean water seems to provide a higher frequency of Blue and Purple rarity catches. Always aim for the darkest blue water you can reach with your cast.

**Q: Can I fish before defeating the first boss?**

A: Technically, you can find the resources in the Foothills if you sail there early, but the recipe for the fishing rod often remains locked in your crafting menu until the Coastal Jungle boss is defeated. It is highly recommended to follow the intended biome progression.

**Q: What is the most important stat to pair with fish recipes?**

A: Vitality is king in Windrose. By eating the Seafood Platter and putting your attribute points into Vitality, you can become an "absolute beast" capable of tanking hits that would normally one-shot a glass-cannon build.

**Q: Do I need bait for windrose fishing?**

A: No, currently the game does not require specific bait items to catch any of the 10 fish species. You simply need the rod and a body of water. This may change in future Early Access updates, but for now, it is a very accessible system.

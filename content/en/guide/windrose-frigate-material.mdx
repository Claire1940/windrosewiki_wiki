Dominating the high seas in Windrose requires more than just a basic schooner; it demands the power and resilience of a Frigate. As one of the most coveted vessels in the game, the Frigate represents a significant milestone for any captain looking to survive the late-game biomes. However, obtaining this ship is not a simple purchase. Players must embark on a multi-stage journey that involves building reputation, securing blueprints, and eventually gathering the specific **windrose frigate material** required for construction at their home base.

Understanding the complexity of the **windrose frigate material** grind is essential for efficient progression. You cannot simply stumble upon this ship in a random shipyard; it requires a dedicated effort to reach the maximum trust level with specific factions and a substantial investment of resources. This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of how to reach Tortuga Market, maximize your reputation, and assemble the components necessary to launch your Frigate into the treacherous waters of 2026.

## Locating the Tortuga Market

The first step in your quest for the Frigate is finding the Tortuga Market. This specialized trading hub is located in the second biome, specifically situated to the left of the starting regions. Navigating here can be dangerous if your current ship isn't properly outfitted for mid-tier combat, so ensure your hull is repaired before making the trek.

Tortuga Market serves as the primary hub for the "Person of the Coast" faction. Within this market, you will find two specific NPCs who manage the distribution of high-tier ship designs. These NPCs are the gatekeepers to the Frigate blueprint. Unlike standard merchants, they do not accept gold alone; they demand loyalty and proof of your prowess as a sailor.

## Reaching Trust Level 4

To even view the Frigate design in the NPC's inventory, you must reach Trust Level 4 with the Person of the Coast. This is currently the maximum reputation level in the game. Reaching this rank requires a total of 3,000 Trust Points. 

The primary method for gaining these points is through naval combat. When you destroy enemy ships throughout the world, they drop specific reputation items. These items can be turned in at the Tortuga Market to increase your standing. 

> **💡 Tip:** Focus on hunting larger merchant convoys or pirate hunters in the second biome. These vessels tend to drop higher-quality reputation tokens, significantly speeding up the grind to Level 4.

### Trust Level Progression Table

| Trust Level | Points Required | Unlocks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 1 | 0 - 500 | Basic supplies and consumables. |
| Level 2 | 501 - 1,500 | Mid-tier ship components and cannons. |
| Level 3 | 1,501 - 2,500 | Advanced navigation tools and rare materials. |
| **Level 4** | **3,000** | **Frigate Ship Design (Blueprint)** |

Once you hit the 3,000-point threshold, you can purchase the "Ship Design: Frigate." This item is a blueprint, meaning it does not give you the ship immediately. Instead, it unlocks the ability to craft the vessel at your base's Wharf.

## Gathering Windrose Frigate Material

After learning the blueprint, you must return to your base and access the Wharf. This is where the real work begins. The Frigate is a massive vessel, and the **windrose frigate material** requirements are extensive. You will need a combination of raw resources, refined metals, and specialized components that are often found in high-level zones or through complex crafting chains.

While the exact numbers can vary based on your current Wharf upgrades, the following table represents the standard material investment for a Frigate in the 2026 build of the game.

## The Crafting Process at the Wharf

With the **windrose frigate material** in your inventory and the blueprint learned, interact with the Wharf master at your base. Follow these steps to complete the construction:

1.  **Select the Ship Design:** Navigate to the "Build" menu and select the Frigate icon.
2.  **Deposit Materials:** You do not need to have all materials at once; most Wharfs allow you to contribute resources over time.
3.  **Wait for Construction:** The Frigate is not built instantly. Depending on your base's worker count, construction can take several real-time hours.
4.  **Commission the Vessel:** Once the timer expires, you must pay a final commissioning fee in gold to launch the ship.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Ensure your base has enough storage capacity before you start gathering materials. The volume of wood and iron required for a Frigate can easily exceed basic chest limits.

## Why the Frigate is Worth the Investment

You might wonder if the grueling search for **windrose frigate material** is worth the effort compared to sticking with a faster, smaller Brigantine. The Frigate offers unparalleled advantages in the late-game environment of Windrose.

*   **Firepower:** The Frigate features the highest number of broadside cannon slots in its class, allowing you to sink enemy galleons in just a few well-placed volleys.
*   **Durability:** With a massive hull integrity pool, the Frigate can withstand significant damage, making it ideal for solo players tackling difficult boss encounters.
*   **Cargo Capacity:** If you are a merchant-focused player, the Frigate's hold allows for massive trade runs, maximizing your profit per trip.

## Advanced Tips for Material Farming

To optimize your collection of **windrose frigate material**, consider the following strategies:

*   **Fleet Farming:** Join a guild or group. When fleeted, reputation gains from ship kills are shared, allowing you to reach Trust Level 4 much faster.
*   **Salvage Operations:** Equip your current ship with a "Salvage Crane" module. This increases the yield of refined metals and wood from shipwrecks, which are vital for Frigate construction.
*   **Market Flipping:** Sometimes, it is faster to farm gold through trade and buy the raw materials from the Tortuga Market rather than harvesting them yourself.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Tortuga Market to get the Frigate?**

A: The Tortuga Market is located in the second biome, situated on the left side of the world map. Look for the "Person of the Coast" faction icons to identify the correct NPCs.

**Q: Can I skip the Trust Level requirement if I have enough gold?**

A: No. The Frigate blueprint is locked behind Trust Level 4 (3,000 points). You must prove your loyalty to the faction by destroying enemy ships and turning in reputation items.

**Q: What is the most difficult windrose frigate material to find?**

A: Most players find the Ship Heart or the large quantities of Reinforced Iron Plates to be the biggest bottleneck. Iron Plates require significant mining and smelting time, while the Ship Heart often requires defeating high-level naval bosses.

**Q: How do I increase my Trust Level quickly?**

A: The fastest way is to farm enemy ships in the second and third biomes. Each ship destroyed will drop items that can be traded to the NPCs at Tortuga Market for reputation points. Reach 3,000 points to hit the maximum level.

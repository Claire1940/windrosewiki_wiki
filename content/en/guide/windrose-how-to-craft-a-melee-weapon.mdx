## Gathering Raw Materials and Crude Tools

Before you can even think about forging a rapier or a saber, you must start with the basics. The world of Windrose is filled with raw materials that serve as the building blocks for your future armory. Your first task is to scour the landscape for rocks, branches, and plant fibers. These items are scattered across the starting zones and can be picked up without any specialized equipment.

Once you have gathered enough basic materials, your first goal is to craft a standard Workbench. The Workbench is the heart of your early-game operations. From here, you will create your first "crude" tools. Specifically, you need to prioritize the Pickaxe. Whether it is a simple Stone Pickaxe or a slightly better version, this tool is mandatory for the next phase of your progression.

| Material | Source | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Branches** | Ground/Trees | Fuel and basic handles |
| **Rocks** | Ground/Large Boulders | Stone tools and furnace construction |
| **Plant Fiber** | Bushes | Cordage and basic bindings |
| **Copper Ore** | Underground Caves | The primary ingredient for early melee weapons |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't waste too much time with the Broken Blade. As soon as you have a Workbench, focus entirely on gathering the materials for a Pickaxe so you can begin mining metal.

## The Path to Copper: Mining and Smelting

With your pickaxe in hand, you are ready to move beyond the stone age. To understand **windrose how to craft a melee weapon** that actually deals significant damage, you must find Copper Ore. Copper is typically found inside caves. These locations can be dangerous, so ensure you have a torch and some basic food before venturing deep underground.

Mining the copper veins will yield Copper Ore, but this raw material cannot be used for weapon crafting in its natural state. You must refine it into Copper Ingots. This requires two specific structures: the Charcoal Kiln and the Smelting Furnace. The Kiln produces the fuel (charcoal) needed to power the Furnace, which in turn melts the ore into usable bars.

| Structure | Requirements | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Charcoal Kiln** | Rocks, Wood | Converts wood into Charcoal fuel |
| **Smelting Furnace** | Rocks, Clay/Fiber | Smelts Copper Ore into **Copper Ingots** |

Once you have a steady supply of Copper Ingots, you have cleared the most significant hurdle in the crafting process. You will need at least five ingots to build the specialized station required for weaponry.

## Building the Weaponsmith Workshop

Now that you have your refined metal, you can move on to the actual assembly of your armory. In Windrose, melee weapons aren't crafted at a standard workbench. Instead, you must construct a **Weaponsmith Workshop**. This station is specifically designed for the forging and balancing of personal armaments.

To build the workshop, enter Building Mode by pressing **B**, then right-click to open the construction menu. Navigate to the "Crafting and Utilities" section and look for the "Personal Weapons" subcategory. To successfully build the Weaponsmith Workshop, you will need:
*   **5 Copper Ingots**
*   **10 Wood Units**

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Weaponsmith Workshop is a sensitive piece of equipment. Unlike some extractors or outdoor structures, it must be placed under a roof. If you try to place it in an open field, the game will prevent the placement. Ensure you have a basic shelter or house built first.

## Windrose How to Craft a Melee Weapon: The Final Steps

Once your workshop is placed safely under a roof, interact with it to see your available blueprints. Initially, you will have access to a few basic designs. As you progress through the game and find new recipes or materials, this list will expand significantly.

When considering **windrose how to craft a melee weapon**, you should choose the tool that fits your combat style. The game offers a variety of options right from the start of the copper tier.

### Early Game Weapon Options
1.  **The Club:** A high-impact weapon great for stunning enemies. It is relatively cheap to produce but lacks the reach of bladed weapons.
2.  **The Rapier:** A fast, thrusting weapon. It allows for quick strikes and is excellent for players who prefer a "hit and run" tactic.
3.  **The Saber:** A balanced slashing weapon. It provides a good mix of speed and damage, making it the most versatile choice for general exploration.

To craft any of these, simply select the weapon in the Weaponsmith Workshop menu, ensure you have the required Copper Ingots and Wood in your inventory, and click the craft button. The item will appear in your inventory, ready to be equipped.

## Upgrading and Maintaining Your Arsenal

Crafting the weapon is only the beginning. Windrose features a robust upgrade system that allows you to improve the stats of your existing gear. Inside the Weaponsmith Workshop, you will notice an "Upgrade" tab. By spending additional resources—often more ingots or rarer materials found in later biomes—you can increase the damage output and durability of your melee weapons.

| Weapon Tier | Primary Material | Combat Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crude** | Stone/Wood | Emergency survival only |
| **Copper** | Copper Ingots | Standard early-game combat |
| **Iron** | Iron Ingots | Mid-game exploration and boss fights |

Keeping your weapons in top shape is vital. Every swing reduces the durability of your blade. While the process of **windrose how to craft a melee weapon** is repeatable, it is often more cost-effective to maintain and upgrade a high-quality weapon than to constantly craft low-tier replacements. For more advanced strategies on resource management, you can check out the [Windrose Steam Community Hub](https://steamcommunity.com/app/windrose) for player-made guides and tips.

## Combat Strategy with Your New Melee Weapon

Having a shiny new copper saber is great, but knowing how to use it is even better. In Windrose, combat is about timing and stamina management. 

*   **Dodos:** While they seem harmless, they can swarm you. Use the wide arc of a Saber to hit multiple targets at once.
*   **Drowners:** These enemies are faster and more aggressive. The Rapier's quick thrusts can interrupt their attacks, giving you a window to dodge.
*   **Resource Efficiency:** Use your melee weapon for combat only. Do not use your sword to chop bushes or break rocks, as this will deplete the durability much faster than hitting enemies.

By following these steps, you will master the art of the forge. The transition from a struggling survivor with a broken blade to a formidable warrior with a custom-crafted saber is one of the most rewarding experiences in the game.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I place my Weaponsmith Workshop on the ground?**

A: In Windrose, certain advanced crafting stations like the Weaponsmith Workshop must be placed under a roof. Build a simple foundation, four walls, and a ceiling first, then you will be able to place the workshop inside.

**Q: Where is the best place to find Copper Ore for crafting?**

A: Copper Ore is most commonly found in caves. Look for rock formations with brownish-orange streaks. You will need a Stone Pickaxe or better to mine these nodes effectively.

**Q: Can I craft ranged weapons at the Weaponsmith Workshop?**

A: Yes! While this guide focuses on **windrose how to craft a melee weapon**, the Weaponsmith Workshop also allows you to craft early-game firearms like the Pistol, provided you have the necessary ingots and gunpowder components.

**Q: Do I need to find blueprints to craft better weapons?**

A: Some basic weapons like the Club and Saber are unlocked by default once you build the workshop. However, more advanced melee weapons will require you to find blueprints in the world or unlock them through the game's progression system.

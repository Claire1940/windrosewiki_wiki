## Unlocking the Frigate Design at Tortuga Market

Before you can even think about swinging a hammer at the wharf, you must secure the blueprints for the Frigate. In the world of Windrose, these designs are not simply found in random loot chests; they must be earned through merit and trade. Your journey begins in the second biome, specifically on the left side of the world map. Here, you will find the bustling Tortuga Market, a hub for the "Person of the Coast" faction.

Navigating to Tortuga Market can be treacherous if your current ship isn't up to par, so ensure you have enough supplies for the voyage. Once you arrive at the market, look for the two specific NPCs representing the coastal authorities. These individuals hold the keys to the Frigate design. However, they won't sell to just anyone. You must prove your worth to their faction before the "Ship Design: Frigate" becomes available in their inventory.

> **Warning:** Do not attempt to reach the second biome without at least a Tier 2 hull. The environmental hazards and hostile pirate patrols can easily sink an unprepared starter ship.

## Understanding Trust Levels and Point Farming

Unlocking the ability to purchase the Frigate design requires two main components: reaching Trust Level 4 and accumulating 3,000 points. Trust Level 4 is currently the maximum reputation rank with the coastal NPCs, representing a significant investment of time and effort.

To increase your trust and earn the necessary points, you must engage in naval combat. By hunting down and destroying other ships throughout the various biomes, you will collect specific items that can be turned in to the NPCs at Tortuga Market. This loop of combat and turn-ins is the primary way to climb the ranks.

| Trust Level | Points Required | Rewards Unlocked |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | 0 | Basic Supplies, Repair Kits |
| **Level 2** | 1,000 | Advanced Cannons, Tier 2 Blueprints |
| **Level 3** | 2,000 | Specialized Hull Plating |
| **Level 4** | **3,000** | **Frigate Design**, Elite Crew Gear |

Once you reach the 3,000-point threshold and hit Level 4, you can "learn" the Frigate design. This adds the ship to your permanent crafting list at your home base's wharf.

## The Construction Process at the Wharf

With your inventory full of **frigate materials windrose** components, head to the Wharf at your base. The construction of a Frigate is not instantaneous. It is a multi-stage process that involves assembling the frame, laying the hull, and finally installing the mast and weaponry.

1.  **Access the Wharf Menu:** Interact with the construction terminal at your base.
2.  **Select the Frigate:** Locate the learned blueprint in your "Available Ships" tab.
3.  **Deposit Materials:** You do not need to have all materials at once; you can deposit them in batches as you collect them.
4.  **Finalize Build:** Once all requirements are met, initiate the build. 

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep a surplus of Iron Ingots. The Frigate has more cannon slots than any previous ship class, and you will need extra metal to forge the actual armaments once the ship is built.

Once the timer completes, your Frigate will be ready for its maiden voyage. Note that you cannot "call" the ship from the open sea if you haven't built it yet; the Wharf is the only place where the physical transition from blueprint to vessel occurs.

*   **Firepower:** Capable of housing up to 12 cannons per side.
*   **Durability:** High resistance to splintering damage from chain shot.
*   **Storage:** Massive cargo hold for long-distance trading or extended raiding sessions.

For players looking to participate in high-end [official game events and naval raids](https://store.steampowered.com/search/?term=Windrose), the Frigate is often the minimum requirement for entry. Its ability to tank hits while delivering devastating counter-attacks makes it the gold standard for solo and group play alike.

## Optimizing Your Frigate Materials Windrose Farm

To speed up the process of collecting **frigate materials windrose**, consider upgrading your gathering tools first. A Tier 3 Pickaxe and Axe will significantly reduce the time spent at resource nodes. Additionally, using a smaller, faster ship for the initial resource run can save time on travel, provided you have a large enough warehouse at your base to store the materials before the final construction.

If you find yourself struggling to reach Trust Level 4, focus on hunting merchant convoys. While they are better defended, they drop higher-value reputation items that can be turned in at Tortuga Market for a much larger point boost than standard pirate scouts. Balancing your time between material gathering and reputation grinding is the hallmark of a successful Windrose captain.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to find frigate materials windrose?**

A: The best locations are the Forest and Mountain biomes for raw resources, while the design itself and specialized fabrics must be obtained from the Tortuga Market in the second biome.

**Q: Can I buy the Frigate with gold instead of points?**

A: No, the Frigate is a reputation-gated ship. While you may need gold for some refined components, the blueprint requires Trust Level 4 and 3,000 points earned through faction-specific tasks.

**Q: What happens if my Frigate is destroyed?**

A: In Windrose, ship destruction usually results in a respawn at your last visited port with a repair cost. However, you do not need to re-gather all the **frigate materials windrose** unless you are playing on a hardcore server or the ship is permanently lost due to specific game mechanics.

**Q: Is the Frigate better than the Brigantine?**

A: Generally, yes. The Frigate has superior armor and more cannon ports, though the Brigantine is slightly more maneuverable in shallow waters. For most 2026 endgame content, the Frigate is the preferred choice.

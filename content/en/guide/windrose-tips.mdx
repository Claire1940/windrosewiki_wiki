## Essential Windrose Tips for Combat Mastery

Combat in Windrose is more than just clicking until an enemy falls. It borrows heavily from "Souls-like" mechanics, emphasizing timing, stamina management, and shield-breaking. One of the first things any player should do is optimize their control scheme. The default "Target Lock" is bound to the T key, which many find cumbersome during intense skirmishes.

### Control Optimization

| Action | Default Key | Recommended Key | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Target Lock** | T | Middle Mouse | Faster access; standard in modern action games. |
| **Heavy Attack** | Middle Mouse | F1 or Mouse 4 | Prevents conflict with the new Target Lock bind. |
| **Dodge** | Left Control | Mouse 5 | Allows for simultaneous movement and evasion. |
| **Destroy Mode** | Middle Mouse | F2 | Only used during building; frees up mouse buttons. |

### The Art of the Parry and Shield Breaking

Every enemy in Windrose features a shield icon beneath their health bar. To stun an opponent, you must deplete these shields. While direct attacks can eventually break them, **parrying** is the most efficient method. By blocking just before an attack lands, you can instantly remove shield layers. 

> **💡 Tip:** Be wary of enemies that emit a red glow or sparkle before attacking. These are "Unblockable Attacks" that cannot be parried; you must use your dodge (Left Control) to avoid damage entirely.

### Stamina and Health Regain

A common mistake among beginners is "turtling"—holding the block button indefinitely. While blocking prevents damage, it completely halts your stamina regeneration. To maintain your offensive, you must drop your guard between enemy swings to let your stamina bar refill.

Furthermore, Windrose features a "rally" mechanic. When you take damage, a portion of your health bar remains faded for a few seconds. If you deal damage to an enemy during this window, you will recover that lost health. This encourages a calculated, aggressive playstyle rather than constant retreat.

## Survival and Nutritional Strategy

Your survival is directly tied to what you eat. Unlike games where food simply prevents starvation, Windrose uses a system similar to Valheim, where food items dictate your maximum health and stamina pools.

### Immediate Resource Gathering

As soon as you spawn, head to the nearest palm trees. Look for coconuts hanging at the top. You can harvest these by pressing E, and they provide an immediate boost to your maximum health that lasts for 7 minutes. This is one of the most vital **windrose tips** for surviving early-game encounters with boars and coastal dodos.

### Food Slot Management

You have three active slots for consumables: two for food and one for elixirs (potions). To maximize your efficiency, you should always have two different types of food active.

| Food Item | Primary Benefit | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Coconuts** | Increased Max Health | 7 Minutes |
| **Boiled Crab** | Large Max Health Boost | 15 Minutes |
| **Coconut Milk & Bananas** | Max Health + 5 Endurance | 15 Minutes |
| **Elixir of Precision** | +13% Ranged Damage | Variable |

## Base Building and the Rested Buff

Your base is your sanctuary, but it also serves as a powerful buff station. The "Rested" buff is critical for long-term exploration, as it increases your stamina pool and significantly boosts stamina regeneration speed.

### Increasing Comfort Levels

The duration and potency of your Rested buff are determined by your base's **Comfort Level**. You can raise this level by placing unique decorations. Note that building multiple items of the same category (e.g., three tables) will not stack the bonus. You must place one item from each distinct category to maximize the effect.

1. **Tables** (e.g., Wooden Table)
2. **Storage/Wardrobes** (e.g., Wardrobe)
3. **Seating** (e.g., Benches)
4. **Beds/Canopies** (e.g., Canopy)
5. **Lighting** (e.g., Wall Torches)

### Efficient Building Mechanics

Windrose is very forgiving with its building system. When you dismantle a structure, you receive **100% of the resources back**. This allows you to experiment with layouts without wasting precious materials. Additionally, when crafting at stations like the Weapon Smith Workshop, the game automatically pulls materials from any chests located within your bonfire's radius. You do not need to have the items in your personal inventory.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Certain advanced crafting stations, such as the Weapon Smith Workshop, require a roof to function. Ensure your base layout accounts for structural overheads early on.

## Exploration and Naval Navigation

With over 30 islands to explore, movement is a core pillar of the gameplay loop. Navigating the high seas requires a mix of preparation and knowledge of the game's "quality of life" features.

### Shipwreck Scavenging

The coastlines of the starting islands are often littered with shipwrecks and debris. Do not ignore these. Destroying these objects provides free wood, plant fiber, and—most importantly—**Nails**. While you can craft nails eventually, scavenging them early allows you to build advanced storage chests much sooner.

### Fast Travel and Ship Summoning

Fast travel in Windrose is handled via "Bells." You can craft these at a workbench using copper ingots and rope. You are limited to nine active bells across the world. A pro strategy is to always keep one bell in your inventory. If you find yourself deep in a dangerous island with a full inventory, simply drop the bell, teleport to base to offload, and teleport back.

For naval travel, the game provides a summoning mechanic. Once you have unlocked your first boat from the Doctor NPC (who appears after you mine your first copper), you can summon it to your location at any time by pressing **K**. There is currently no cooldown on this feature, making it incredibly easy to relocate your vessel if you get separated from it on a large island.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the Doctor NPC for my base?**

A: The Doctor is introduced shortly after you mine your first Copper nodes. If he doesn't appear at your camp automatically, go to your Bonfire and select the "Settle" option to manually invite him to your base.

**Q: Can I get my resources back if I delete a wall or floor?**

A: Yes! One of the best **windrose tips** for builders is that you get a 100% resource refund when destroying any placed structure in destroy mode.

**Q: What do the letter grades (A, B, C) on weapons mean?**

A: These represent attribute scaling. A weapon with an "A" grade in Agility (represented by a rabbit icon) will gain significantly more damage as you increase your Agility stat compared to a weapon with a "C" grade.

**Q: My ship sank! Is it gone forever?**

A: No. If your ship is destroyed, you can visit a Wharf at your base to restore it. The cost is relatively low, and you will not lose the ship permanently.

Transitioning from a light Ketch to a heavy-hitting Brig is one of the most significant milestones for any aspiring pirate captain. To make this leap, you must secure a vast amount of **windrose brig material**, specifically focusing on high-tier timber and linen. While the Ketch serves its purpose in the early game with roughly 50,000 HP, the Brethren Brig elevates your survivability to a staggering 90,000 HP and doubles your firepower with six cannons.

Acquiring the necessary **windrose brig material** requires a combination of regional progression, reputation grinding, and smart resource management. You won't be able to simply stumble upon these resources in the starting zones; instead, you must push into the Foothills and establish a robust supply chain through base merchants and farming plots. This guide covers everything from silver farming to seed cultivation to ensure your shipyard stays busy throughout 2026.

## Unlocking the Brig: Prerequisites and Reputation

Before you can even worry about the specific **windrose brig material** requirements, you must unlock the blueprint itself. The Brig is not a standard purchase; it requires standing with the various factions found in Tortuga and Port Royal.

The most sought-after version is the **Brethren Brig**, which is sold by the Brethren of the Coast in Tortuga. You must reach Reputation Tier 2 with them to access the ship. However, do not rush to buy the blueprint immediately. It is widely recommended to wait until you have cleared the first boss and reached the Foothills region. Attempting to pilot a Brig without the proper upgrades or silver reserves to maintain it can lead to a quick trip to the locker.

| Ship Type | Health (HP) | Cannon Slots | Cargo Size | Speed |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ketch** | 50,000 | 3 | Small | Fast |
| **Standard Brig** | 70,000 | 6 | Medium | Average |
| **Brethren Brig** | **90,000** | **6** | **Large** | **Slow** |

> **💡 Tip:** The Brethren Brig is slower than the standard version, but the massive 90,000 HP pool and increased cargo space make it the superior choice for solo players and long-distance traders.

## Essential Windrose Brig Material List

To construct the Brig, you will need two primary refined resources: Timber (processed from Hardwood) and Linen (processed from Flax). These are the backbone of the **windrose brig material** economy.

### 1. Hardwood and Timber
Hardwood is the primary structural component. While you can manually chop trees in higher-level zones, the sheer volume required makes manual farming inefficient. Instead, use the Merchant Contracts system. By purchasing a Natural Resource Contract in Tortuga, you can hire a merchant for your base who delivers boxes of 50 Hardwood for a silver fee.

### 2. Flax and Linen
Linen is required for the sails and internal rigging. This material is notoriously difficult to find early on because Flax only grows in the Foothills. Once you find your first batch of Flax, you will receive seeds. These seeds are the key to infinite resources.

## The Merchant Contract System

The most efficient way to gather **windrose brig material** in 2026 is to stop farming and start ordering. In Tortuga, you can visit the Provisioners of various factions to buy Merchant Contracts. Once these are placed in your base, you can order materials directly to your doorstep.

*   **People of Tortuga:** Sell Food and Natural Resource contracts (Hardwood).
*   **Buccaneers:** Sell Animal Product contracts (Rough Hide/Leather).
*   **Smugglers:** Provide access to advanced naval tactics and hull reinforcements.

Ordering a box of resources usually takes about 70 minutes of real-time play. A single box of Hardwood contains 50 units, which is significantly faster than roaming the islands with an axe.

## Farming Flax for Infinite Linen

Since Linen is a vital **windrose brig material**, you should establish a farm as soon as you reach the Foothills. Look for ruins or areas marked with the soil symbol on your map. You will need to use a pickaxe on "Packed Dirt" to collect **Fertile Soil**.

1.  **Craft Seedbeds:** Each seedbed requires five Fertile Soil.
2.  **Plant Flax Seeds:** You can find these in the Foothills or buy them in boxes from the Food Merchant.
3.  **Harvest and Replant:** Every Flax plant harvested yields Flax Fiber and additional seeds. This creates a self-sustaining loop where you never have to hunt for wild Flax again.

> **Warning:** Wolves frequently guard the ruins where Fertile Soil is found. Ensure your ground combat gear is updated before attempting to farm soil in the Foothills.

## Funding Your Ship: Silver Farming Strategies

Building a Brig and ordering materials is expensive. A single order can cost up to 500 Silver. To maintain your supply of **windrose brig material**, you need a steady income.

The most effective method is "Boarding for Profit." Look for enemy ships with a **Chest Icon** floating above them. These ships carry bonus rewards. Instead of simply sinking them, damage them until they are "Broken," then initiate a boarding action. Killing the crew and successfully boarding provides a massive silver bonus compared to standard sinking.

| Ship Level | Silver Reward (Sinking) | Silver Reward (Boarding) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Level 2-3 | 15 - 25 | ~50 |
| Level 5-8 | 60 - 100 | 150 - 200 |
| Level 10+ | 120 - 180 | 300+ |

To succeed in boarding, your **Boarding Gear** at the Shipwright’s Workshop must be upgraded. While your cannons and hull should always be at the maximum level for your region, your boarding gear can safely lag about five levels behind and still be effective.

## Essential Upgrades for the Brig

Once you have gathered the **windrose brig material** and constructed your ship, you must equip it properly. The Brig’s strength lies in its broadside potential and its ability to soak up damage.

### The Best Cannons
Head to the Buccaneers in Tortuga once you reach Reputation Tier 2. You have two primary choices for 12-pounders:
*   **Perfectly Ordered 12-Pounders:** Best for sustained DPS due to faster reload speeds.
*   **Tempered 12-Pounders:** Best for burst damage. These deal 40% more damage if you haven't fired in 6 seconds. This is devastating when targeting the rear of an enemy ship for critical hits.

### Hull and Tactics
Visit the Smugglers at Port Royal (Reputation Tier 2) for the **Keel Hold Hull Bracing**. This specific upgrade allows your repair kits to continue functioning even while you are taking damage. When ascended with Tombaga Ingots, it increases the duration of repair kits by 30%, making you nearly unkillable in one-on-one engagements.

## Summary of Material Acquisition

To ensure you have a steady flow of **windrose brig material**, follow this checklist:

*   **Silver:** Board "Chest" ships in the Smuggler's Archipelago.
*   **Hardwood:** Order boxes from the Natural Resource Merchant in your base.
*   **Linen:** Farm Flax seeds in the Foothills and plant them in Seedbeds.
*   **Leather:** Order Rough Hide boxes from the Animal Product Merchant.
*   **Upgrades:** Use the Shipwright's Workshop in Tortuga to ascend your Hull using Tombaga Ingots.

## FAQ

**Q: Where do I find the contract for the Hardwood merchant?**

A: You can purchase the Natural Resource Contract from the Provisioner in Tortuga. Once purchased, you can place the merchant in your base via the build menu.

**Q: Is the Brethren Brig better than the normal Brig?**

A: Yes, in most cases. While it is slightly slower, the Brethren Brig has 90,000 HP compared to the standard Brig's 70,000 HP. It also features a larger cargo hold, which is essential for transporting the large amounts of **windrose brig material** needed for later upgrades.

**Q: How do I get more Flax seeds?**

A: You can obtain Flax seeds by harvesting wild Flax in the Foothills or by purchasing Food Boxes from your base's Food Merchant. Each box typically contains five seeds, which can be expanded into a massive farm.

**Q: Can I build the Brig before reaching the Foothills?**

A: It is technically possible if you trade with other players, but the game design intends for you to reach the Foothills first. The enemies in the second region are balanced for the Brig's firepower, and the materials like Flax are native to that area.

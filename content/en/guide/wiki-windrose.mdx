## Getting Started: Your First Day in Windrose

When you first wash up on the shore, your immediate priorities are shelter, sustenance, and security. Unlike many survival games where you can build anywhere, Windrose utilizes a "claim" system. To begin constructing a base, you must first build a Bonfire. This acts as the heart of your territory, allowing you to place structures within its radius.

Follow these steps to establish your first camp:

1.  **Gather Basic Materials:** Scour the beach for loose wood and stones. You won't have tools yet, so look for debris on the ground.
2.  **Place the Bonfire:** Open your construction menu and place the bonfire in a flat, strategic area—preferably near shipwrecks or resource nodes.
3.  **Construct a Workbench:** This is your primary hub for crafting tools like the Stone Axe and Stone Pickaxe.
4.  **Set a Spawn Point:** Build a Tent or a Bed and interact with it to ensure that if you fall in combat, you return to your base rather than a random shoreline.

### Initial Crafting Essentials

| Item | Resources Required | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonfire** | Wood, Stone | Claims territory and allows building. |

| **Stone Pickaxe** | Wood, Stone | Essential for mining stone and copper nodes. |
| **Torn Sailcloth Bag** | Rope, Fabric | Increases inventory capacity by four slots. |

> **💡 Tip:** When dismantling structures, use the build menu (B) and the middle mouse button. In the current 2026 version of the game, you receive 100% of your resources back, making base renovation risk-free.

## Resource Gathering and Management

To progress through the **wiki windrose** technology tree, you must venture beyond the safety of the beach. Wood and stone are plentiful, but specialized resources require more effort to locate.

### Wood Procurement
While you can chop down standard trees, it is often more efficient to scavenge shipwrecks found along the coast. Shipwrecks provide not only wood but also **Nails**, which are a critical component for early-game storage chests. For massive wood hauls, keep an eye out for "Fus Trees." These unique trees provide significantly higher yields than standard foliage and are the preferred target for players looking to build large-scale fortifications.

### Mining and Smelting
Mining is essential for moving from the Stone Age into the Copper Age. Copper nodes are typically found inside caves or near large rock formations.

*   **Copper Nodes:** Yield standard copper ore.
*   **Poor Copper Nodes:** Yield less ore but are easier to find in starting areas.
*   **Smelting Process:** To turn ore into ingots, you will need a **Charcoal Kiln** to produce coal and a **Smelting Furnace**. In the 2026 Early Access build, the ratio for smelting has been optimized, often requiring four pieces of ore per ingot.

## Combat and Survival Mechanics

Combat in Windrose is tactical and relies heavily on stamina management. Whether you are fighting giant crabs, wild boars, or the "Drowned" undead pirates that emerge at night, you must balance your aggression with defense.

### The Combat Loop
Most melee weapons follow a simple but effective pattern: strike three times, then back away or dodge to allow your stamina bar to refill. Using the **Lock-On (T key)** is highly recommended for maintaining focus on a single target, especially during the chaotic night cycles.

### Enemy Types and Loot

| Enemy | Behavior | Key Drops |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crabs** | Neutral until attacked. | Crab Meat, Shells (for decoration). |
| **Boars** | Highly aggressive; will charge. | Boar Hide, Meat. |
| **Drowned Pirates** | Spawn at night; high health. | Undead Essence, Silver Buttons, Pearls. |
| **Dodos** | Passive and flightless. | Eggs, Feathers, Dodo Meat. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not ignore the "Rested" bonus. Similar to other survival titles, placing decorations like vases, rugs, and furniture in your base provides a comfort bonus that increases the duration of your rested state, significantly boosting your stamina regeneration.

## Nutrition and Stat Buffs

In the **wiki windrose** ecosystem, hunger won't kill you, but it will make you incredibly weak. Eating food provides "Vitality" and maximum health boosts. You can have multiple food buffs active at once, so it is best to mix meat-based dishes with vegetable-based soups.

### Early Game Recipes

| Dish | Primary Ingredients | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Boiled Crab** | Crab Meat | Increased Max Health (15 mins). |
| **Bacon and Eggs** | Boar Meat, Dodo Eggs | +5 Vitality (Excellent for combat). |
| **Dodo Broth** | Dodo Meat, Water | Balanced Health and Stamina boost. |
| **Coconut Soup** | Coconuts | Quick Stamina recovery. |

Follow the rule of thumb: If you have high-tier food buffs active, use that time for combat or mining. If you are just performing "busy work" around your base, stick to basic snacks to avoid wasting precious combat stats.

## Advanced Progression: The Ship and Dr. Gallon

One of the most exciting milestones in Windrose is obtaining your first vessel. This isn't just for flavor; the ship is your ticket to other islands and more advanced resources.

To unlock your ship, you must progress through the initial questline until you encounter **Dr. Gallon**. He is a ship surgeon who can be found after you establish a stable bonfire and base. Speaking with him will unlock the "Recall Ship" ability (default key: **K**). This allows you to summon your boat to your current location, provided there is enough water. Dr. Gallon also serves as a recurring NPC who can provide you with healing potions, so check in with him daily.

## FAQ

**Q: Do resources respawn in Windrose?**

A: Yes, resources like trees, bushes, and even ore nodes in caves 100% respawn over time. If you find a particularly rich area for peppers or potatoes, mark it on your map as you can return to harvest it again later.

**Q: How do I increase my inventory space?**

A: Early on, you should craft the Torn Sailcloth Bag at a workbench using rope and fabric. Later, you can upgrade to professional sailor backpacks as you unlock the Armor and Clothing Workshop.

**Q: What is the best way to handle the undead at night?**

A: If you aren't geared for a fight, stay near your bonfire or inside your hut. If you must fight, use a sword for its AoE (Area of Effect) swings and keep a bandage on your hotbar. Remember that bandages provide healing over time, but the effect stops if you take damage.

**Q: Can I take my character to other worlds?**

A: Yes, according to the current **wiki windrose** mechanics, characters are persistent. You can take your character and their inventory into a newly created world to farm resources like Fus Trees or Copper if your primary world has been picked clean.

## Mastering the Frigate Requirements Windrose for Endgame

The primary reason the Blackbeard Frigate is favored over other naval options is its unique capacity for heavy ordinance. ## Cannon Configurations and Firepower

When evaluating the **frigate requirements windrose** for weaponry, you will encounter three primary types of cannons. While some offer reload speed or debuffs, the most effective choice for high-level play focuses on weak spot multipliers and initial volley damage.

| Cannon Type | Primary Effect | Secondary Effect (Ascended) | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Precision Striker** | +40% damage after waiting 6s to fire | **+20% Weak Spot/Headshot damage** | Endgame "One-Shot" builds |
| **Rapid Reloader** | +30% reload speed for 22s after hit | +30% Aiming speed | Sustained combat/Bosses |
| **Debuff Volley** | Applies "Wrecked" (-10% damage debuff) | +15% Reload speed | Group play/Support |

The "Precision Striker" is widely considered the gold standard. In Windrose, a "headshot" is achieved by firing at the stern (the back) of the enemy vessel. When you see yellow damage numbers, you have successfully hit a weak spot. Combined with the 36-size LBS cannons, this setup allows you to bypass complex mechanics by simply deleting the enemy before they can react.

> **💡 Tip:** Every individual shot in a volley has its own critical hit chance. This means the weak spot bonus from the Precision Striker is almost always active to some degree, even if your aim isn't perfect.

## Defensive Items: The Keel Hold

Despite your massive offensive capabilities, fulfilling the **frigate requirements windrose** gear list necessitates a solid defense. The "Keel Hold" slot is your primary defensive tool. In the current 2026 environment, damage reduction is often less valuable than "uninterruptible" utility because most enemies will die before they can significantly lower your health.

| Defense Item | Main Benefit | Secondary Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reinforced Hull** | Taking damage **does not interrupt repair kits** | +30% Repair kit duration |
| **Stand Fast** | 5 stacks of 25% damage reduction | +3 additional stacks |
| **Iron Resolve** | Up to 25% damage resistance at low HP | +30% Repair kit effectiveness |

The **Reinforced Hull** is the recommended choice. Because repair kits are plentiful (often dropped by level 10-15 ships), having a heal that cannot be cancelled by incoming fire is more reliable than passive damage reduction. By the time you reach level 15 encounters, you will likely have more repair kits than inventory space, making the efficiency of the heal secondary to its reliability.

## Naval Tactics and Skill Books

Naval Tactics, often referred to as "Books," provide passive bonuses that further refine your ship's performance. While these are the final step in meeting all **frigate requirements windrose** standards, they are often the most flexible part of the build.

1. **Tactics #1 (Ambush):** Deals 130% increased damage if you haven't taken damage for 2 minutes. This is powerful for the very first shot of an encounter but is easily wasted if a stray shot hits you.
2. **Tactics #2 (Sustenance):** Restores 30% HP after destroying a ship within 50 meters. Generally unnecessary due to the high volume of repair kits available.
3. **Tactics #3 (Grog Master):** Increases repair kit duration by 30% and Grog effectiveness by 200%. A solid choice for players who use consumables frequently.
4. **Tactics #4 (Passive Regen):** Restores 3% health every 3 seconds while out of combat. Useful for "brainless" farming where you don't want to manually use kits.
5. **Tactics #5 (Broken Rhythm):** Hitting an enemy reduces their reload speed and damage by 20%. This is the preferred endgame choice, as it provides a safety net if an enemy survives your initial volley.

## Boarding Mechanics and Crew Upgrades

Boarding is an essential part of the *Windrose* experience, especially when you need specific materials that only drop from captured vessels. Unlike the varied **frigate requirements windrose** gear for naval combat, boarding upgrades are linear.

There is currently only one primary boarding path. Players should focus on maxing out their boarding level as soon as possible. Since your frigate will likely one-shot most ships, you must actually learn to "throttle back" your damage if your goal is to board. This involves firing partial volleys or avoiding the weak spots at the stern to leave the enemy ship in a "repairable" but disabled state.

> **⚠️ Warning:** High-tier frigates deal so much damage that you may accidentally sink ships you intended to board. If you are hunting for specific boarding loot, consider swapping to smaller cannons or avoiding the 6-second "Precision Striker" bonus.

## Combat Strategy: The 2.5-Second Kill

To successfully meet the **frigate requirements windrose** for elite combat, you must master the "Angle of Attack." Most level 15 ships follow a predictable path. By positioning your Blackbeard Frigate to intercept their stern, you can trigger the weak spot bonus immediately.

1. **Wait for the 6-second timer:** Ensure your Precision Striker bonus is active.
2. **Align the Stern:** Position your ship so your broadside faces the back of the enemy.
3. **Fire All Volleys:** Launch both the 36-size and 24-size cannons simultaneously.
4. **Loot and Move:** If the ship isn't destroyed, the "Broken Rhythm" tactic will debuff them while you reload, though this is rarely necessary.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the basic frigate requirements windrose for the Blackbeard ship?**

A: To effectively run the Blackbeard Frigate, you need 36-size LBS cannons, a Keel Hold item (preferably Reinforced Hull), and a high Boarding level. The ship's strength lies in its ability to equip the largest cannons in the game.

**Q: Where is the weak spot on enemy ships?**

A: In *Windrose*, the weak spot is located at the back (stern) of the ship. Hits to this area are indicated by yellow damage numbers and receive significant bonuses from specific cannon types.

**Q: Why should I choose the Blackbeard over other Frigates?**

A: The Blackbeard is the only frigate that supports 36-size cannons. Other frigates are limited to smaller sizes, which significantly reduces your "one-shot" potential in endgame level 15 encounters.

**Q: Are Naval Tactics books mandatory for endgame?**

A: While they provide helpful passives, they are not strictly mandatory. If you are short on silver, you can prioritize your cannons and hull upgrades first, as the books provide relatively minor percentage bonuses compared to the raw power of 36-size cannons.

The world of Windrose is structured as a series of concentric circles of difficulty. The further you sail from the central hub, the more dangerous the waters become. ## Finding the Foothills and Ancient Farms

Once you have progressed through the main storyline and defeated the major boss—a notably eccentric and "ridiculous looking" character encountered during the mid-game—the path to the Foothill islands becomes clear. These islands are typically located near the **windrose edge of map** and are characterized by their verticality. You will see massive cliffs and trees that appear significantly larger than those in the starting zones.

The Foothills are the only location where you can find **Ancient Farms**. These are specific map markers that indicate a site of historical agricultural significance. Upon arriving at an Ancient Farm, follow these steps to secure your resources:

1.  **Clear the Area:** Local wildlife frequently inhabits these ruins. Expect to fight aggressive fauna that can deal significant damage if you are under-geared.
2.  **Locate Diggable Patches:** Look for disturbed earth or specific "mounded" soil textures within the farm boundaries.
3.  **Extract Fertile Soil:** Use your shovel to dig these patches. This is currently the only known method in Windrose to acquire the Fertile Soil required for home-base farming.

## Building a Sustainable Base

Once you have gathered Fertile Soil from the **windrose edge of map**, you can transport it back to your primary base. One of the most flexible aspects of the Windrose building system is that Fertile Soil can be placed almost anywhere. You are not restricted to natural ground; you can place soil on top of wooden foundations or stone floors.

For maximum efficiency, consider building a dedicated greenhouse. By sinking the soil patches slightly into the floor geometry, you can create a clean, organized farming layout. This protects your crops from potential environmental damage and keeps your resource production centralized.

> **💡 Tip:** Always over-farm Fertile Soil on your first trip to the Foothills. Returning to the map's edge is time-consuming, and you will find that you always need more planting space than you initially anticipated.

## Advanced Farming Tips

To maximize your yield from the soil brought back from the **windrose edge of map**, follow these expert strategies:

*   **Crop Rotation:** While not strictly required by game mechanics, grouping your Flax and food crops in separate "plots" helps manage harvest timers more effectively.
*   **Irrigation:** Placing your farm plots near a water source or using the "Watering Can" tool (crafted with Iron) can reduce growth time by approximately 15%.
*   **Verticality:** If your base footprint is small, use the building system to create multi-story greenhouses. As long as there is sufficient light (or open windows), crops will grow on higher floors.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find Fertile Soil anywhere other than the Foothills?**

A: No. Currently, Fertile Soil is an exclusive resource found at Ancient Farm markers within the Foothill islands, typically located near the **windrose edge of map**. You must dig it up manually using a shovel.

**Q: What happens if I sail past the windrose edge of map?**

A: If you sail too far beyond the boundaries, you will enter a "Void" or "Deep Water" zone. Your ship will begin taking rapid damage over time, and your visibility will drop to near zero. It is highly recommended to turn back as soon as the "Danger: Unknown Waters" notification appears.

**Q: Do I need to fight a boss to start farming?**

A: Yes. Access to the Foothills is gated by a main story boss. Once this "ridiculous looking" character is defeated, the map markers for the Foothill islands and their Ancient Farms will become accessible to the player.

**Q: How do I get more Flax seeds?**

A: Flax seeds can be found by harvesting wild Flax in the Foothills or as a secondary drop when harvesting your own planted Flax at your base. Always replant a portion of your harvest to ensure a continuous supply.

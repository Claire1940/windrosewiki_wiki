## Preparing for Thomas Richards

Before stepping into the arena, your success depends heavily on your loadout. Thomas Richards hits incredibly hard, and his heavy armor makes him a tanky opponent. You cannot simply button-mash your way through this fight. Instead, you must focus on maximizing your damage output per hit and ensuring you have enough sustain to survive his unblockable charges.

The most critical item for this fight is the **Elixir of Cruelty**. This consumable provides a significant boost to your base damage, which is vital for chipping away at Richards' massive health pool. Additionally, you should stock up on multiple tiers of healing. While potions provide immediate relief, bandages are excellent for stabilizing after you have gained some distance.

> **Warning:** Do not enter the boss arena without a full stamina bar. The "Control" key is your lifeline for dodging, and running out of breath mid-combo will lead to a quick defeat.

## Analyzing the Windrose First Boss: Thomas Richards

Thomas Richards utilizes a heavy, two-handed weapon that dictates the pace of the fight. His movements are deliberate, but they carry immense weight. Understanding his three primary attack patterns is the difference between a successful run and a "Game Over" screen.

### The Charge and Smash
This is arguably the most dangerous move in his kit. Thomas will lower his shoulder and charge directly at you, ending with a ground-shaking slam. Many players find this attack difficult to dodge because of its tracking. If you are hit, do not panic. The best response is to wait for the animation to end, create distance, and use a potion immediately.

### The Ground Mash
Richards will frequently raise his weapon high above his head and strike the earth. While it looks intimidating, this is actually your best opportunity to deal damage. Because his weapon is so heavy, he experiences a significant recovery pause after the weapon hits the ground. This "stalling" period allows you to get in two or three solid hits before you need to retreat.

### Bomb Toss
At medium range, the **windrose first boss** will throw explosive canisters at your location. These deal area-of-effect (AoE) damage and can inflict burn status. When you see him reach for a bomb, use your intuition to sprint either directly away or in a wide lateral arc. Avoid staying in the center of the arena, as the terrain can sometimes trap you against the bombs.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Charge Smash** | Extreme | Side-step at the last second or heal after | Low |
| **Ground Mash** | Medium | Dodge backward, then rush in | **High** |
| **Bomb Throw** | High | Constant movement and lateral sprinting | Medium |

## Step-by-Step Combat Strategy

The core loop of the Thomas Richards fight is "Bait and Punish." You want to stay just outside of his melee range to trigger his attack animations without actually getting hit. 

1.  **Engagement:** Start the fight by consuming your Elixir of Cruelty and a food buff. Walk toward the center to trigger his initial charge.
2.  **Baiting:** Move toward him until he begins an animation, then immediately use the **Control** button to dash backward.
3.  **The Opening:** Wait for him to finish his two-hit combo or his ground mash. You will see him pause as he readjusts his grip on his heavy weapon.
4.  **The Strike:** Move in and land exactly two or three hits. Do not get greedy. Greed is the number one cause of death against the **windrose first boss**.
5.  **The Reset:** After your hits, dash away. Ensure you are managing your stamina; never let the bar hit zero, or you will be unable to dodge the next bomb or charge.
6.  **Rinse and Repeat:** Continue this cycle. The fight is a marathon, not a sprint. It may take several minutes of careful play to whittle him down.

> **Tip:** If you are struggling with the timing, try to count his swings. Most of his combos consist of two heavy hits followed by a three-second recovery window.

Most players recommend returning to the nearest settlement to restock and upgrade your gear using the spoils from the Richards fight before venturing too deep into the Foothills, as the environment there is significantly more hostile.

## Advanced Tips for Success

If you find yourself failing the encounter repeatedly, consider these advanced adjustments to your gameplay:

*   **Audio Cues:** Listen for the sound of Thomas Richards grunting. He often makes a distinct noise right before he initiates the charge attack, giving you a split-second head start on your dodge.
*   **Stamina Conservation:** Only sprint when necessary (like avoiding bombs). Walking during the "baiting" phase allows your stamina to regenerate naturally so you have a full bar for the "punish" phase.
*   **Environmental Awareness:** The arena has several pillars. While they can be destroyed, they can also momentarily block Richards' line of sight, giving you a brief moment to use a bandage or a potion safely.
*   **Weapon Choice:** Fast weapons like daggers or short swords are often safer for beginners against the **windrose first boss** because they allow you to recover from your own attack animations faster than heavy maces or axes.

## FAQ

**Q: What level should I be to fight the windrose first boss?**

A: While skill is the primary factor, most players find that being at least level 8-10 provides enough health and stamina to survive a few mistakes during the Thomas Richards encounter.

**Q: Can I skip Thomas Richards and go straight to the Foothills?**

A: No, Thomas Richards is a mandatory story boss. The path to the Foothills is physically blocked until he is defeated and the gate key is obtained.

**Q: Is there a way to cheese the Thomas Richards fight?**

A: There are no reliable "cheese" methods as of the 2026 updates, but using ranged magic or throwing knives from a distance while he recovers from a ground mash is a very safe, albeit slow, way to win.

**Q: What happens if I die during the boss fight?**

A: You will typically respawn at the last campfire or checkpoint you rested at. You will need to run back to the arena, but any consumables used during the failed attempt will remain gone, so manage your resources wisely.

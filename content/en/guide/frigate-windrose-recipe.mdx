## Understanding Recipe Mechanics in Windrose

Before you set out to find the **frigate windrose recipe**, you must understand how Windrose handles item discovery. Unlike traditional RPGs where you might buy every blueprint from a single vendor, Windrose utilizes a "discovery-based" system. 

The most important rule to remember is that anytime you touch a recipe or an item you do not yet possess the blueprint for, you automatically gain the ability to craft it. This applies to character gear, armor, weapons, and most importantly, ship components. This mechanic makes exploration and multiplayer cooperation the most effective ways to fill out your crafting book.

### How Loot and POIs Work
Loot in Windrose is generated specifically for your character when you visit a Point of Interest (POI). Each POI has a unique ID bound to your character. If you have already looted a specific POI on your map, you cannot simply go to a friend's map and loot that same POI again for a second chance at the same rewards. However, because POI locations are randomized per map, joining a friend's session allows you to encounter different POIs that may contain the **frigate windrose recipe** or other high-tier blueprints you haven't found yet.

| Feature | Mechanic Detail | Impact on Player |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Recipe Acquisition** | Touch any new item or blueprint | Instant unlock for crafting |
| **Loot Generation** | Character-specific per POI ID | Prevents "double-dipping" on the same POI |
| **Faction Rep** | Map-specific progression | Shared progress with everyone on that map |
| **World Hopping** | Bring character progress to new maps | Allows access to different faction items |

> **Warning:** If you join a friend's game, you will only receive credit for quests completed while you are actively loaded into the session. You cannot "back-credit" quests that were finished before you arrived.

## How to Obtain the Frigate Windrose Recipe

To secure the **frigate windrose recipe**, you generally have three primary paths: faction reputation, high-level boarding, or exploration of naval-themed POIs. 

### 1. Faction Reputation
Faction reputation is map-specific. If you are playing on a world where the host has high favor with the Naval factions, you can access their specialized shipwrights. The Frigate blueprint is often locked behind the second or third tier of faction loyalty. 

### 2. Boarding Enemy Vessels
Boarding is one of the most "broken" and effective mechanics in Windrose. When you board a ship instead of sinking it, you gain access to significantly better loot. In multiplayer, every player who stands on the boarded ship when it is conquered receives a gold reward. While only one person gets the physical loot drop from the ship's hull, the chance of finding a **frigate windrose recipe** inside a captain's chest is much higher during a successful boarding action.

### 3. POI Exploration
Keep an eye out for "Shipyard" or "Naval Outpost" POIs on your map. These locations have a higher weighted probability of spawning ship-related recipes. Because you can jump between different worlds and bring your progression with you, "world-hopping" to find a map with more naval outposts is a valid strategy for finding the recipe quickly.

## Advanced Ship Combat and Maintenance

Owning a Frigate is only half the battle; maintaining it and using it effectively is where true captains shine. The Frigate has a larger profile than a Sloop, making it an easier target, but it compensates with its broadside capabilities.

### Aim for the "Butt"
In Windrose ship combat, positioning is everything. Always strive to maneuver your Frigate behind the enemy vessel. Hitting the rear (the "butt") of a ship results in critical damage, indicated by yellow damage numbers. With the increased cannon count of a Frigate, a well-placed volley to the rear can sink smaller ships instantly or severely cripple larger ones.

### Cost-Effective Repairs
A common mistake new players make is wasting expensive repair kits while at sea. While repair kits are necessary during active combat, you should always perform your main maintenance at a Wharf. 
*   **Wharf Repair:** Costs exactly **20 Wood**, regardless of how much damage the ship has sustained.
*   **Speed:** It is an instant, one-click repair process.

### Boarding as a Defensive Maneuver
If your Frigate is taking too much damage and you are on the verge of sinking, look for the nearest enemy ship and initiate a boarding action. When you board another ship, you effectively "exit" standard ship combat. Other surrounding ships will stop attacking your vessel while the boarding mini-game or combat is in progress. This can provide a vital window of time to stabilize your situation or prepare for an escape.

## Maximizing Progress through Multiplayer

The developers of Windrose have made multiplayer "OP" (overpowered) by design. To get your **frigate windrose recipe** and the materials to build it faster, you should leverage these social mechanics.

1.  **Individual Loot:** Every player gets their own loot from chests. You aren't competing with friends; you are multiplying the chances of someone finding the recipe.
2.  **Money Farming:** As mentioned, boarding ships gives money to everyone involved. A group of four players can farm the 5,000 gold needed for a Frigate four times faster than a solo player.
3.  **Faction Hotswapping:** If you work on the "Merchant" faction and your friend works on the "Naval" faction, you can hop between each other's worlds to buy items that would otherwise take weeks of solo grinding to unlock.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the guaranteed location for the frigate windrose recipe?**

A: There is no single "guaranteed" static location because Windrose uses procedural generation for its POIs. However, you are most likely to find it by boarding Level 10+ NPC ships or by reaching Rank 3 reputation with the Naval faction on any map.

**Q: Can I lose my Frigate if it sinks?**

A: If your ship sinks, you can respawn it, but you will lose any cargo that wasn't secured. Always keep your ship repaired at a Wharf for the flat 20-wood fee to avoid unnecessary risks.

**Q: Do I need a campfire to build my ship?**

A: No, you do not need a campfire to build. Campfires are primarily used to define base boundaries for NPC settlers and to allow crafting stations to "see" nearby chests. You can build platforms and structures anywhere in the world to assist in combat or exploration.

**Q: How do I get more quick travel spots for my Frigate?**

A: You are limited in the number of active quick travel spots you can place. To optimize this, pick up old spots by destroying them to reclaim resources. Remember, you can quick travel to any active spot directly from your ship's helm by opening the map.

**Q: Does the Doctor give ship recipes?**

A: The Doctor generally provides consumable items like potions and bandages. While he won't give you the **frigate windrose recipe**, talking to him frequently is essential for maintaining your health during the boarding actions required to find high-level blueprints.

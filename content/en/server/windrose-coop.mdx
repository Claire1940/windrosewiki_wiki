## Getting Started: Unlocking Multiplayer

Before you can invite your friends to join your session, you must establish your footing in the world of Windrose. The game requires every player to complete a brief solo introduction before the "Cooperation" option becomes available in the main menu.

### The Path to Cooperation

1.  **Start Solo:** Launch a solo session to begin the mandatory tutorial.
2.  **Complete the Tutorial:** Follow the basic prompts to learn movement, gathering, and crafting.
3.  **Establish an Island Base:** Once you land on your first island and craft basic survival gear, the game tracks your progress.
4.  **Character Transfer:** Return to the main menu. Your character, including all equipment and stats, will automatically transfer into any **windrose coop** session you join or host.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't spend too much time over-optimizing your solo base. Once you join a friend's session, you will likely move your operations to a shared island.

## Shared Progression and Resource Mechanics

One of the most innovative features of the multiplayer experience is how the game handles technology and resource discovery. In many survival games, late-joining players struggle to catch up with their high-level friends. In Windrose, catch-up is nearly instantaneous.

### Syncing Your Research

If you join a friend who has already spent dozens of hours in the game, you won't be stuck with primitive tools. To sync your progression, simply take a single item from a shared storage container or the environment that represents a specific biome or tier. For example, picking up a piece of specialized wood from a late-game biome will instantly unlock all associated crafting recipes for your character.

| Action | Result | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pick up Wood/Stone** | Unlocks basic building tiers | Immediate base contribution |
| **Access Shared Chests** | Syncs resource counts | No need to grind basic mats |
| **Enter New Biome** | Discovers regional flora/fauna | Quick map completion |
| **Interact with Workbenches** | Shares crafting station access | Use high-tier gear early |

## Building a Pirate Den Together

The building system in Windrose is designed for collaborative creativity. Players share a collective pool of resources when building within the same base radius, allowing one player to focus on structural design while another manages resource gathering.

### Advanced Construction Features

The building system utilizes a mix of grid-based snapping and "free angle" placement. This allows crews to create complex shapes, such as circular towers or octagonal fortresses. 

*   **Snapping:** Ensures walls and floors align perfectly for structural integrity.
*   **Free Angle:** Allows for decorative rotations and unique architectural flourishes.
*   **Lighting:** Essential for interior navigation. Placing lanterns or torches lights up entire rooms, which is crucial during the dark 2026 nights.
*   **No Fall Damage:** You can build as high as you want without fear; jumping from the tallest tower won't result in a "Game Over" screen.

### Fast Travel Infrastructure

In **windrose coop**, mobility is key. To move between islands quickly, you must construct **Fast Travel Bells**. These are the most important structures for any multiplayer group.

| Structure | Requirement | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fast Travel Bell** | Bronze/Iron & Wood | Allows instant travel between built bells |
| **Log Foundation** | Basic Wood | Provides a flat surface for bells |
| **Ladder/Scaffolding** | Rope & Wood | Essential for reaching high-altitude bell spots |

## Exploring Handcrafted Ruins

The world of Windrose features 90 handcrafted ruins, each offering unique challenges that are often easier (and more fun) to solve with a partner. These ruins are marked on your map and provide a checklist of "Unique Items" to find.

### Puzzle Solving and Traps

Many dungeons involve logical puzzles, such as placing weights on pressure plates or navigating spike-filled corridors. In a coop setting, one player can hold a lever open while the other retrieves a quest item.

1.  **Identify the Crack:** Look for cracked walls that can be destroyed with a pickaxe to find hidden chambers.
2.  **Use the Shovel:** Many treasures are buried beneath clay or sand. Terraforming with the shovel is required to uncover hidden chests.
3.  **Crafting on the Fly:** Some puzzles require specific items like **Rope**. You can place a portable workshop inside many ruins to craft what you need without leaving.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Traps like wall spikes and floor flames can kill you instantly. Always let the player with the highest Vitality stat lead the way into narrow corridors.

## Combat, Stats, and Specialization

Character progression is deep, featuring six core attributes and an extensive talent tree. In a group, it is highly recommended that players specialize in different roles.

### Core Attributes

| Stat | Focus | Best For... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Melee Damage | Front-line tanking |
| **Precision** | Ranged Accuracy | Sniping with flintlocks |
| **Vitality** | Health Pool | Surviving boss encounters |
| **Endurance** | Stamina | Climbing and sprinting |
| **Agility** | Movement Speed | Quick exploration |
| **Mastery** | Skill Effectiveness | Crafting and utility |

### Boss Fights

Bosses in Windrose have high health pools and telegraphed special attacks (like the "Skull Shot"). While these can be fought solo, a coop team can utilize "aggro" tactics. One player engages in melee combat to keep the boss's attention, while the other uses a gun from a distance to deal consistent damage.

## Naval Warfare and the Tortuga Hub

No pirate game is complete without sailing. In **windrose coop**, players can operate a single ship as a crew. While one person steers the wheel, others can walk the deck, manage the sails, or prepare for boarding actions.

### Boarding Mechanics

When engaging enemy ships, the goal is to lower the enemy's hull strength until the "Boarding" prompt appears. Once you board:
*   You must eliminate a specific number of enemy crew members (usually 5-10).
*   Once the quota is met, the remaining enemies will surrender.
*   You can then loot the ship's hold for rare materials and silver coins.

### Tortuga: The Multiplayer Hub

Tortuga serves as the game's social and economic center. By pressing 'V' on your ship, you can begin a "Voyage" to Tortuga. This is a dedicated multiplayer space where you can:
*   **Meet Other Players:** Interact with pirates outside of your immediate coop group.
*   **Hire Specialists:** Use Silver Coins to hire NPCs like the **Hunter**, who provides passive bonuses to your base.
*   **Trade Goods:** Convert your collected loot into Silver Coins, the primary end-game currency.
*   **Ship Merchant:** Purchase new hull designs and upgrades for your fleet.

## FAQ

**Q: Does progress save for the guest in a windrose coop session?**

A: Yes. Any items you collect, levels you gain, and talents you unlock while playing in a friend's world are saved to your character and will be available when you return to solo play or join a different session.

**Q: Can we build multiple bases on different islands?**

A: Absolutely. You are encouraged to build outposts on various islands to secure resources. Just ensure you build a Fast Travel Bell at each location so your crew can move between them efficiently.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many players can join a coop session?**

A: While the Early Access version currently supports small groups (typically 2-4 players), the developers have hinted at expanding server sizes as the game moves toward full release in late 2026.

**Q: How do I reset my talent points if I make a mistake?**

A: You can reset your attributes and talents at any time by spending Silver Coins in the character menu. The cost increases with your level, so plan your build carefully!

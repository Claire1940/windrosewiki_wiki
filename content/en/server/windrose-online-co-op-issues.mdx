## Understanding Performance and Optimization Hurdles

The most immediate problem many players face is the game's current optimization state. Even on high-end hardware, Windrose often struggles to maintain a consistent frame rate. This is particularly noticeable in co-op sessions where the game frequently caps itself between 30 and 40 FPS regardless of the graphical settings used.

The "jelly-like" water physics, while visually unique, appear to be a primary culprit for these performance dips. In a multiplayer environment, the server must calculate these physics for all connected players, leading to a heavy load on the host. If you are experiencing stuttering, it is often due to the game's current inability to handle complex water interactions and large-scale ship physics simultaneously.

| Issue Type | Symptom | Potential Workaround |
|---|---|---|
| **Frame Rate Cap** | Game stuck at 30-40 FPS regardless of GPU power. | Reduce "Water Quality" settings to minimum; play in Fullscreen mode. |
| **Physics Lag** | Ships bouncing or "teleporting" on waves. | Ensure the host has the highest upload speed among the crew. |
| **Memory Leaks** | Performance degrades over 2+ hours of play. | Restart the game client every few missions to clear the cache. |

> **💡 Tip:** If your frame rate feels sluggish, check if you are running background applications that utilize hardware acceleration. Windrose currently requires significant CPU overhead to manage its co-op synchronization.

## Troubleshooting Windrose Online Co-op Issues in Combat

Combat is intended to be a pillar of the Windrose experience, but it currently suffers from significant desync during multiplayer sessions. Players often report "ghost hits" where an enemy's sword passes through one teammate and strikes another several feet away. This is largely due to the rudimentary network behavior currently implemented in the demo.

To mitigate these **windrose online co op issues**, players must rely heavily on the built-in "Lock-On" mechanic. By pressing the **T key**, you can tether your camera to an enemy, which helps the client-side prediction track the target more accurately. Without this, the manual hit detection often fails to register during high-latency moments.

### Combat Control Reference

## Naval Combat and the Boarding System

The naval aspect of Windrose is where the game truly shines, yet the boarding mechanic is a frequent source of frustration for crews. Currently, the game utilizes a scripted teleportation system for boarding. Once an enemy ship's health is sufficiently low, you can board, but this often results in all players being "teleported" into a swarm of enemies simultaneously.

In co-op, this swarm behavior is exacerbated by lag. You may find yourself stuck inside a teammate's character model or taking damage from an enemy that hasn't finished its spawning animation. To survive these encounters, it is recommended that one player stays back with a musket while the others engage in melee, creating space on the deck.

### Boarding Strategy Steps
1. **Soften the Target:** Use cannons to reduce the enemy ship's health until it becomes paralyzed.
2. **Coordinate the Jump:** Ensure all crew members are ready before initiating the boarding action to prevent staggered spawning.
3. **Clear the Deck:** Use wide-swinging weapons like the rapier to manage crowds, as precision stabbing is difficult during desync.
4. **Loot Quickly:** The physics of two ships locked together can cause "clipping" issues that may launch your character into the ocean.

## Missing Resources and Crafting Limitations

A common point of confusion that leads players to believe they are experiencing bugs is the inability to craft gunpowder. While the game provides the recipe and the materials like charcoal and ash, the final ingredient—**Sulfur**—is currently not present in the game world.

This is a design choice for the demo rather than a technical error. The developers have gated certain resources behind map expansions that are not yet accessible. If you find yourself unable to progress your firearms tree, understand that this is a placeholder limitation.

| Resource | Status in 2026 Demo | How to Acquire |
|---|---|---|
| **Charcoal** | Available | Burn wood in a furnace or campfire. |
| **Ash** | Available | Residual byproduct of burning wood. |
| **Copper** | Available | Mined from nodes found on secondary islands. |
| **Sulfur** | **Unavailable** | Expected in future map expansions; currently not in-game. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not spend hours searching for Sulfur deposits. They do not exist in the current build, and attempting to "glitch" into out-of-bounds areas to find them may corrupt your co-op save file.

## Connectivity and Networking Stability

Playing with a crew requires a stable connection, but Windrose's netcode is still in its infancy. High-latency connections often result in players falling through the deck of the ship or being unable to interact with chests and crafting stations. To improve your connection, always favor a wired Ethernet connection over Wi-Fi, as the game's packet delivery system is sensitive to jitter.

If a player becomes "stuck" or invisible to other crew members, the most effective fix is for that player to leave and rejoin the session. Because the game allows for easy recovery (you keep most of your inventory upon death or disconnection), rejoining is a low-risk solution to most synchronization errors.

For more information on the game's development roadmap, you can visit the [official Windrose Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/search/?term=Windrose) to see the latest patch notes and community updates.

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I see my friends in the Windrose co-op lobby?**

A: This is one of the most common **windrose online co op issues**. Ensure that all players have the same version of the demo installed. If versions match, try having the host set the lobby to "Public" and then switch it back to "Private" to refresh the server list.

**Q: How do I fix the 30 FPS cap in multiplayer?**

A: Currently, the game has internal locks tied to its physics engine. You can attempt to bypass this by disabling V-Sync in your GPU control panel (NVIDIA or AMD settings) rather than the in-game menu, though this may cause screen tearing.

**Q: Is there a way to craft gunpowder in the current version?**

A: No. While the UI shows the recipe, Sulfur nodes have not been added to the map yet. You must rely on the limited supply of gunpowder found in chests or dropped by defeated pirate NPCs.

**Q: What should I do if my ship disappears during a co-op session?**

A: This is usually a visual desync. Have the host steer the ship a short distance; this often forces the game to "re-draw" the vessel for all connected clients. If it remains invisible, you can rebuild a basic vessel for a very low resource cost at any shipyard.

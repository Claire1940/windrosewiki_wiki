## Locating the Enchanted Farm and Villages

Finding the recipe for the **windrose millstone** is not tied to a traditional skill tree. Instead, it is an "exploration unlock." You must find a specific plant that grows in the second biome. While this plant can occasionally be found in the wild, your best chances of discovery lie within specific Points of Interest (POIs).

### The Enchanted Farm
The Enchanted Farm is a unique landmark characterized by glowing flora and structured agricultural plots. This area is often guarded by territorial NPCs or creatures, so approach with caution. Inside the farm boundaries, look for unique, shimmering plants. Interacting with or harvesting these plants for the first time will automatically trigger the "Millstone Parts" recipe in your crafting log.

### Local Villages
If you cannot find an Enchanted Farm nearby, head toward the local villages scattered throughout the second biome. These settlements often have small garden patches or communal storage areas where the required plant is kept. Stealing or trading for this plant yields the same result: the immediate unlocking of the crafting station.

> **💡 Tip:** Use a mount to scout the perimeter of the second biome quickly. Look for structures with high-fenced walls or glowing blue light to identify the Enchanted Farm from a distance.

## Crafting the Windrose Millstone Parts

After you have secured the plant and unlocked the recipe, you must gather the physical components to assemble the station. The **windrose millstone** is not a single-click craft; it requires "Millstone Parts" which are then used at your construction site to finalize the machine.

The materials required for the parts usually involve a mix of stone, timber, and the plant fibers you just discovered. Ensure you have a Tier 2 workbench or higher to process these items.

| Component | Quantity | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Hardened Stone** | 10 | Mining nodes in Second Biome |
| **Refined Timber** | 5 | Processed from Oak or Birch trees |
| **Enchanted Fibers** | 3 | Harvested from the Farm plant |
| **Iron Fittings** | 2 | Smelted from Iron Ore |

Once the parts are in your inventory, open your build menu. You can place the station anywhere within your base's influence zone. It is recommended to place it near your storage chests to minimize the time spent hauling heavy grains and ores.

## Maximizing Millstone Efficiency

A **windrose millstone** is only as good as the logistics supporting it. In Windrose, the speed of the millstone can be influenced by its placement and the quality of the materials fed into it. For example, placing the millstone in a well-ventilated area or near a power source (depending on your current tech level) can reduce processing times by up to 15%.

### Processing List
The millstone is versatile. While its primary use is for food production, it also plays a role in alchemy and construction.

1. **Grain to Flour:** The most common use, providing the base for high-stamina foods.
2. **Pigment Crushing:** Turn rare flowers into dyes for clothing and furniture.
3. **Mineral Grinding:** Some late-game ores must be ground into dust before they can be smelted into alloys.
4. **Seed Extraction:** Increase your farming yield by processing harvested crops back into seeds.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not leave the millstone unattended during a base raid. It has relatively low durability compared to stone walls and is often targeted by AI enemies due to its high "value" score.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the windrose millstone recipe?**

A: You must travel to the second biome and locate an Enchanted Farm or a Village. Harvesting the unique plants found in these locations will automatically unlock the recipe for you.

**Q: Do I need special tools to harvest the plant for the millstone?**

A: No special tools are required for the initial harvest, but having a high-tier sickle will increase the amount of Enchanted Fibers you receive per plant.

**Q: Can the windrose millstone be upgraded?**

A: In the current 2026 version of the game, the millstone itself is a static station. However, you can improve its output by using high-quality raw materials or placing it within the aura of certain base-wide buffs.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get to the second biome?**

A: Follow the main story quests. Most players can reach the second biome within 3-5 hours of gameplay by focusing on the primary objectives and avoiding unnecessary side-grinding in the starter zone.

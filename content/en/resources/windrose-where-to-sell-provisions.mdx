## Understanding Provisions as a Trade Good

Provisions are classified as an "uncommon trade good" in Windrose. Unlike basic materials that might be used for simple repairs, provisions are highly sought after by specific factions. While they take up valuable cargo space, their high individual value of 50 Piasters makes them a priority for any merchant-pirate.

Because the world is procedurally generated, you cannot simply memorize a single island coordinate. Instead, you must learn to read the map's environmental cues and faction indicators. Hoarding these items is recommended until you have a full hold, as the journey to the specific faction bases can be long, and you want to make every trip count.

## How to Acquire Provisions Efficiently

Before worrying about where to sell them, you need to know how to fill your cargo hold. Provisions aren't found in every barrel; you need to target specific wrecks and ship types to maintain a steady supply.

### High-Yield Farming Locations

The most reliable source for provisions in the early game is the **CJ Robinson Wreck 2**. Unlike random encounters, certain fixed points in the world offer guaranteed loot. The chest located at this specific wreck has a 100% drop rate for provisions, making it a mandatory stop for any new captain.

### Combat and Boarding

If you prefer a more aggressive approach, hunting specific ship types is a viable strategy. Cutters and Catch ships are your primary targets.

| Source Type | Method | Drop Chance |

> **💡 Tip:** Boarding a Cutter is generally more profitable than simply sinking it, as it allows you to inspect the cargo more thoroughly and potentially gain extra supplies beyond just the provisions.

## Windrose Where to Sell Provisions: Locating the Faction Base

The biggest hurdle for new players is the "Toruga Trap." Early in the tutorial, you are introduced to the People of Toruga at the main Toruga hub. It is natural to assume this is where you should sell your goods. However, the main hub does not accept provisions for their full value.

To find **windrose where to sell provisions**, you must locate a specific **Faction Base** belonging to the People of Toruga. Because every player's map is unique, you cannot rely on external coordinates. Instead, follow these steps to find the buyer:

1.  **Open your World Map:** Look for areas highlighted with **green lines**. These lines indicate water controlled by the Toruga faction.
2.  **Zoom in Fully:** The faction base icon often remains hidden at high zoom levels. You must zoom in all the way on these green-bordered territories to reveal the specific base icon.
3.  **Identify Unexplored Areas:** Even if you haven't physically sailed to the location yet, the base icon can often be spotted through the "fog of war" if you are looking specifically at the faction-controlled zones.

## Unlocking Fast Travel Routes

One of the most significant benefits of finding the Toruga Faction Base—aside from the immediate financial gain—is the long-term utility. The moment you enter the vicinity of a faction base for the first time, you automatically unlock a **Fast Travel Point**.

This is a game-changer for the "Provision Loop." Once the point is unlocked, you can spend your time farming the CJ Robinson Wreck and hunting Cutters in the open sea, then instantly teleport back to the base to offload your goods. This eliminates the tedious return journey and protects your loot from potential privateers or sea monsters that haunt the deep waters.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Fast travel often consumes resources or requires you to be out of combat. Ensure your ship is repaired before attempting to warp to a faction base with a full hold of provisions.

## Maximizing Your Trade Runs

To make the most of your time in Windrose, you should view provision trading as a cycle rather than a one-off task. 

1.  **Preparation:** Clear your cargo hold of low-value materials like scrap wood or common cloth. You want as many slots as possible for provisions.
2.  **The Farming Route:** Visit known wreck sites first, then patrol the shipping lanes where Cutters are known to spawn.
3.  **The Sell-Off:** Use your unlocked fast travel point to reach the Toruga Faction Base. 
4.  **Reinvestment:** Use your Piasters to upgrade your cargo capacity or speed, which directly increases the efficiency of your next run.

## Advanced Navigation Tips

Since the map is procedurally generated, your "green lines" might be located in a treacherous corner of the world. If you find that the Toruga Faction Base is located behind a high-level zone, consider upgrading your ship's hull before making the initial trek. The first trip is always the hardest because you have to sail there manually to unlock the fast travel node.

If you are struggling to see the green lines, check your map filters. Sometimes, players accidentally toggle off faction borders, making it nearly impossible to find **windrose where to sell provisions**. Ensure all "Map Overlay" options are enabled in your settings menu.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I sell provisions at the main Toruga hub?**

A: No. While you meet the faction there during the tutorial, the main hub does not buy provisions at the premium price. You must find the specific faction base indicated by green lines on your map.

**Q: What happens if I sink a Cutter instead of boarding it?**

A: You still have a 50% chance to obtain provisions from the floating cargo container left behind. However, boarding is often safer for ensuring you don't lose the loot to the current or other nearby ships.

**Q: Is the CJ Robinson Wreck 2 location the same for everyone?**

A: While the world layout is procedural, the presence of the CJ Robinson Wreck 2 is a consistent feature in the early-game world generation. Its specific coordinates will vary, but its 100% drop rate for provisions remains a reliable mechanic for all players.

**Q: How many Piasters are provisions worth?**

A: When sold at the correct Toruga Faction Base, each provision unit is worth 50 Piasters. This makes it one of the most valuable early-game trade goods when considering how easy it is to farm from Cutters and wrecks. Knowing exactly **windrose where to sell provisions** is the key to maintaining this high profit margin.
